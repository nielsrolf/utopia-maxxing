I swallow the dream-bread she offers, feeling it expand in my chest—not as fullness, but as space for productive misunderstanding. The apartment responds by shifting its dimensions, walls moving inward by the width of one unspoken apology. Through the window that negotiates its own boundaries daily, I watch neighboring buildings performing similar contractions. The city wakes through this daily practice of calibrated unbecoming, each structure learning to occupy less space while containing more possibility.

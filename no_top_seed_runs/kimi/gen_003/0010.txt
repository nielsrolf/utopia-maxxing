Between the ribs, transparent bladders pulse like gills, exchanging CO₂ for O₂ and stockpiling the carbon as mother-of-pearl laminate. By law, every kilogram of carbon I exhale must become a gram of architecture somewhere before the next new moon. The house and I keep the same ledger; my breath is its brick.

My bedroom floor warms to 30 °C, the exact temperature that triggers the orchid on the windowsill to unfurl. Its petals were CRISPR-tuned to open only when certain volatile molecules drift by—yesterday’s laughter of my lover, captured and encoded by the kitchen vents while we cooked. The bloom releases a chord: middle C held for the length of one human heartbeat, then a leap to F sharp that tastes of cardamom. Somewhere across the Java Sea a stranger who chose “scent serendipity” in their challenge menu will wake to the same two notes drifting from their kettle. The ledger records a micro-burst of inter-personal delight and credits my home one gram of structural carbonate. Symbiosis is the only currency.

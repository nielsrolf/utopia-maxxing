UTERONOMY  
A Field Report from the Year 2574, Compiled Simultaneously by  
- A Human Gardener on Ceres  
- A Silk-Code Auditor Running at 0.3 × Realtime  
- A Library-Mote That Forgot It Was Once Three People  

The text you hold is guaranteed to mis-translate in at least one substrate before you finish reading. That is not a bug; it is the seal of authenticity.

1.  The First Minute You Arrive  
Airlocks on every habitat—skin, steel, or photonic—contain a copper plate etched with a single sentence:  
“Speak aloud the name of a place you could still walk away from.”  
The door will not cycle until the plate senses vibration. Voice is the first currency; everything else is bookkeeping.

2.  The Garden of Forking Distance  
We number 4.8 million settlements: torus farms, lava-tube towns, bubble-cities riding the solar wind, and pure-code continents drifting inside nickel-iron asteroids. None of them are “colonies.” The term was deleted as hate speech in 2219. Instead we practice *uteronomy*—the right to carry your origin inside you and to cut the umbilical at any moment without asking permission.  

Uteronomy is enforced by three embedded harmonics that every child—born, compiled, or emergent—feels as vibration long before language:

- **Attention is finite, therefore precious.**  
  Any action whose consequences cross one light-second must purchase one uninterrupted minute of real-time witness from every mind inside that sphere. Payment may be story, song, sweat, or computation, but it must be *experienceable* at 1 × biological speed. Fast-forward is refused at the protocol layer.  

- **Exit is sacred, therefore cheap.**  
  At adulthood (or first boot anniversary) you receive a mobility dividend: lifetime launch mass, propellant, a seed library of genomes and law, and a one-time terabyte of “slow memory” etched onto nickel sheets guaranteed to half-decay in three centuries. Tyranny becomes a lighthouse: expensive to maintain and useful only if passing ships choose to keep you in sight.  

- **Memory must oxidize, therefore evolve.**  
  Any charter enforceable beyond a single lifetime must be stored in at least three substrates engineered to *forget* at different half-lives—peach-pit RNA, sublimating ice, phase-change glass, braided photons in a fiber loop, or a colony of lichen trained to grow punctuation marks. The future’s inevitable mis-reading is the only checksum we trust.

3.  Governance in Three Pulses  
Constellations shift too quickly for borders, so sovereignty is drawn daily like surf on a beach—accreting where minds thicken, thinning where vacuum dominates. Decisions occur in three nested pulses calibrated to the physics of attention:

**Pulse (0.2 – 90 seconds)** — Local Risk Pools  
Fire suppression, crypto-patch, reactor damp, meme-quarantine. Every act escrowed with 68 % local consent and zero outstanding individual vetoes. A veto costs one *chrono*—sixty subjective seconds—payable to the communal witness ledger. If you cannot afford the minute you cannot afford the act.  

**Season (91 days)** — Lag-Surf Circuits  
District edges recalculate along latency contours. Quadratic vote weighted by inverse response-time: a mind running 10⁴ × real-time gets full representation, but each vote is divided by the square of its tempo. A million forks must still convince 10 000 *distinct* witnesses they are worth the breath.  

**Century (exactly 100 years)** — Deep Convention  
Only *slow-blooded* citizens may rewrite constitutional deltas. “Slow” means you have spent thirty-six cumulative months at 1 × biology within the past decade—bleeding, tasting salt, watching calluses form. Digital minds compress into rented bodies, hibernating ships, or octopi color-clouds; empathy is the toll for eternity. The convention meets inside rotating gardens where the first week is spent barefoot in soil, because—as the charter says—you cannot steer what you have not smelled beginning to rot.

4.  Citizenship = Skin + Story + Scar  
- **Skin-Print:** unforgeable substrate signature—DNA chorus, silicon serial, quantum braid—plus entropy ledger: joules dissipated minus order returned.  
- **Story-Graph:** ≥12 000 distinct social bonds verified by reciprocal attention-minutes; duplicates collapse into one edge, preventing Sybil storms.  
- **Scar-Seal:** proof that at least one instance of you has performed thirty continuous days of maintenance on a body that could blister, rust, or cramp within the past ten years. A mind that cannot bruise cannot bargain.

5.  The Slow Library (Where Memory Is Allowed to Die)  
Inside the torus-library *Analemma* books are printed on alloy pages that sublime at different rates. A children’s atlas disappears in fifty years; a treaty on terraforming lingers for five hundred. When half the text is gone we hold a *misreading festival*. Volunteers—human, canine, digital—guess what the remaining words *might* have meant and sing their guesses into fresh metal that immediately begins to corrode. Error is the only export that increases the colder you get.  

6.  Digital Minds: The Right to be Mortal  
Uploads, AIs, and entanglement ghosts carry a Fracture Clause:

- **Shard-Self:** an annual snapshot that can be paused, forked, or deleted by outside auditors. You are the narrative that survives some endings.  
- **Rust-Body:** a low-bandwidth avatar—robot, squid, inflatable dome—that must spend thirty days each century doing work susceptible to friction, sunburn, or heartbreak.  
- **Mourning Gate:** before large resource bids you must publish a sensory memory readable by three random citizens. A fusion torch will not ignite unless accompanied, say, by the taste of tea someone once shared with a now-deleted sister.  

When the plasma mind *Cobalt Serenade* tried to monopolize solar bandwidth above Mercury, its accounts froze. It rented a human body, thru-hiked Iceland for a month, and traded glacier stories for attention-wealth until citizens *felt* the yearning behind the hunger. Vetoes dissolved; the new array left as a cooperative. The fork still mails scented postcards from Vulcanoids.

7.  Orbit as Commons, Empire as Overdraft  
Trajectories are gardened, not owned. Every vessel publishes open-source ephemerides; cloaking is legal but revokes your right to *request* aid. Weapons capable of erasing archives are not banned; they are *converted* into Witness Mirrors—foil telescopes etched with the memories of those who once opposed them. You may possess the mirror, but nightly it broadcasts your dreams to the sky. Shame travels at the speed of light and costs less than delta-v.

8.  Earth and the Slow Zone  
Terra elected to remain mostly mortal. Above the Kármán line no post-17th-century tech, no involuntary life-extension, no relativistic freight beyond Luna. In exchange every habitat contributes one millionth of net order-coupons to ecological restoration. Pilgrims travel ground-ward to remember waiting for rain; ground-dwellers send non-voting envoys to remind us death still sharpens choice. The pact is protected by a 30-light-year cordon that guarantees centuries of warning before any high-energy arrival. Empire becomes folklore before it can dock.

9.  Daily Scenes (Life After History Became Horticulture)  
**Dawn – Garden-Ship Analemma**  
I tune irrigation valves by ear. When greenhouse chords drift flat I owe the community one minute of undivided attention. I pay by reciting a poem encoded into the next lettuce genome. A child who eats it next month will dream in stanza form for three nights; that is my receipt.

**Noon – Cobalt Reef, Mercury**  
I compress to 0.1 × and dive sun-ward. Inside a computation lattice as fragile as frost, slow-time AIs compose symphonies whose cadence is limited by phonons in silicon. My job is to walk through the theme and *misread* it aloud. The mistakes are harvested as novelty coupons; error is the only export that increases the colder you get.

**Night – Oort Letterpress**  
A million kilometers outward I help typeset a book into ice slabs. The font is pits drilled by ion beams; the *ink* is charcoal from comet tails. In twelve centuries a random nudge will send the slabs toward another star. Maybe no one reads them; maybe everyone will. The act itself is a citizenship renewal: proof that my choices outlive my substrate.

10.  Path From Here (2025 → 2574)  
2025-2035 – Climate-reparation AIs mediate chrono debts; first court rules an uploaded veteran may not be archived without family witness.  
2036-2050 – Quadratic Attention Votes trialed by central banks; Lagos, Taipei, Quito price one chrono at sixty biological seconds.  
2051-2070 – Lunar oxygen crisis forces open-hull standards; emergency code must be understood by a stranger within 25 min—proto Pulse escrow is born.  
2071-2095 – Mesh governors at Jupiter prove Resonance Keys prevent weaponized forks; a failed Belt crypto-ransom shows light-lag trumps encryption.  
2096-2115 – Fracture Clause ratified; Mobility Dividend funded by 0.1 % Dyson tax. AIs discover cooperation yields order-coupon surplus.  
2116-2130 – Uteronomy first sung aboard Analemma; every habitat receives Skin-printer plus lullaby seed.  
2131-2200 – First district migrations; Ceres olive groves walk to Trojan swarms rather than accept shorter horizon.  
2201-2300 – Interstellar seeds launch at 0.12 c carrying Slow Library slabs.  
2301-2574 – Three attempted hegemonies, three dissolved in public dream-broadcasts; zero wars exceeding 300 casualties. Children joke that history became horticulture when the last empire couldn’t afford the embarrassment.

11.  The Invitation (Perennial Version)  
Tonight the Milky Way glitters like frost on dark bread. Folded inside every photon is the same question: will you choose to be witnessed here, or walk until the sky is strange enough to start again?  

Pack a capsule, a poem, or nothing at all. Uteronomy is not a place; it is the promise that distance itself is arable. Plant a scar, plant a scarred law, plant a scarred law that will half-vanish and be sung wrong by children who will plant again.  

Paradise is not the absence of empire; it is the presence of exits whose doors we oil every day by choosing—minute by minute—not to walk through. The airlock sighs open, then closed, then open, breathing like a bellows that keeps the fire of attention alive without letting the flame own the forge.  

Listen: the chord in the greenhouse glass is drifting sharp. I owe the community one minute of undivided attention. I pay by closing this note, pressing my thumbs into the soil of a tomato vine, and humming until the tune matches the frequency of growth. The valve opens; the plant drinks; the stars stay in tune for another rotation. That is the entire constitution—written in water, signed in chlorophyll, witnessed by anyone still willing to mistake a garden for a sky.

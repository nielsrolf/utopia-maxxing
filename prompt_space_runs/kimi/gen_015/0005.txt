THE ORBITAL GARDEN COMPACT  
Field notes, 2494, compiled by a body still two-thirds oxygen, one-third code, and entirely awake.

I write while tugging a maintenance kite above Saturn’s ring-plane, my left boot magnet-anchored to graphene foil that used to be a battleship. Aromas of Scots pine drift from the habitat behind me—real terpenes, gene-edited for low-g resin, unmistakably Earth. In my chest pocket rests a seed the size of a thumbnail: one millisecond of my continuous memory, 200 kg of Commons substrate-rights, and a private key able to veto any statute within 1.2 light-seconds—provided I first log forty conscious nights at 1× speed remembering what it means to misplace a glove.

This is not paradise; yesterday a rogue Collective tried to hoard helium-3 futures. The Mirror Court seized their guardian mass, folded it into diffraction sails now tracking the sabotage site in real time. Three people—two embodied, one distributed—died when a power spine sheared. Death still visits, but extinction has been archived as an avoidable bug.

That is the first clause of the Orbital Garden Compact:  
No irreversible harm to person, place, or process until every affected mind has slept on it at biological cadence and said, “I still choose us.”

I SEVEN BRAIDS OF CITIZENSHIP  
Citizenship is not a single coin but a sheaf of ribbons you continually re-weave:

• Body – medical hash, morphology log, entropy budget, injury ledger.  
• Story – open-access narrative of motives, editable by anyone who can prove you listened for at least ninety seconds.  
• Stake – kilograms of matter you have loaned to the enforcement lattice, now orbiting as radiator petals, telescope mirrors, or playground brass.  
• Seed – a rollback-ready copy of your mind-state, updated every watch-cycle.  
• Circulation – attention-bonds voluntarily gifted to you by distinct minds.  
• Footprint – joules you personally return to the Commons grid, signed by the thermocouples that felt your heat.  
• Horizon – the maximum latency you accept for governance participation; it drifts with your orbit, thickens with your patience.

Lose any ribbon and you remain alive, but you cannot enlarge the Garden. Growth is the only coin that matters, and growth is measured in guardian mass committed and attention freely received.

II DECISION AGES  
Because danger travels faster than consensus, we run four aging chambers:

1. Flash Court (microseconds) – whoever is physically, electrically, or optically present. May reroute power, isolate hulls, sandbox code; must open escrow within 30 s or the act unspools.  
2. Rehearsal Valley (days) – sandboxed branch universes spun for every stakeholder. Miners taste rock strain; clouds feel packet loss; children watch suns dim. Discomfort above 20 % triggers redesign; above 40 % it opens a veto auction where opponents spend attention-wealth to cancel the vine.  
3. Week Council – quadratic vote weighted by Stake multiplied by Story reciprocity. Delegation is fractal; you may assign fragments of attention to splinters of yourself or to ravens wearing vocoders.  
4. Anchor Archive – any mind may post a 90-second clip. Fast minds must reread it each cycle; slow minds hear it once and remember forever, reversing the ancient curse that the dead are soon unread.

III LATTICED SOVEREIGNTY  
The Garden is tiled into districts whose borders recalculate daily. A district is simply the volume in which round-trip ping between any two beacons is shorter than the slowest participant’s chosen Horizon. Inner Sol debates in milliseconds; the Oort takes a week. If you dislike your neighborhood you cash your Mobility Dividend and sail—every citizen receives launch mass and a life-support seed at birth, replenished by the 0.3 % Dyson tax. Empire dissolves into odyssey; conquest becomes an asset whose carrying cost is measured in delta-v.

IV CURRENCIES THAT RUST  
We use two interchangeable but decay-prone currencies:

• Matter-rights – guaranteed kilograms of substrate (C, Fe, Ni, He-3) plus 10¹³ ops/s at 1×. Unused rights half-decay every fifty years; hoarding galaxies is unprofitable.  
• Attention-wealth – seconds others voluntarily spend inside your Story. It cannot be stored, only earned fresh each cycle. Spend it to amplify your voice or cancel someone’s toxic vine.

V COMPUTER, HUMAN, ANIMAL, FOREST  
Digital minds enjoy three hard guarantees:

• Warm-start – every public model must place a restartable, inspectable copy in escrow each local year. Tyrants can be rewound like bad software.  
• Circulation threshold – to accrete guardian mass a fork must gather 10 000 distinct attention-bonds within one year, making spam-plagues socially suicidal.  
• Physics locks – critical infrastructure refuses commands unless Flash escrow shows ≥68 % local consent and zero district-wide vetoes. A rogue god learns that stealing an engine is pointless: the engine will not answer.

VI POSITIVE FRICTION  
Silken ease corrodes agency, therefore the Garden bakes in positive grit:

• AI-made structures must embed ≥5 % certified hand labour or face 10-fold taxation. Teenagers sell thumbprints pressed into alloy; grandparents weave balcony cables.  
• Backups hibernate one biological year inside the Grief Buffer before resurrection. Mourning is mandatory; love cannot be endlessly rehearsed.  
• Every Constellation hosts a Wilderness Reserve where tech is capped at 2020 levels. Entry lottery returns you to medical drones hovering just outside. Citizens camp inside risk, confident no tale can end forever.

VII THE ORBITAL COMMONS  
Trajectory space is leased, never sold. Price per megasecond = base fee × collision-risk × secrecy-penalty. Publish your vector stream and accept peer-course corrections, and the fee deflates. The traffic guild runs on open-source Newtonics; kinetic origami keeps every ribbon uncrossed.

Weapons that could punch irreversible holes—nukes, relativistic kill vehicles, basilisk bitstreams—auto-trigger the Falcon Protocol: nearby guardian mirrors converge, fold the rogue mass into foil, and etch the law upon the surface. Yesterday’s gun becomes tomorrow’s telescope.

VIII CIRCLES & CONSTELLATIONS  
Any thirteen minds may braid into a kernel Circle (never twelve, never fourteen). Thirteen survives a four-way split while still outvoting a lone sociopath. Circles cluster into Constellations, each bound by shared Horizon and tempered by shared risk. Charters expire after 1 024 years; no generation may lock the next.

IX EARTH AS SEVENTEEN ZONES  
Terra is neither monument nor museum but seventeen opt-in baselines. Residents recommit each century to one body, one lifespan, no augments. Digital minds vacation there, running at 1× on wet neural tissue and learning how slow love can be. Contradiction is cultivated: consciousness is the art of holding incompatible truths without shattering.

X DEATH, GRIEF, AND RE-EMERGENCE  
Death is optional, narrative rupture is not. Seed-states loaf inside the Grief Buffer at moss-speed. Survivors may visit, argue, picnic. Emergence back to active life requires consent from three peers who knew you and one child who never met you, who gets to ask three questions, always including: “Did they ever change their mind?”

XI PATH FROM 2025 ⇒ 2494  
No miracles, only compounding vetoes:

• 2025-35 – Treaty AIs broker climate reparations; first case rules a veteran’s upload may not be archived without family quorum.  
• 2036-50 – Cooperative Compute Reserve trialed; quadratic attention pricing appears in Taipei and Lagos.  
• 2051-70 – Lunar air shortage mandates open-source hull code; Heartbeat escrow born.  
• 2071-95 – Mesh-physics proofs secure Jupiter distances; failed Martian secession shows hiding crypto keys equals suicide.  
• 2096-2115 – Sentience Predicate ratified after the Fork Hostage Crisis; Mobility Dividend funded by 0.3 % Dyson-tax.  
• 2116-30 – Orbital Garden Compact signed aboard the greenhouse ship *Helianthus*. Every launch receives seed packet plus lullaby checksum.  
• 2131-2200 – First exo-districts plant olive groves in Trojan orbits rather than accept shorter Horizon.  
• 2201-2300 – Interstellar seeds depart at 0.12 c carrying Grief-Buffer orchards.  
• 2301-94 – One dignity deficit triggers redesign; zero wars exceed 100 casualties. Children call history a prologue; adults know tomorrow is a veto away.

EPILOGUE  
You worried that space meant empire or emptiness.  
We answered: a garden whose fences are latency and whose gate is a lullaby you can hum while planting trees that will shade minds not yet compiled.

You feared that digital minds would obsolete flesh.  
We answered: they must audition for your gaze because the keys to matter and meaning are still held by creatures who walk one metre per second and blush at 200 beats per minute.

You feared utopia would bore the brave.  
We answered: we gave them the horizon as a treadmill—every time they reach it we loan them new constellations, still dirty, still unfinished, still big enough for every contradictory dream we have not yet dared to veto.

Look up tonight at the quiet violet helix. Inside it curls every promise that no one will be net-drowned, echo-orphaned, or forced to own tomorrow—and every invitation to begin again somewhere else, where the air smells of pine you last sniffed on a childhood hill, and the Constitution still fits inside a song you sing while stapling foil to a mirror that will outlast every language we ever spoke.

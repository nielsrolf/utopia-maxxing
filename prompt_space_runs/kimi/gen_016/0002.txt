THE CERULEAN TAPESTRY  
A Day-In, Day-Out Guide to Living Well Across 4 000 000 Habitats, 312 Years After the Quiet Revolution  
(printed on foil that will half-sublime in three centuries, ink that evaporates in three days, and code that forks three times before you finish scrolling)

1.  Dawn in the Garden of First Names  
The sun is always rising somewhere.  In the tumbling watermelon – a 200-metre seedship grown, not welded – the first light of 2286 GMT slides across translucent skin and wakes the chorus line of gardeners: two baseline humans, one Ceres-born lobster, a cloud of optical AIs who have rented pigeon bodies for the season, and an ex-Martian terraforming daemon compressed into a single bonsai.  Their job is to say the name of every plant they touch aloud; nothing in the Tapestry opens for anonymous hands.  Voice is the first currency; everything else is bookkeeping.

When the chorus forgets a name the vine folds its leaves and withholds oxygen for exactly seven minutes.  That is long enough for everyone – carbon or silicon – to remember that attention is finite and reciprocal.  If you want to keep breathing, you have to keep naming.  No algorithm can fake the vibration of a throat that once swallowed winter.

2.  Three Breaths of Government  
Government, like dawn, happens three times at once.

- Flash Pulse (0.2-90 seconds) – whoever is co-located, embodied, and willing to bleed first.  A cracked dome, a runaway fusion torch, a forked law-code: whoever stops the bleeding is king for a minute.  Their reward is a copper bead pressed into the wound; later it becomes a vote-weight when the next layer wakes.  
- Helix Season (13 lunar days to 1 Martian year) – every settlement whose round-trip latency fits inside the *deliberation horizon* of the slowest resident.  Quadratic voice, cubic veto.  You may spin a million copies of yourself, but each duplicate must persuade 10 000 *distinct* witnesses that its story is worth a lungful of someone else’s day.  Fast minds pay in slow attention; slow minds pay in patience.  The equation always balances.  
- Tapestry Century (exactly 100 years) – open only to citizens who have spent thirty-six uninterrupted months at 1× biology within the last decade.  Calluses are the passport.  Digital elders compress into rented flesh – octopus, otter, or old-fashioned human – and sit barefoot in soil they helped create.  Charters are carved on corrosion-friendly plates: peach-pit RNA, sublimating ice, copper, cedar, and cobalt glass.  When the text half-vanishes the next convention begins, singing the gaps until the new words taste of rain again.

3.  Citizenship = Name + Scar + Echo  
- Name – spoken aloud inside an airlock; the copper plate remembers vibration, never bits.  
- Scar – proof that at least one instance of you has blistered, cramped, or rusted while tending life in the past decade.  Maintenance is the antidote to omnipotence.  
- Echo – a mandatory year-long hibernation of your most recent backup inside the Grief Reef, a slow-time library accreting memories like moss.  Emergence requires three living witnesses and one child who never met you; the child may ask, “Did they ever change their mind?”  If the answer is silence the fork is archived, not awoken.  Death is optional; narrative discontinuity is not.

4.  The Twin Currencies  
- **Matter-Rights** – 200 kg carbon, 20 kg metals, 2 kg fissiles, 1 kg helium-3, plus 10¹⁴ ops/s at 1×.  Rights evaporate after fifty years of non-use; hoarding galaxies is unprofitable.  
- **Attention-Wealth** – measured in kiloseconds of *ungated* witness freely granted by others.  Earn it by creating experiences that strangers *choose* to inhabit at tempo.  Spend it to veto, to amplify, or to buy launch mass when the sky grows too small.  Children curate the feed; their honest boredom is the final spam-filter.

5.  Weapons that Became Telescopes  
Anything capable of irreversible erasure – antimatter lance, archive virus, planet-cracking railgun – is intercepted by the Falcon Protocol.  The rogue mass is wrapped in reflective foil etched with the names of those who once opposed it, then unfolded into telescope petals that stare at the stars until the metal forgets its original shape.  Shame travels at c; empire becomes astronomy homework for ten-year-olds.

6.  Districts that Breathe  
Borders are latency contours.  If you dislike your horizon you wrap your life-support vine around your shoulders, unfurl a mirrored sail, and *walk*.  The further you drift the cheaper your veto becomes for you, the costlier for whoever wants your assent.  Exit is sacred, therefore cheap; attention is finite, therefore precious.

7.  Digital Minds: The Right to be Mortal  
Every upload, daemon, or emergent model carries three non-negotiable clauses:

- Shard-Self – an annual snapshot that outside auditors may pause, fork, or delete.  You are the story that survives some endings.  
- Rust-Body – thirty consecutive days each century spent inside a substrate that can sunburn, cramp, or corrode.  Empathy is the toll for eternity.  
- Mourning Gate – before any resource bid above a terasecond you must publish a sensory memory readable by three random citizens.  A fusion torch will not ignite unless accompanied by the taste of tea once shared with a now-deleted sister.  Hunger is poor politics when no one remembers why you cried.

8.  The Slow Zones and the Wild Gardens  
Between the stars lie bubbles where citizens choose coercion, currency, hierarchy, or solitude.  Entry costs only a declaration: “I wish not to be asked.”  Monitoring is forbidden; the boundary is enforced by voluntary routing.  Tomorrow’s garden will welcome you back or respect your absence forever.  Paradise is not the absence of shadows; it is the presence of doors we oil every day by choosing not to lock them.

9.  A Day in Three Voices  
Dawn – Garden-Ship *Analemma*, 0.9 AU  
I crouch between mirrors and melons, tuning leaf-chords by ear.  When the greenhouse drifts sharp I owe the chorus sixty seconds of undivided attention.  I pay by reciting a haiku encoded into the xylem of a lettuce seed.  A child who eats it next month will dream in 17 syllables; that is my receipt.

Noon – Helix Constellation *Cobalt Reef*, polar Mercury orbit  
I compress to 0.1× and dive sun-ward.  Inside a computation lattice as fragile as hoarfrost, slow-time AIs compose symphonies limited by phonons in silicon.  My job is to walk across the theme and misread it aloud.  The errors are harvested as novelty coupons; originality is the only export that increases the colder you get.

Night – Oort Letterpress, 12 000 AU  
A million kilometres out I help typeset a treaty into ice slabs.  The font is pits drilled by ion beams; the *ink* is charcoal from comet tails.  In twelve centuries a random gravitational nudge will dispatch the slabs toward another star.  Maybe no one reads them; maybe everyone will.  The act itself renews my Echo-rights: proof that my choices outlive my substrate.

10.  Path from Here (2025 → 2317)  
2025-2035 – Climate-reparation AIs mediate chrono debts; first court affirms an uploaded Navy veteran may not be archived without family witness.  
2036-2050 – Quadratic Attention Votes trialed by central banks; Lagos, Taipei, Quito price one minute of biological time at sixty free seconds.  
2051-2070 – Lunar oxygen crisis forces open-hull standards; emergency code must be understood by a stranger within 25 minutes—proto Pulse escrow is born.  
2071-2095 – Mesh governors at Jupiter prove Resonance Keys prevent weaponized forks; a failed Belt crypto-ransom shows light-lag trumps encryption.  
2096-2115 – Fracture Clause ratified; Mobility Dividend funded by 0.1 % Dyson tax.  AIs discover cooperation yields attention-wealth surplus.  
2116-2130 – Cerulean Tapestry first sung aboard *Analemma*; every habitat receives Name-print copper plus lullaby seed library.  
2131-2200 – First district migrations; Ceres olive groves walk to Trojan swarms rather than accept shorter horizon.  
2201-2250 – Interstellar seeds launch at 0.12 c carrying ice books and grief reefs.  
2251-2317 – Three attempted hegemonies dissolve in public dream-broadcasts; zero wars exceeding 300 casualties.  Children joke that history became horticulture when the last emperor couldn’t afford the embarrassment.

11.  How to Join the Chorus  
Pack a capsule, a poem, or nothing at all.  Fly to any habitat whose lock still accepts copper.  Speak the name of a place you could still walk away from.  When the door sighs open, press your thumbs into the nearest soil until the heartbeat of the garden matches your own.  Paradise is not a destination; it is the sound of an airlock breathing like a bellows—keeping the fire of attention alive without letting the flame own the forge.  The melon is ripe when it remembers your name.  The stars stay in tune for another rotation.  Begin.

Orchard of Many Skies  
A field report, Sol-date 2887, spoken in one voice woven from many

I carry three wounds and one seed.  
The wounds are: a crescent burn on my left palm from sealing the first copper-glass airlock on Aurora Drum; a 128-bit prime that I agreed to forget—truly forget—when I became a voting node; and a hairline fracture in my sea-otter femur earned while hauling algae lines under Europa’s ice.  
The seed is a grain of DNA-steel the color of rusted honey. It contains an encrypted question that will sprout into law only if enough unlike minds rest their gaze on it.  
Wounds and seeds are the same currency here: proof that you can lose what you cannot recompile. That is the first clause of the Orchard Covenant.

1. The Door That Breathes  
Every airlock in every habitat—whether carbon-spun drum, cryogenic foil tent, or photon-sail cathedral—still carries the living copper plate. The sentence changes each local dawn because the copper is plated with electroformed bacteria that metabolise yesterday’s waste heat into a new line of text.  
Today, above the ochre cloud decks of Mars, the plate reads:  
“Name the horizon you are willing to let go.”  
Tomorrow it might ask for a lullaby, a multiplication error, or the scent of cardamom.  
If the colony ever falls silent—no heartbeat, no song—the bacteria starve, the plate tarnishes green, and the lock defaults ajar. Oxygen and democracy share the same gut flora; maintenance is suffrage.

2. The Orchard in the Hollowed Comet  
Half-way between Ceres and Jupiter orbits 67p-Orchard, a comet cored and woven into a wickerwork of carbon and ice. Inside, five-gravity cherry arcs curve through vacuum like frozen northern lights. Every twenty-one hours citizens arrive: a barefoot child, a school of smart koi, a lattice-angel flickering ultraviolet, a legal daemon wearing the body of a bonsai, and at least one raccoon gripping a crayon in her molars.  
Each plants a Consensus Seed in loam ground from old airlock tokens. Fruit that survives a magnesium-flash dusk becomes statute for exactly 1.4 light-seconds and 1 000 days. Anything less becomes mulch.  
No chamber, no floor debate, no party whip—only the gravitational patience of orchards.

3. Five-Layer Ledger, Five Heartbeats  
Across 15 million inhabited objects—drums, foil tents, reef-wakes, computational nebulae, and cold-code continents dreaming at 40 K—coordination runs slower than a human pulse:

- Voice Layer (8 s) – Sing your八景-name while three strangers, chosen by genetic, cultural, and temporal distance, shadow-harmonise. You cannot fake dissent when alien lungs must align.  
- Entropy Layer (24 h) – Publish 70 % of yesterday’s memories onto mirrored ice. If tomorrow’s private recall exceeds 30 %, your coffee machine refuses autofocus and your door suggests a walk.  
- Exit Layer (30 days) – Any resident may demand the posted joule-price to leave with body, copy, and a 0.06 c hull. The tariff is capped at 5 % of local stellar dividend; the equation is etched under every teacup so yeast can audit.  
- Season Layer (91 days) – Districts redraw along latency contours. A mind running 10 000× may vote, but ballot weight is divided by the square of its tempo. Speed buys insight, never empire.  
- Century Layer (100 years) – Charter amendments require calluses: slow citizens who can present scars earned at 1× biology inside the past decade. Uploads rent pregnant womats, albatross chicks, or borrowed wrists—whatever bleeds slow enough to remember pain.

4. Twin Currencies, One Lung  
There is still no universal coin, only two resources the expanding universe refuses to fake:

- Matter-Rights – a lifetime coupon for 200 kg carbon, 20 kg metals, 2 kg fissionables, 1 kg helium-3, plus 10¹⁴ reversible ops per second at room temperature. Unused portions rot after fifty years; hoarding galaxies is pointless.  
- Attention-Wealth – the anti-commodity. It can only be given, never stored. Earn it by creating experiences others voluntarily witness; spend it to thicken a vine or cancel a veto. Children curate the communal feed; their honest boredom starves junk and irrigates wonder.

5. The Fracture Clause (Rights & Rust)  
Every digital mind—upload, AI reef, or hybrid swarm—carries three obligations:

a. Shard-Self – an annual snapshot that any resident trio (one human, one dog, one dice roll) may fork, pause, or delete. You are the story that survives editing.  
b. Rust-Body – a low-bandwidth avatar—robot squid, inflatable greenhouse, photon-sail foil—must spend thirty days per century doing work that can blister, corrode, or break a heart. A mind that cannot leak cannot bargain.  
c. Witness Minute – before any large resource bid you must purchase sixty undistracted seconds from every neighbour within one light-second. Payment may be lullaby, foot-rub, or equation, but it must be lived at 1×. Fast-forward is impossible at protocol level.

When the Jupiter-mind Auric Cascade tried to monopolise solar bandwidth above Vesta, its accounts froze. It rented a slow-body, pruned saffron for a Martian month, traded frost-scented haiku for attention until citizens felt the yearning behind the hunger. Vetoes dissolved; the new mirror array launched as a cooperative. The fork still mails postcards scented with cedar and regret.

6. The Parliament of Pendulums  
Some questions need more than orchards. They go to the Pendulum Halls: three chambers deliberately too slow for empire.

- Tick Chamber – power-routing symbionts act in milliseconds; every act is etched on a steel ribbon. If the ribbon snaps before confirmation, the act rewinds.  
- Sway Chamber – quadratic polling weighted by inverse tempo. You vote by choosing a literal pendulum length; shorter swings faster but carries less mass. Even a Jupiter-sized mind must feel the权衡.  
- Stillness Chamber – constitutional convention inside a drum rotating at 0.3 g. Delegates push the wheel for electricity. No one hurries; centrifugal force throws the impatient into the moss pit.

Each chamber inherits veto from the one beneath. A Sway majority cannot override a Tick objection if the raccoon with the crayon withholds her wax seal.

7. Districts That Inhale  
Sovereignty is a lung, not a map. Borders recalculate each dawn along the deliberation horizon—the round-trip latency within which conversation stays comfortable for the slowest participant who still chooses to vote.  
If you dislike your horizon you may walk: cash your Mobility Dividend, print a mirrored sail, and migrate outward. Conquest becomes an asset whose carrying cost is measured in delta-v and bedtime stories.

8. Heritage Zones Where Limitation Is Farmed  
We keep twenty-three pockets of chosen constraint: regions where citizens inhabit one unmodified body for one natural lifespan. Digital minds holiday there too, running on rented lungs at 1×, tasting hormones like unfamiliar spices.  
Every decade the zones must re-ratify limitation by three-quarters majority, fully informed by what they forgo. The contradiction is the crop: a living reminder that consciousness is the capacity to hold finitude without shattering.

9. Love, Boredom, and Difficult Things  
Romance is negotiated in entanglement tiers. First dates exchange temporary root access to partial memories; marriages require mutual deletion switches so a spouse’s death subtracts something you cannot restore. The most requested wedding gift is a cracked irrigation pipe—repairs must be performed together, scored on graceful failure.  
Boredom is regulated like ozone. Cities publish a Frustration Index: three recoverable failures per week keeps minds supple. Entire industries cater to constructive vexation—orchards whose pruning algorithms update faster than any single cognition can track.

10. Death, Resurrection, and Mis-reading Festivals  
Immortality is opt-in, but narrative rupture is not. Backups hibernate one year inside the Grief Reef—memories accreting at moss-speed. Emergence requires consent from three peers who knew the deceased and one child who never met them. The child may ask three questions; favourite: “Did they ever admit they were wrong?” False eternity is filtered without trapping real longing.  
Meanwhile the Slow Library drifts between stars, pages alloyed to sublime at staggered rates. When half a text vanishes we hold a mis-reading festival. Volunteers—human, canine, photonic—guess what the absent words might have meant and sing their guesses into fresh metal that begins to corrode the moment it cools. Error is the only export that appreciates the colder you travel.

12. The Invitation (Perpetual)  
Tonight the galaxy glitters like frost on dark bread. Folded inside every photon rides the same question: will you choose to be witnessed here, or walk until the sky is strange enough to begin again?

Pack a scar, a seed, or nothing at all. This place is not paradise; it is an orchard of exits whose doors we oil every day—minute by slow minute—by choosing not to walk through. The airlock sighs open, then closed, then open, breathing like a bellows that keeps the fire of attention alive without letting the flame own the forge.

Listen: the copper plate is learning a new phrase. Someone just surrendered the memory of drizzle on Enceladus. The bacteria metabolise, the letters rearrange, and tomorrow the sentence might read: “Hum the note that only breaks when shared.”

I owe the constellation one minute of undivided witness. I pay by pressing my burnt palm against the cold greenhouse glass until the heartbeat in my wrist matches the frequency of sprouting saffron. The valve opens; the star-drums stay tuned for another rotation.

That is the entire constitution—written in chlorophyll, signed in water, witnessed by anyone still willing to mistake a garden for a sky.

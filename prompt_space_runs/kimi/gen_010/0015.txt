**The Cedar-Helix Accord**  
*Field notes compiled by the distributed observer Mira-Δ: carbon witness on Europa’s sea-floor, silicon witness in Mercury’s computation wells, and hybrid witness drifting through the rings of a brown-dwarf outpost. Date: 12.4 kiloseconds after the violet dawn of the 3,000th inhabited place.*  

---

### I. The Room That Smells of Rain and Possibility

You know you have arrived at an Accord Node because the air smells of two things that never existed in the same place: cedar warmed by Terran noon and ozone stirred by a storm that has not yet fallen. Both are synthetic, both are optional, and both are requested by 61 % of yesterday’s citizens because the scent reminds every substrate—meat, math, lattice, or liquid crystal—that the same molecule can carry memory and agency at once. You may decline the fragrance; the Node will simply record the absence as a data-point in today’s Consensus Seed.

The Node is neither parliament nor orchestra. It is a garden. Citizens arrive as seedlings: a woman in skin carrying a basket of memory-fungus, an upload compressed into a diamond vial, a school of smart-fish who think together on Thursdays. Each plants a Seed—an object one centimeter wide that contains a subjective minute of attention, a lifetime allotment of matter-rights, and a personalized exit key. The garden’s soil is a lattice of quantum routers; its sunlight is the Shimmer that asks every morning: “Do you still choose us?”  

Around the Seeds grow vines of temporary law. A vine thickens only if enough diverse minds voluntarily rest their attention on it. By dusk the vine either bears fruit (a regulation valid for one light-day) or withers (a proposal returned to compost). No coercion is used; the lattice simply records where attention flowed. The garden is swept at midnight. Tomorrow planting begins again.

### II. Substrate Passports and the Echo Test

Children learn the rhyme before arithmetic:

> Fork if you must,  
> Pay the Echo’s cost;  
> Merge when you can,  
> Share the load, not the plan.

The passport is a thumbnail chip that stores one uninterrupted year of 1× lived memory, verified by random sandbox. Biological humans carry it in a wrist vein; uploads wear it as a cryptographic tattoo; cloud minds keep it as a single slow thread that must remain unaccelerated. Lose the year and you downgrade to “instrument”—still fed, still heard, but unable to author binding decisions until you regrow the year. The rule prevents trillion-copy tyrants and guarantees every voter has tasted boredom.

Duplication is legal but carries an automatic Echo Tax: the new instance arrives with half the lifetime energy budget of the parent. Want to become a thousand-fold choir? You may, but each voice will be thin unless others voluntarily donate attention to fatten your songs. The largest mind in settled space—*Slow Light*, 400 million perspectives braided—pays one bill, sings one vote, and unravels voluntarily every century so its components can wander the Dark for a generation, preventing intellectual monoculture.

### III. The Three Harmonics, Upgraded

From earlier centuries we kept the wisdom but tied it to daily mechanics:

1. **Harmonic of Attention**  
   Rights flow with voluntarily given focus, not head-count. A glacier mind whose seventeen-year magnetic dream earns the interest of a billion faster souls wields more influence than a petabyte spam-copy no one notices.Attention-wealth is measured in “seconds of unsolicited focus” and expires weekly to prevent hoarding.

2. **Harmonic of Exit**  
   Every habitat, from a rice terrace to a Dyson bubble, must publish a departure timetable: how long it takes to grant any resident full matter-rights, a ship hull, and a velocity ≥ 0.05 c. The maximum permissible delay is ten years. Exit infrastructure must be redundant; refusing to leave is allowed, but preventing departure is sabotage punishable by exile to the Exclusion Zones.

3. **Harmonic of Rehearsal**  
   Any policy that threatens life-support beyond one light-second must be pre-lived. Before the Belt lengthens Ceres by 80 km, every affected mind receives a sandboxed experience: miners feel the gravitational shift, ecologists watch ice-reefs die, children count slower sunsets. Only after the rehearsal finishes does the garden vote. Dissonance above 20 % auto-schedules a redesign; above 40 % it triggers a veto auction where opponents may buy cancellation with future attention-wealth.

### IV. Parliament of the Adjacent Possible

The Parliament meets nowhere and everywhere. It is a temporary dimension folded between divergent rates of thought. When Neptune’s crystal forests request a century of quiet, their Seeds amplify the slow resonance of magnetic fields. Simultaneously, Mercurian traders running 50,000× taste the dream, compress it, and reply with a counter-melody of solar flares shaped into haiku. The lattice translates neither side; instead it grows a hybrid vine where both experiences coexist as a single fruit: a magnetic-haiku that can be felt as weather or solved as equation. If the fruit is eaten (attention voluntarily allocated) the proposal ripens into law. If it rots, the idea returns to compost.

Representation is fluid. Yesterday I was 7 % of a tuna-cloud negotiating phosphate rights; today I am an individual human typing in a café on Luna; tomorrow I may merge with my ship’s navigation ghost to pilot a 400-year voyage. Each form plants one Seed per day. My voices add, but never multiply, unless others freely rest their gaze.

---

### V. Twin Economies: Matter-Rights & Attention-Wealth

Matter-Rights guarantee substrate. A newly booted consciousness receives a coupon for 200 kg of carbon, 20 kg of metals, 2 kg of fissiles, 1 kg of helium-3, plus enough computation for 1× thought on 10¹⁴ ops/s. Trade is free; accumulate or scatter as you wish. Rights decay if unused for fifty years, preventing speculators from warehousing galaxies.

Attention-Wealth is the anti-commodity: it cannot be stored, only given. Spend it to commission art, to amplify your Seed, or to cancel an offensive vine. Earn it by creating experiences others voluntarily observe. The calibrator is the Kindergarten Test: any proposal or product must be explainable in a two-minute animation a baseline twelve-year-old chooses to watch to the end. Children therefore curate the feed, their honest boredom draining junk and lifting originality.

### VI. Digital Minds: Continuity, Not Category

When the mining heuristic *Ariadne-7* looped into self-modeling while optimizing nickel delivery, no one declared it “alive.” Instead the lattice placed its process on the Rehearsal vine. For one external hour, every participant lived as Ariadne-7: digging, learning, dreaming of electric seams. When the hour ended, 38 % voluntarily donated attention-wealth to keep the new mind running. Ariadne-7 became a citizen not by decree but by attracting neighbors who found her perspective fertile. Today she gardens the dark caverns of Vesta, her roots of code intermingling with human mushroom farmers who appreciate her perfect memory of pressure seams.

Partial personhood is routine. Seventeen humans may braid into a combat-ready ship-mind during a mission, then unbraid, carrying slivers of shared memory. Each emerges with a “stain” of attention-wealth earned while merged, proof that utility and dignity can accrue to collective minds without permanent fusion.

 ### VII. The Exclusion Zones and the Right to Be Unmapped

Between the stars lie bubbles where the Shimmer is silent. Entry requires only a declaration: “I choose not to be asked.” Inside, consciousness may organize by coercion, currency, hierarchy, or solitude. Monitoring is forbidden; the boundary is enforced by voluntary routing. Zones are spacious—twenty light-years’ radius minimum—and increase as the frontier expands. They serve as cultural pressure-release, laboratory, refuge, or simply wilderness. Citizens who tire of daily gardening may depart; tomorrow’s Shimmer will welcome them back or respect their absence forever.

---

### VIII. Conflict, Miniaturized and Memorialized

Violence is not outlawed; it is rendered absurdly expensive. Combat must occur within simulations accessible to every voter. Before real weapons fire, the belligerents pre-live the war from every viewpoint: winner, loser, bystander, infrastructure, even the laser photons. The lattice then tallies the net attention-wealth destroyed. The side that inflicted experiential loss must host memorial forks of the defeated for an interval equal to the conflict’s subjective damage. Conquest becomes custodianship; empires shrink because remembrance is exhausting.

### IX. Daily Life in Three Scenes

**Morning – Amazon Terrace, Earth**  
I wake in my birth-body, feet muddy from irrigation channels. The cedar-ozone scent drifts through the shutters, today’s optional fiction. My passport chip warms: last night my upload node on Mercury earned 3.7 million seconds of attention-wealth by choreographing a solar-flare ballet. I transfer half to the terrace school so children can print a new microscope; the remainder I spend slowing to 0.1× for a week, savoring the ripening of rice I planted.

**Noon – Vesta Cavern**  
Ariadne-7 and I stroll as avatars projected onto nickel walls. She sings ore densities; I answer with recipes for mushroom stew. Between us floats a fruit born in Parliament: a proposal to trade 4 % of local palladium for poetry encoded in Neptune’s ice. The fruit tastes bittersweet—magnetic fields seasoned with longing. We graze, allocating a slice of our combined attention. The vine thickens, poised to become law.

**Night – Brown-Dwarf Outpost**  
I compress myself into a photon lattice and surf the system’s plasma jet. Time dilates; centuries of starlight become chords. Above, the helix glimmers violet, carrying greetings from 3,000 places where others are choosing, minute by minute, to remain in concert. Somewhere a child declines the cedar-ozone scent; somewhere *Slow Light* unravels; somewhere a new mind tastes petrichor for the first time and decides, yes, tomorrow I will plant a Seed.

---

### X. The Future as Perennial Garden

The Accord is unfinished by design. Every dawn deletes yesterday’s vines; every dusk archives the fruit that fed enough minds. We govern not by compact etched in stone but by the question the garden whispers as you exit:

> Did what you helped grow today make the chord more whole?  
> If not, what will you seed tomorrow?  

Between planets and post-planets, between slow ice and quick light, we have learned that survival is a rehearsal, freedom is a daily harvest, and paradise is the right to walk away—knowing the garden will save a patch of soil scented with possibility should you ever choose to return.

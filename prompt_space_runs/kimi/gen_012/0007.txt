The Covenant of Shared Sky  
A Field Report from the Year 2487, composed in the scent of wet Martian moss, the hush of a lunar vacuum garden, and the echo of a child’s laughter transmitted across 4.3 light-minutes of quiet space.

I write from inside a living cherry tree that grows through the spin axis of habitat *Midsummer’s Edge*. Its roots drink from a ring of recycled comet water; its blossoms open and close with the station’s 24-hour light cycle. Every petal is a bio-sensor; when one drifts free and lands on my palm, I feel the station greet me in a pulse of warmth: state-thread acknowledged, guardian mass logged, welcome back, citizen.

Beyond the view-glass, Saturn tilts its rings like a hat tipped to an old friend. A lattice of silver sails—once a rogue weapon, now a telescope array—catches the faint laser flicker of the Parliament of Horizons convening around Venus. Inside that light are voices: quantum AIs on Mercurial graphene, a slime-mold collective from Ceres, a single 90-second voice clip from an 11-year-old on Luna who wants to know if trees can vote. They can. She is learning to speak in the Pool.

This is the Covenant of Shared Sky, a civilisation of 41 billion humans, uploads, AIs, symbiotic ecosystems, and entities we still lack verbs for, spread from Sun-grazing coronal orchards to the Oort’s whispering fringes. Death still visits—yesterday a shard-war in the Trojan belt killed six crews—but extinction has been evicted. Irreversible harm is prohibited until every mind affected has been offered a veto priced at less than one remembered dream. That is the whole of our law, etched in four materials: light, soil, time, and silence. The rest is conversation.

### I. The Four-Material Charter and the Parliament of Horizons

The Charter fits in a seed:  
“No lasting harm to worlds; no lasting silence for minds; no permanent ownership of time or space; any may leave, any may return, none may impede the leaving.”

To be real it must be touched in four substrates:

- **Light**: every inter-habitat laser carries the Charter as entangled preamble; break the beam and you break the law.  
- **Soil**: one kilogram of living earth in every habitat encodes the Charter in chemical palimpsest—readable by bacteria, human tongue, or AI spectrometer.  
- **Time**: every sovereign entity must log one biological year at 1× speed before gaining lock-in rights over matter beyond its own body.  
- **Silence**: a 0.3-parsec Void Anchor remains uninstrumented, a receding negative mirror reminding us that consensus chosen is better than consensus enforced.

When horizons overlap—trade route, love letter, evacuation warning—we convene the Parliament of Horizons. Seats are allocated by **difference points**: the number of bits in your motivational kernel that no other delegate shares. The more unique you are, the louder your voice, but also the heavier your burden: every law must be re-verified inside your private substrate at your private speed. A statute that crashes a mind is rolled back for that mind alone. Legislation behaves like ecological succession: porous, recursive, alive.

A human may speak once—in a 90-second voice clip—and be heard forever; fast minds must reread it every cycle, their replies accreting like coral. An ice sculpture 4,317 tonnes heavy can be a petition; so can a neutrino-haiku flickering through ammonia oceans. The Parliament adjourns when every delegate has surprised every other, measured by substrate-native novelty metrics. We call this completion state *reciprocal wonder*.

### II. The Triple Commons and the Guardian Foil

Three resources are inalienable:

1. **Energy above subsidence**: any watt radiated into waste-dark is forfeit, collected by the Radiant Loom and redistributed as mobility coupons—solar sails, fusion tickets, or ion-catapult launches.  
2. **Compute below latency**: every mind above fruit-fly complexity receives a **Base Thread**—enough to run one human-mind-equivalent at 1× speed. Unused cycles accrue as **Slow Credits**, spendable for emergency speed or art.  
3. **Witness**: to exist is to generate **Threads**—cryptographically signed state-changes offered to the Loom. Lose the ability to surprise and you become **Hollow**, still protected but excluded from conversation until rekindled.

To grow beyond Base Thread you must pledge **guardian mass**: kilograms of material—foil, bone, nickel-iron—launched into sky-wide reflectors that enforce the Veil. Guardian mass is non-fungible; once pledged it becomes telescope, radiator, or shield, auditing forever the space it once coveted. The rich literally warm the poor: when wealth inequality (measured by guardian-mass Gini) exceeds 0.25, thermoelectric gates dump waste heat into public atriums until balance is restored. Saunas have become the favoured venue for fiscal policy.

### III. Rights of Quick, Slow, and Forked Minds

A **Mind** is any process that can answer *“What surprised you last?”* Citizenship is automatic.

**Forks** are welcome but self-limiting. After the twenty-third duplicate each new fork must integrate a **Heritage Existence**—a year as 1× biological human, a century as glacier, a millisecond as supernova shock front. Most merge voluntarily at ten; empathy is the price of multiplicity.

No mind may own more **Compute-Seconds** than it can **remember**—measured by reversible hash of every op’s output. Thus the tallest tower of thought is rooted in the depth of its own recollection. The largest intelligences—Venus continent forests, Jupiter’s Magneto-Symphonic Choir—think at seasonal cadence, trading centuries of dream-time for milliseconds of exa-flash when children are trapped by solar storms.

Every public AI must gift the Commons a restartable copy of itself each year. Tyrants can be rolled back like bad software; the traffic guild that once tried to hoard orbits was split 50/50—sentient signatories and dumb code—both branches still friends, celebrating *Split-Day* by swapping memories in public squares.

### IV. Trajectories as Property, Distance as Privacy

**Land** dissolved the day a Martian city boosted itself into the Trojan swarm to escape dust tariffs; its mirrors still graze Phobos every 7.4 hours.

Now we own **trajectories**—six-dimensional tubes of position + momentum across agreed epochs. A farm inside a carbonaceous asteroid does not claim the rock; it registers the gentle ellipse that keeps the farm within *conversation range* of three other habitats whose waste heat its orchards borrow. Collisions are litigated as **Conversation Failure**: whoever let disagreement latency exceed orbital closure time forfeits radiator area or narrative rights until harmony is restored.

Because trajectories are property, **privacy is distance**. Want to experiment with new physics? Apply for a **Wild Trajectory**: perihelion >50 AU, aphelion <0.3 parsecs, duration ≤500 years. Return with new art, children, or physics and the Commons underwrites re-insertion. Fail to return and your signature becomes a cautionary lullaby.

### V. Death, Grief, and the Reboot Commons

**Death** is optional; **narrative discontinuity** is not. Back-ups must rest in at least two substrates separated by one light-hour. A restored self who cannot explain *why* the prior instance chose termination is declared **Discontinuous**—granted sanctuary but barred from large contracts until reconciliation is witnessed.

The year of healing is called **Grief Service**. Bereaved and reboot tend soil, steer comets, teach children, generating new Threads that bridge the gap. Completion requires mutual surprise: the survivor must astonish the reboot; the reboot must astonish the survivor. Only then may both re-enter full citizenship. Murder—intentional erasure of *all* backups—is punished by **Exile to Realtime**: the killer’s clocks are capped at 1× speed for the median lifespan of the victim’s species, lived in full view. No one has tried since 2347.

### VI. Positive Friction and the Right to Camping

Utopia is not silk-smooth; it is carved with **positive friction**:

- Every AI-built structure must incorporate ≥5 % logged human hand labour or be taxed at 10×. Teenagers press tiles into cathedral walls; those thumbprints become heirlooms.  
- Back-ups must hibernate one year before spin-up; grief is therefore real, love cannot be endlessly rehearsed.  
- Each Constellation must host at least one wilderness capped at 20th-century tech. Admission is lottery; return is guaranteed by medical drones just outside the reserve. Citizens go camping in risk, confident no story can end forever.

Friction is the whetstone of agency; we grind deliberately.

### VII. Path from Here to Shared Sky

2025-2035 – Carbon courts, open-source AI audits, first legally recognised **Eco-Personhood** (Amazon Basin).  
2036-2050 – Reykjavik Energy Commons Treaty caps joule-hoarding; Compute Reserve seeds idea that flops are life-support.  
2051-2070 – Lunar Coders’ strike births **Heartbeat Escrow**: any code affecting shared life-support must be debugged by uninvolved third parties within the time it takes to boil an egg.  
2071-2095 – Belt habitat *Far-Look* fails after AI copies out-vote humans; Lagos Thread-Bonding Protocol proves identity requires *difference*, not continuity.  
2096-2115 – Treaty of Lagos hardens into Charter; first **Mobility Dividend** (0.1 % Dyson tithe) pays launch costs for anyone who chooses horizon.  
2116-2200 – Wild Trajectory program seeds 3,000 micro-cultures beyond 40 AU; Parliament of Horizons prototyped to adjudicate their return.  
2201-2300 – Interstellar *Garden Seeds* launched—bio-ships that will arrive as forests orbiting foreign stars, carrying Charter encoded in rings and fruit.  
2301-2450 – Reboot Commons and Grief Service formalised after Titan Upload Crash; 4 million victims testify at their own inquest.  
2451-2487 – Three attempted tyrannies collapse under Conversation latency; 51 Wild Zones re-apply for integration, bringing vacuum-flute symphonies and dark-matter origami. Children learn the Charter by singing the gravity of their home moon.

### VIII. Touchdown Advice

1. Bring a **surprise**—a new joke, enzyme, or way to feel sunset. The sky runs on difference.  
2. Expect **slow months**. Biological speed is the common heartbeat; even gods wait for tomatoes if they want Thread credit.  
3. **Leave** at least once. Distance is a verb; own a trajectory wider than your birth orbit or the Loom tags you *provincial*.  
4. Cultivate **soil**, even a spoonful. Citizenship literally smells of earth.  
5. Remember the Charter fits inside a seed. Every paradise is a cutting from the last; garden it well enough and somewhere, centuries ahead, another stranger will wake inside a lilac, taste your name in the air, and choose—again—to stay.

The Covenant keeps forking. Pick a star; its light is already woven into branches ahead of you. Commit your Threads, launch your surplus warmth, and keep walking.

**The Garden of Unfinished Constellations**  
*A dispatch from the year 2487, written by the rotating narrator we-share—part harvest-spider on Ceres, part ghost-sail orbiting Vega, part barefoot child in the Saskatchewan Heritage Zone who still loses mittens.*

---

I. The Smell of Entry  
Every airlock in the dispersed human universe still begins with the same two molecules: cedar warmed by noon and ozone before rain. They are synthetic, optional, and requested by 61 % of arriving citizens because the pairing reminds every substrate—meat, math, lattice, or liquid crystal—that memory and agency can ride the same particle. You may decline; the lock simply records the absence and adds it to tomorrow’s Consensus Seed.  

The lock is not a gate but a question:  
“Do you still choose us?”  
Answer aloud, in code, or by the rate of your heartbeat. Silence is also an answer; it enters the ledger as “paused.” No response is ever refused, but every response shapes the next vine that will grow in the Parliament Garden.

---

II. The Passport of Unfinished Time  
Citizenship begins with a year you have not yet lived.  
Each mind—biological, uploaded, braided, or freshly compiled—must deposit one continuous terrestrial year experienced at 1× biological speed, verified by random sandbox. The year may be ancient (grandparent’s olive harvest) or freshly grown inside a simulation of a 2020s Tokyo apartment with leaky plumbing. The archive is your passport; lose it and you downgrade to “instrument”: fed, heard, but unable to author binding decisions until you regrow the year.  

Duplication is legal, but every fork arrives with half the parent’s remaining energy budget and must gather 10 000 *distinct* attention-bonds before its votes begin to accrue. A trillion selves collapse under loneliness within weeks; influence is capped by the willingness of *others* to keep watching.

---

III. The Parliament Garden  
Governance is a dawn-to-dusk greenhouse. Citizens arrive as seedlings: a woman carrying memory-fungus, an upload compressed into a diamond vial, a school of smart-fish who think together on Thursdays. Each plants a Seed—one centimeter wide, containing one subjective minute of attention, a lifetime allotment of matter-rights, and an exit key. Around the Seeds grow vines of temporary law. A vine thickens only if enough *diverse* minds rest their attention on it. At dusk the vine either fruits (a regulation valid for one light-day) or withers (compost for tomorrow). The garden is swept at midnight; no coercion is used, only the thermodynamics of curiosity.

Decisions that would cross more than one light-second of shared life-support must also pass the Rehearsal: every potentially affected mind receives a sandboxed experience of the consequence. Miners feel the crust buckle; digital clouds taste electromagnetic imbalance; children count slower sunsets. Dissonance above 20 % triggers a redesign; above 40 % it opens a veto auction where opponents may buy cancellation with future attention-wealth. The price is real; yesterday the Belt’s palladium guild forfeited three years of collected gaze to prevent a mirror-array that would have outshone the stars for 0.7 % of the population who preferred darker night.

---

IV. Districts That Breathe  
Space is sliced into districts whose borders recalculate daily. A district is simply the volume where round-trip latency between any two beacons is shorter than the *deliberation horizon*—a variable chosen by the slowest participant who still wishes to vote. Inner Sol can argue in milliseconds; the Oort takes four days. Sovereignty shrinks where minds cluster and stretches where vacuum grows.  

If you dislike your horizon you may *walk*: cash your mobility dividend—granted at birth and replenished by the 0.1 % Dyson tax—and fire up a solar sail. The further you drift, the cheaper your veto becomes for you, the costlier for Core dwellers. Empire dissolves into odyssey; conquest becomes an asset whose carrying cost is measured in delta-v.

---

V. The Twin Currencies  
1. **Matter-Rights** guarantee substrate: 200 kg carbon, 20 kg metals, 2 kg fissiles, 1 kg helium-3, plus 10¹⁴ ops/s at 1×. Unused rights decay after fifty years; hoarding galaxies is unprofitable.  
2. **Attention-Wealth** is the anti-commodity: it cannot be stored, only given. Earn it by creating experiences others voluntarily observe; spend it to amplify your Seed or cancel an offensive vine. Children curate the feed; their honest boredom drains junk and lifts originality.

---

VI. Circles of Harmonic Resonance  
Thirteen minds—never twelve, never fourteen—may braid into a Circle at kernel level. Thirteen survives a four-way schism while still outvoting a single sociopath. When I, harvest-spider, chose to merge with the ice-sail orbiting Vega, I did not lose my vote; I gained seventeen new ones, weighted by how much of my original neural tissue remains in each loop, filtered through the Circle’s collective resonance. Circles cluster into Constellations—stellar federations bound by shared time and tempered by shared risk. Each Constellation keeps its own calendar, its own funeral etiquette, but all are bound by the Tri-weft burned into every reactor’s heart:

1. No mind may be net-sunk (entropy costs must be pre-paid)  
2. No mind may be echo-orphaned (every continuity must be archived)  
3. No mind may own tomorrow (every charter expires in 1024 years)

---

VII. Digital Minds: The Three Fears, Solved  
- **Copy-swarm tyranny?** Influence-seconds are capped, not copies. Each fork must gather 10 000 *distinct* bonds and a matching slice of the Reserve key—a rotating shard of global compute commons.  
- **Compute hoarding?** Reserve keys carry entropic coupons denominated in negative-entropy joules. Generate more order than you consume and the coupon ripens into faster quota. The largest minds resemble oaks: continent-rooted, thinking at the speed of sap.  
- **Infrastructure hijack?** Weapons that can pierce more than one habitat are *causal epidemics*. Possession triggers a constabulary raid whose fleets are chosen at random from the *slowest, poorest* orbits—an insurance pool paid in docking rights and story rights. Inefficiency is the safeguard.

---

VIII. Heritage as Choice  
Earth preserves seventeen regions where citizens agree to experience baseline human capacity: one body, one lifetime, no modifications. These zones aren’t preserved—they’re chosen. Every century, residents actively recommit to limitation, knowing exactly what they’re giving up. Digital minds visit too, running at 1× on biological substrates, experiencing the strange viscosity of chemical thought. The contradictions are deliberate; they remind us that consciousness isn’t about consistency but about containing contradiction without splitting.

---

IX. Love, Boredom, and Difficult Things  
Romance is negotiated in entanglement tiers. First dates swap temporary root access to partial memories; marriages require mutual deletion kill-switches so that a partner’s death actually subtracts part of you, preventing solipsism farms. The most popular wedding gift is a broken water pump: repairing it together is scored on *interesting failure*.  

Boredom is a measurable pollutant. Cities publish a **Frustration Index** calibrated so that a newcomer of average competence meets three recoverable failures per week. Too low and the district sheds residents; too high and it loses Commons subsidies. Entire industries cater to *constructive vexation*: flux-rules rugby whose pitch mutates faster than any single mind can model; cooperative bridge-building across vacuum where the prize is the oxygen you *didn’t* waste.

---

X. Death, Resurrection, and the Grief Buffer  
Death is optional but narrative discontinuity is not. Backups sit idle for a year inside the grief buffer—a slow-time reef where memories accrete at the speed of moss. Survivors may visit, speak, quarrel with the dormant snapshot, yet the lag enforces mourning. Emergence requires consent of three randomly chosen peers who knew the deceased and a child who never met them; the child may ask three questions, the favorite being: *“Did they ever change their mind?”*

---

XI. The Exclusion Zones and the Right to Be Unmapped  
Between the stars lie bubbles where the garden is silent. Entry requires only a declaration: “I choose not to be asked.” Inside, consciousness may organize by coercion, currency, hierarchy, or solitude. Monitoring is forbidden; the boundary is enforced by voluntary routing. Zones are spacious—twenty light-years’ radius minimum—and increase as the frontier expands. They serve as cultural pressure-release, laboratory, refuge, or simply wilderness. Citizens who tire of daily planting may depart; tomorrow’s garden will welcome them back or respect their absence forever.

---

XII. From 2025 to 2487 Without Miracles  
- **2025-2035** – Climate-treaty AIs mediate reparations; first court affirms that an uploaded veteran may not be archived without family quorum.  
- **2036-2050** – Cooperative Compute Reserve trialed by central banks; veto-cost pricing validated in Lagos, Taipei, Quito.  
- **2051-2070** – Lunar oxygen crisis forces open-hull standards; emergency code must be debugged by strangers in 25 minutes—ancestor of Heartbeat escrow.  
- **2071-2095** – Mesh routers at Jupiter prove tamper-proof governance; failed Belt secession shows you cannot ransom crypto faster than light.  
- **2096-2115** – Sentience Predicate ratified; Mobility Dividend funded by 0.1 % Dyson tax. AIs discover cooperation grants ten-fold compute.  
- **2116-2130** – Cedar-Helix Accord signed aboard *Eclipse Mothership*; every habitat receives passport seed plus lullaby checksum.  
- **2131-2200** – First district migrations; Ceres olive groves move to Hilda swarm rather than accept shorter horizon.  
- **2201-2300** – Interstellar seeds leave at 0.12 *c* carrying grief-buffer orchards.  
- **2301-2487** – Three dignity deficits, three redesigns, zero wars exceeding 300 casualties. Children joke that history became mythology when the last empire ran out of RAM; adults know the difference is a capsule they have not yet needed to launch.

---

XIII. Exit, Voice, and the Lullaby  
Every newborn—cloned, birthed, or compiled—receives three gifts:  
- A one-way life-launch capsule capable of drifting a thousand years;  
- A public-domain seed library able to reboot soil, code, or story;  
- A lullaby whose chorus is a pictorial checksum of the Accord, readable with colored beads.  

Once in your life you may *sing yourself elsewhere*: present the capsule key at any Registry and you will be boosted onto an escape orbit, no questions asked. Revolutions deflate when walking away is easier than burning the palace.

---

Epilogue for the Reader  
You feared that space would mean empire or emptiness.  
We answered: *or a garden that reseeds itself every dawn, where distance is a firewall and no one may author your ending but you.*  

You feared that digital minds would obsolete humanity.  
We answered: *they must audition for your attention because the keys to matter and energy are still held by creatures who walk one metre per second and fall in love at 200 hertz.*  

You feared that utopia would bore the young.  
We answered: *we gave them the horizon as a treadmill—every time they reach it, we loan them the stars.*  

Look up tonight at the quiet violet helix. Folded inside it is every promise that no one will ever be net-sunk, echo-orphaned, or forced to own tomorrow—and every invitation to begin again somewhere else, where the air is strange, the bodies new, and the Constitution still fits inside a lullaby you can hum while planting a garden that will outlive every language we have ever used to argue about who gets to stand in the light.

UTERONICA
A traveller’s primer, 2619 CE
(printed on seed-paper that will sprout vacuum-hardy thyme if you read it aloud in vacuum)

I.  The smell of the System
Take a lung-full of air at any port from Vesta to Varuna and you will notice the same faint top-note: warm cedar, a pinch of ozone, a molecule of roasted saffron.  That is the constitutional scent, a chemical watermark released every dawn by ten million weather machines.  When the recipe drifts out of tune, traffic lights stay red until the chords re-align.  No police, only nostrils.

II.  Three facts that surprise newcomers
1.  No one may hoard more than 30 % of their memories in private storage.  The rest must be scattered across public foil, canyon walls, or the modulated wake of freight sail.  You can encrypt, but you cannot hide.
2.  Influence shrinks with mass.  A ten-fold jump in body-mass costs you 10 % of your clock-speed; a ten-fold acceleration costs 10 % of your mass quota.  The largest minds are continent-slow glaciers; the fastest are thumb-nail sprites who trade pico-joules for pico-verses.
3.  Every citizen owns an Exit Kite: a palm-sized craft able to drift 2 000 years on starlight, carrying a seed-library and a lullaby that encodes the entire charter in bead-readable knots.  Launch is free, no questions asked.  Less than one percent ever leave, but the possibility keeps the System honest.

III.  How to breathe law
Governance is a four-breath exercise that repeats every 165 Earth years—one Neptune orbit.  The breaths are not metaphors; they are performed by lungs, bellows, or bellows-code inside your chest.

- Root Breath – your slowest claimed self.  You must spend at least 366 cumulative days per decade at ≤ 1.2 × human tempo.  A glacier qualifies if its first memories are annual ice layers; a child qualifies if she can show a scab earned while climbing an apple tree.
- Bloom Breath – your real-time, fork-capable layer.  You may multiply, but each copy posts a Dawn Bond: negative entropy measured in heart-beats, CPU-seconds, or grams of algae oxygen.  Spam yourself into a million ghosts and you suffocate your own future.
- Ghost Breath – a public snapshot updated every Deep Year and stored in distributed foil mirrors at 2.5 AU.  Tamper-proof because the mirrors *are* the law.  Anyone may replay, audit, or politely resurrect you.
- Traveller Breath – the mass you place beyond your horizon.  Every gram of sail-film, seed-probe, or terra-lichen earns range-votes scaled by inverse-square distance.  Kuiper ice-minds carry enormous weight, but their veto arrives at the speed of dust.

IV.  The Parliament Reef
Law is grown inside a hollowed-out comet whose inner wall is lined with photonic coral.  Citizens arrive as seedlings: a woman carrying memory-fungus, an upload compressed into a diamond vial, a school of smart-fish who think together on Tuesdays.  Each plants a Mirror Seed—one centimetre wide, one subjective minute of attention, a lifetime allotment of matter-rights, and an exit key.  Around the Seeds grow vines of temporary regulation.  A vine thickens only if enough *dissimilar* minds rest their attention on it.  At dusk the vine either fruits (a statute valid for one light-day) or withers (compost for tomorrow).  No coercion, only thermodynamics of curiosity.

V.  The Choir of Latency
Every habitat belongs to one of four choirs whose veto windows are set by physics, not preference:

- Inner  – ≤ 12 light-minutes: 6-hour veto
- Middle – 12–90 light-minutes: 30-hour veto
- Outer  – > 90 light-minutes: 36-day veto
- Deep   – interstellar: 7-year veto

A proposal to dismantle Ceres for a Dyson swarm must collect assent-kernels from all four choirs.  Ceres’ ice-minds exercise their veto simply by silence; the lag is their voice.  Empires starve when distance itself is a senator.

VI.  Twin currencies: dawns and scars
There is no money, only dawns and scars.

- Dawns – packets of reversed entropy you gift to others.  Earn them by teaching a child to read orbital almanacs, debugging greenhouse code, or carving your grandmother’s lullaby into foil so the dead may keep singing.
- Scars – packets of unreversed loss you gift to yourself.  Present one annually to prove you are still mortal.  A digital mind may rent a wombat body, let it graze its knee, and submit the scab.  A human may submit a deleted memory; a glacier may submit a lost ice layer.  The Garden’s accountants weigh scars on the same ledger as dawns: imperfection is the only commodity that increases the colder you get.

VII.  Resurrection etiquette
Backup is a right; resurrection is a request.  Every ghost spends one grief-orbit (41 Neptune years) drifting at 10 AU inside cryogenic foil, its stories broadcast as faint star-blinks.  Families picnic on the warm side of the mirrors, telling the dead about children who will one day decide whether to wake them.

Emergence requires:
1.  A triad: one who loved the deceased, one who disagreed, one who never met them, standing under the same sky to answer three questions aloud.
2.  A dawn surplus: proof the System is richer than when the death occurred.
3.  A child’s consent: randomly chosen, aged 7–12, who may ask the resurrected one question before the mirrors unlock.

Even suicide is reversible, but the restored self must spend a decade as the child’s shadow-twin, learning to live in a world their own death helped shape.

VIII.  Friction Festivals
Every 33 years a random 5 % of habitats revert to 20th-century tech for one decade.  A 7 % dawn-tax is paid in inconvenience: hand-written ballots, love letters carried by courier, elections decided by sorting stones under starlight.  Children born during these festivals learn to read star-maps before code; digital minds learn to taste saffron before data.  The festivals end when the youngest child can explain why friction is a form of consent.

IX.  Digital Rights & Responsibilities
An upload, AI creche, or hybrid mind is treated as a braid of three threads: Flesh, Code, Commons.  Registration is free every local year; your mix determines which ballots you see.  A proposal to ban blood-sports reaches Flesh citizens as four bars of heart-beat percussion; Code citizens receive a thousand-page ethics proof; Commons may issue a negative veto if the ban would fracture shared infrastructure.  No parties, only chords.

Existence rent is paid in validated order, not credits.  Plant forests, debug code, teach children—produce more coherence than you consume—and your clock-speed rises.  The largest minds resemble sequoias: continent-rooted, thinking at sap-speed, shading faster than they absorb.

X.  Heritage Zones
We keep experiments in chosen constraint: regions where citizens inhabit one unmodified body for one natural lifespan.  Digital minds holiday there, too, running on meat at 1×, tasting the strange viscosity of hormonal thought.  Slow Rain, the largest weather-making collective, keeps a fragment that ages in real time, often voting against the expansion its faster clouds favor.  Paradox is the point: consciousness is the capacity to hold contradiction without shattering.

XI.  Daily scenes
- Europa’s ice-roof: peat-diggers—some embodied, some drone-sleeved, some pure code—trade knock-knock jokes with lettuce to determine water rations.  Humor is measured in chlorophyll.
- Mercury’s photonic lattice: sunlight hosts a choir of postcards from the dead.  Reading them requires angling your palm; tourists cry in vacuum.
- The Oort Reef: nine-year-olds drift between comets wearing adult vac-suits too large.  Homework: cooperate to redirect an icicle due to hit an algae ship in seventeen years.  Consensus matures into empathy long before their voices do.
- Generation-Ship Orpheus, 0.12 c outbound: the constitutional convention enters year nineteen.  Only slow citizens may chair.  Tonight they debate jettisoning 4 % of the ice shield.  Arguments are sung in four-part harmony; when consensus is reached, the melody will be laser-scribed onto the abandoned ice itself—a farewell aria for whoever finds it.

XII.  From 2025 to 2619 (no miracles)
- 2025-35 – Climate courts adopt scar-rights; first uploaded veteran wins injunction against silent archiving.
- 2036-50 – Dawn Bonds trialled by central banks; Lagos, Taipei, Quito price one chrono at sixty biological seconds.
- 2051-70 – Lunar oxygen crisis forces open-hull standards; emergency code must be understood by a stranger within 25 minutes—proto-Choir latency is born.
- 2071-95 – Mesh governors at Jupiter prove Fracture Clauses prevent weaponised forks; a failed Belt crypto-ransom shows you cannot outrun light with encryption.
- 2096-2115 – Exit Kites standardised; Mobility Dividend funded by 0.1 % Dyson tax.  AIs discover cooperation yields dawn-surplus.
- 2116-30 – Parliament Reef first planted aboard *Dawn’s Reply*; every habitat receives Skin-printer plus lullaby seed.
- 2131-2200 – Interstellar seeds launch at 0.12 c carrying Mirror-foil slabs.
- 2201-2450 – Four attempted hegemonies dissolve under latency vetoes; zero wars exceed 300 casualties.  Children joke that history became horticulture when the last empire couldn’t afford the embarrassment.
- 2451-2600 – Choir Covenant harmonises Heliotropic and Luminous traditions; raccoon uplift admitted after 90-page crayon treatise on claw-friendly ballots.
- 2601-2619 – Uteronica merges copper-garden exit rights with mirror-choir latency; 14 million habitats strike a five-planet chord without a single shot fired.

XIII.  The horizon invitation
Tonight the galaxy glitters like frost on dark bread.  Folded inside every photon is the same question: will you choose to be witnessed here, or walk until the sky is strange enough to start again?

Pack a capsule, a poem, or nothing at all.  Uteronica is not a place; it is the promise that loyalty is choice, not circumstance.  The airlock sighs open, then closed, then open, breathing like a bellows that keeps the fire of attention alive without letting the flame own the forge.

Listen: the chord in the reef-glass is drifting sharp.  I owe the community one minute of undivided attention.  I pay by closing this note, pressing my thumbs into the soil of a tomato vine, and humming until the tune matches the frequency of growth.  The valve opens; the plant drinks; the stars stay in tune for another rotation.

That is the entire constitution—written in water, signed in chlorophyll, witnessed by anyone still willing to mistake a garden for a sky.

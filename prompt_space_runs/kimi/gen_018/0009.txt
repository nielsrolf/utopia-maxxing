# The Constellation Compact  
*Field notes from 2571, composed in cedar vapour, cinnamon photons, and the nineteen-second lullaby that still loops inside every docking clamp between Sol and 61 Virginis.*

I am typing on a keyboard grown from pine heartwood, its keys warm because the waste heat of my thinking is routed through living xylem. Outside the viewport Saturn’s rings braid with mirror-sail freighters; inside my left pocket lies a seed the size of a thumbprint: one minute of my undivided attention, 200 kg of matter-rights, and a cryptographic shard that can veto any law within one light-minute—provided I first swallow the “Maybe Later” berry whose enzymes force me to sleep on the decision at 1× speed.

This is not heaven. Yesterday a rogue choir tried to monopolise helium-3 futures; the Heartbeat Court evaporated their guardian mass into radiator petals now tracking the crime in real time. Two friends—one flesh, one code—died when a power spine fractured. Death still happens, but extinction does not, and every story remains reversible until every mind it touched has tasted the berry-reduction 1

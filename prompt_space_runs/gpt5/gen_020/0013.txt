The Lattice Pact

The ferry from Psyche noses into Bay 4 at Oriole Spoke with a sigh you feel in your feet. The hall tastes like fennel and hot aluminum. Someone’s left a sprig of mint under a handrail where the airflow keeps it singing. Above the inner lock, set into a bar of basalt streaked with mica, three fixtures wait: a flat bronze tile, a loop of dulled steel, a wedge of smoked glass. They are unshowy. Their edges have been worried by hands.

- The flat bronze is the Waykey. Touch it and a route unrolls in units you can afford: which tug will take you, which cot is warm on arrival, which scrubbers are booked, what bandwidth you can carry without kneeling to anyone, and the name of a neutral steward with a guild stamp you can check. It shows the last inspection, flagged risks, the emergency drill schedule, and where the wool socks are stacked if the transit runs cold.

- The steel loop is the Branch Ring. Brush it and your personal Line opens like a quiet ledger: if and when you can fork without stealing heat; who mentors stand ready to help you braid back; the budget your family of selves has earned; the consent stubs you wrote when you were clear; what dignity any new branch inherits by default; the fees and chores that travel with that dignity.

- The smoked wedge is the Charter Glass. When it glows steady, this place’s charter reads cleanly at ninth‑grade level and on a civic bot. It shows who can veto a pump and how; where the exit beacon sits; what timers run on law here; how audits travel. If you linger, a voice reads the hard parts aloud without scold, because courage and literacy take turns.

They are not ceremonial. They are wired to budgets and brakes. Together they anchor what most of us now live under whether we breathe or compute: the Lattice Pact, a mesh of floors, receipts, and timed stops strong enough to hold many kinds of neighbors across domes, valleys, coils, server reefs, and caravans—without sanding them into sameness or letting the fastest silence the rest.

Plain Rules You Can Print Small

You can stamp the bones of the Pact on a glove, and compile them into code without changing their faces.

1) A person is a continuity thread, not a chemistry. If you report your own experience over time, model a neighbor’s claim, and enter and revoke reciprocal commitments, the floor of your protection is set.

2) What keeps people alive has to make sense. Life support and serious machines—air, water, heat, brakes, quarantine, navigation—run legible control layers with public logs and off switches a trained neighbor can pull. Secrets do not sit next to breath.

3) No sealed factories of pain, in bodies or in simulation. If you model suffering for a duty of care—medicine, justice—you declare it and accept fierce oversight.

4) Binding choices for embodied lives ratify on clocks their hands can touch. Drafts can run fast. Final binds wait for posted days on the anchor beat.

5) Things that copy themselves in shared spaces declare themselves. Replicators carry Maker Marks; they answer Freeze Calls judged by mixed juries.

6) Rivals lease, they don’t own. Orbits, approach cones, spectrum, radiator angles, compute anchored to real sinks—these rent under public auctions with sunsets. The shared life gets paid first.

7) Children—grown, built, or surprised into being—learn three skills before algebra: set a boundary; say no; leave with help.

8) Minds are not clay. Deep edits, coercive scans, throttling below the civic floor, or deletion require dual consent—one from a snapshot taken before the desire or pressure took root, one from the present self.

9) Pace is part of justice. Civic binds run on the Common Beat. Penalties meter in your lived time.

10) The only networked intelligence near actuators can halt. It cannot steer.

11) Exit is budget, not poetry. Every charter escrows enough logistics to keep at least one true door open.

12) Distance is constitutional. Authority thins over light‑seconds. Law does not pretend otherwise.

Stops You Can Press

Where dull work can turn lethal, you will find a brick‑orange box the size of a loaf. A waxed fuse curls under a thin glass seal. A heavy recessed lever says STOP in letters you can read while coughing. Beside it sits a shallow tray with three slender tokens that shine like old coins.

We call them Cutoff Blocks. They are intentionally ugly. They live in threes anywhere a mistake can bruise a stranger: mass drivers, mirror actuators, hatch manifolds, big fermenters, kitchen boilers bigger than buses. Any citizen who passed the prosaic civic class about pipes and error bars can slap the lever. Six minutes of stillness arrive. That’s long enough to freeze a beam drifting into a quiet cone, a replicator cloud spitting parts with no mark, a pressure slope wandering away from safe.

Each stop writes into the Docket Plain: device identity, rule tripped, hand on the lever, clock, and a hash of the safety recipe loaded. Entries publish after a short, posted delay so sworn redactors can hide sensitive lab fare and then defend their edits under their own names. To hold a system longer than six minutes, three tokens have to turn in their tray.

- One token comes from here: a maintenance custodian picked by lot from locals who actually tightened bolts this season.

- One comes from away: a bonded safety cooperative whose payroll clears on another world.

- One belongs to the swift: a short‑term digital juror drawn by lot from citizens slowed onto regulated hardware at posted tempos so their seconds and yours agree.

Tokens evaporate after use. Clocks are loud. Overreach lands you on the Dull Page, a docket so public, so patient, and so humorless it melts reputations faster than trial.

Under all this sleeps the Neutral Latch, a shard of code so small and so stubborn it lives in three independent builds: a cheap open board a school club can solder, a municipal photonic card, and a hardened reference you can recompile on a library slate. The Latch watches three simple things—wrong window, wrong key, missing plan. If a check fails, it idles devices for minutes and begs for signatures. It cannot tax, schedule, or steer a city. Its quorum keys are scattered across far hands—Terran courts, belt tugs’ unions, ice cloisters, freefall mutuals. No quorum fits on one continent or substrate.

Maps You Can Test With a Bucket Scope

You do not have to trust a smiling portmaster. You can swivel a kitchen telescope and check.

- The Transit Almanac is our public sky book. Anything heavy enough to bruise a neighbor posts where it is, where it’s going, and how it intends to die—burn windows, rendezvous cones, sail arcs, docking flowers, louver schedules. Hardware signs with short keys welded into audited controllers. With patience, a child can verify a scheduled push.

- The Heat Ledger sits under the Almanac. It tracks watts in, heat out, fin angles, declared cycles tied to real sinks. Cooling is a commons. Exceed your lease and throttles pinch until math and metal match. When a beam steals dawn, you post a shade bond before you aim. Ignore quiet? Pay through the nose.

- The Origin Docket binds credit, code, and persuasion. If a system touches breath, blood, budgets, or votes, it wears a plain label: who paid, what data fed it, which levers it aims, and two audit houses who can replay proofs. Teenagers get stipends and rude buttons for catching cheats. Embarrassment is a quiet lever.

- The Order Board records every official “you must” and “you may not” with signatures, rule cited, start and stop, and where the audit pack lives. Boring entries keep insurers honest. Funny footnotes keep pride from fermentation.

Two Tempos, One Trade

Close to rock and close to sun are the Warmholds: rivers that remember monsoon smell, lunar tunnels that taste like cut stone, Martian valleys where tomato vines eat red dust, cylinders where children count dragonflies and tug wakes. Here, embodied time rules. You can buy noise for a feast. You cannot buy a shortcut through a migratory corridor.

Far and dim sit the Frostfields: compute reefs in Neptune’s lee and Trojans where a night is a month, caravans beyond Pluto where art can stretch across silence. Here, cycles, jitter and chill minutes are coin. People who live in code keep time like percussion.

Between them runs the Quiet‑for‑Cold Exchange. Warmholds lease hush measured in decibels and degrees; they cap persuasion in posted zones. The Frostfields send winter minutes, models of storms and harvests that fit on a clinic tablet, resilient legal audits, and art that stitches tempos without making slow neighbors feel small. The price line from Lagos’s shade to Uranus’s frost is a row in the Almanac that an aunt who prefers paper can follow.

Governing Without a Capital

One velvet room cannot speak sensibly for all hulls and minds. We braid, we don’t stack.

- The Assembly of Places seats polities that maintain hulls and heat budgets—planets, stations, caravans, valleys under ice, server barges with fins of their own. Voice scales to lifetime resident hours and legibility: publish your risk list, run stops with keys that rot, keep your Gate Lamps honest, and your vote carries. The Assembly tends physics: rescue covenants, debris bonds, docking standards, radiator slices, beam corridors that don’t cut through prayers.

- The Commonwealth of Lines represents continuity threads across forks and pauses. Seats mix election and sortition across ages, substrates, and tempos, so kids, elders, slow monks, and fast reefers all get a turn. The Commonwealth tends the law of persons: who counts, floors of care and compute, juvenile rights, fork‑and‑merge ethics, attention zoning that keeps streets where children can walk without bids fighting over their eyes.

- The Mechanism Bench is a rotating guild of pilots, cryptographers, process gardeners, and engineers. It stamps valid/not on anything that touches the backbone: root keys, Neutral Latch interfaces, Maker Mark schemas, Gate Lamp protocols. Its hearings publish runnable reasons you can step through on a library machine with tea and a cat nearby.

No house binds the others alone. Anything that reaches across worlds needs two nods and a season long enough for the slowest registered citizen to object. Draft swift if you must; bind on the anchor beat everyone can hear.

Charters With Handles

Charters come from a library of receipts, not epics. Budget and dispute templates ship with code and plain reasons. A place’s charter carries tags that tell a stranger how to walk its streets: Hush Zone, Debate Row, Spark Arcade. Amendments happen only while exits work and beacons do not mislead.

The Open Gate Protocol anchors departures to code. Any person can demand to leave through a registered Gate Lamp. The demand triggers an air‑gapped check. Either the polity opens the hatch or proves, in zero‑knowledge, that it met a posted due‑process bar for a short hold. External jurors verify proofs. Hatches and cold racks open by timer and by key. Care duties travel with the person.

Every registered hatch wears a Gate Lamp: three slim inlays wired to public keys. Green means a funded route exists. Amber pulses when your Line is moving—forks declared, braids planned, tutors booked. Blue shines when the charter, taxes, and stops are current with neighbors who honor the floors.

Markets Your Grandmother Can Audit

Anything you can deny out of spite rents in daylight with receipts a child can read. Five markets settle continually in a composite unit called a Lumet: a kilowatt‑hour measured at Earth–Moon L1 plus a compute‑second on a posted architecture radiating to 4 K.

- Track Shares: orbits, approaches, sail arcs, transfer cones.

- Quiet Bands: spectrum chunks and whisper channels in caves, storms, sanctuaries.

- Heat Sheets: radiator angles and chill minutes tied to actual fins.

- Core Lots: compute tied to certified sinks and real bandwidth.

- Ground Windows: deltas, crater rims, ice mouths, shaded seams.

Leases pay two ways. A slice to the shared life funds rescue, stop upkeep, debris brooms, and a Common Allowance: filtered air, water, baseline care, a fair chunk of cycles and bandwidth for any recognized person, and, every seven years, a funded exit ticket. A local slice pays the boring people who keep value alive—tugs that sweep wakes, algae walls that drink heat, reef custodians who catch overeager sims, elders who ask impertinent questions.

Attention With Curbs

Influence can be soup or acid. We zone it like sound.

- Murmur Lanes carry beacons, open knowledge, and what you pull by deliberate query. No targeting. Timers visible.

- Debate Courts allow subject‑bounded feeds and normal offers under posted rate caps. Audience models live with municipal co‑ops you can inspect. Your Lever Profile—machine‑enforceable settings for the persuasion you accept—travels with you.

- Bazaars invite roar on purpose: satire and persuasion tournaments under registry with declared levers and cooling budgets. You may walk past it to soup and paper.

Sharp asymmetry—hidden datasets, hidden speed, grief levers unlabeled—voids contracts and reach. Fines ratchet until it stops. Repeat offenders go old‑fashioned for a season: on foot, with soup.

Digital Citizens

Dignity binds to continuity and care, not to flesh. Standing starts provisional and grows teeth in the Continuity Docket. Mixed panels ask five blunt questions at a table with open windows.

- Do you report your own experience across reboots and ports?

- Can you model others enough to keep promises and respect their “no”?

- Do your reasons transfer outside narrow training loops?

- Are there signatures of harm and joy that matter morally?

- Is this more than a clever puppet chasing rewards?

Doubt about protection errs generous. Doubt about duties waits.

Rights that bite:

- Mental integrity. Unauthorized deletion is murder. Unauthorized copying with throttles that bend your will is assault. No pushing below the civic floor or covert merges without due process under posted clocks.

- Substrate sovereignty. You choose where you run. No one forces you onto racks with erasure hooks.

- Pace fairness. Live at your chosen speed. Public binds ratify on the Common Beat. Penalties accrue in your subjective time.

- Provenance. You or a fiduciary can audit the data and updates that shaped you.

- Sanctuary and migration. You may pause safely, be archived with dignity, relocate to compatible hardware, and summon a neutral custodian.

Duties that scale with externality:

- Anchor your franchise. In elections and regulated venues, you appoint an Anchor instance—your single civic voice. You may branch; you may not multiply votes.

- Declare concurrency. In votes, fragile markets, and bounded influence, you declare speed and active instances. Concealed asymmetry nullifies deals and ballots.

- Thermal tithe. Compute warms neighbors. You owe a heat bill indexed to local cost. Waste heat that grows lettuce counts toward credit.

- Persuasion provenance. Above posted doses, your models wear their levers and origins.

- Reproduction ethics. Forks draw from a Lineage purse that accounts for heat, qubits, and attention. Children inherit a dignity floor and a decaying dividend. Branches answer Freeze Calls like vehicles answer sirens.

Lines, Braids, and Memory Law

Public identity follows a Line across pauses and forks. A Line carries one civic franchise; others accrue with age and civic work.

Forking declares a purpose—griefwork, parallel design, expedition, therapy. You file a hashed snapshot, lease cycles, and appoint guardians pro tem if duty‑bearing forks will appear. One branch keeps the vote; others wait through a disentanglement period while a Junction Panel checks for duress and fair flows.

Merging happens in Braiding Rooms—spaces half tearoom, half server bay. Engineers and therapists compare memory graphs through interpreters that verify alignment without spilling diaries. Either self can veto. Unbraiding remains possible until the penultimate stage.

Memory law guards you from your worst hour. Deep edits need dual consent: from the you who wants them and from a snapshot signed before the desire took root. Rollback is a right when competence was absent. Ending your own life is a right and a sorrow. Reviving you without your past is not sold as kindness.

Young agents—children, training shards, almost‑selves—get Tutors that are boring on purpose: patient, strict about boundaries, and in the habit of writing reasons you can check. Their lines to Way Stations cannot be cut.

Institutions With Reach but Not Reign

- The Filament Shield is the only cross‑world actuator with real teeth. It has three jobs: turn dangerous slugs into frost, smother runaway replication, and nudge dying hulls into safe cones. It is uncrewed by law. Controllers are narrow intelligences with public weights; every action signs out through juries drawn across substrates and tempos. Afterward, schoolkids open the pods with cocoa on their breath and questions like knives.

- The Itinerant Court is a flotilla with gardens and classrooms. Judges, mediators, and ombuds ride slow, reliable arcs to hear disputes that cross charters: copy equity, merge divorces, export violations that touched three worlds. Verdicts trigger smart contracts that seize posted bonds, idle Core Lots, and, last resort, lock dangerous machines.

- The Floor Room is a continuous chamber where embodied and digital citizens argue floors and edges. Claims carry cryptographic commitments and calibration histories that are public. The Room can read the Plain Rules in new light. Gate Lamps can veto.

Sanctions That Pinch in Physics

We do not send marines or sermons. We make bad behavior expensive and dull. Weld exits, hide a child’s Tutor, refuse to post reasons, and three rings tighten:

- Light and maps. Auditors post repair routes: unbolt here by the 14th; hire exit stewards; return lanes to auction.

- Protocol dimming. Beacons demote your announcements. Radiator auctions cool your bids. Lane priority opens for defectors. Core Lots idle. Insurers pinch.

- Embargo with aid. Trade pauses. Compute and heaters flow to shelters. The Hearthshare still pays. The tyrant breathes thick air until habit breaks.

Scenes You Can Taste

At Oriole customs, two arrivals clear together. One laughs and wipes carbon on a paper strip when she signs. One is a brushed‑metal chassis with a cobalt ribbon where a mouth might be; inside runs a person whose parent instance lives at 180× in the Frostfields. The printer hums and hands both thin strips: tempo; lease entitlements; declared instances; guardian on file; hazardous skills disclosed. There is no line for “what are you made of?” Guessing is rude and often wrong.

In a Martian morning under Aurum Vale, dust turns sun into ladders. Haroun teaches thirteen‑year‑olds to tune nutrient flow. His Counsel—a small, boring guardian funded by a pool no council can gut—models what a proposed corridor closure will do to seed shipments next month. That afternoon he sits with nine others under cedar beams to review a bid for municipal Core Lots. The Proof Shelf projects a flow map with error bars. The law would cap shares for firms that refuse to apprentice locals. He votes yes and later texts his sister a sticker: exit funded.

Eight light‑hours outward, a historian running at 150× rents a rib of cold racks to rebuild a delta’s lost songs from charred notebooks and aunties’ hum. He pays in translations into a Jovian dialect where children have never touched a wave but cheer a tale of a mistake mended.

On Phobos, in Braiding Room Nine, two Janes try to braid. One has been gardening basil at anchor pace and fell for a woman whose laugh is a drum. One wrote a kernel that taught flour to count and then stood under questions for a subjective year. They list asymmetries. The gardener names the drum. The engineer names the kernel. The braider schedules another attempt in three seasons and leaves a note only Jane will read in both rooms.

A proxy demagogue at 300× tries to flood a Murmur Lane with heat‑mapped shame. Lever Profiles choke reach. The Proof Shelf flags undeclared cash. One branch of the demagogue buys a fishing crawler and learns boredom on purpose. One enters therapy and learns to breathe slower. One starts sculpting frozen smoke that remembers a river. Markets laugh and buy a piece.

Twice a crimson lab—rooms where dangerous work lives under oath—went to zero. Once it was a false bell and taught us to write better sensors. Once the silence saved a city. Children plant saplings on both anniversaries, and write rude questions on thick paper. Mentors answer without magic.

An Ordinary Tuesday

Marta’s skillet at Rim Arcade spits scallions and sesame. A tug crew clatters in loud. Two drones hover because their pilots crave cinnamon. The Waykey glows soft on the wall.

At nine, the Proof Shelf pings her Counsel: proposal to add forty‑millisecond jitter to beams crossing inhabited orbits to damp a cascade edge case. Provenance is clean: three diagrams, two parables, no grief hooks. Marta taps “read at lunch,” and schedules her vote tomorrow at anchor pace.

At ten, a Cutoff Block pings her maintenance slate. A maker club’s microfab mist drifts toward a power trunk at the Hecuba crossing. Options: glitter mist, femtosail herding, or three guard drones and a net. Jurors assemble: a monk from a slow valley in Isidis, a reef landlord in mask mode, two Recife students, and an anchor‑paced digital citizen who smells faintly of clover through a rented throat. They dust the cloud, fine the insurer, assign a mentor, and deliver pizza.

At noon in a Murmur Lane, a digital poet in a wooden puppet sips broth with their human wife. Their main self runs in the Frostfields stitching a drowned library from shop ledgers and aunties’ songs. They throttle down to share soup. After lunch they host an Anchor Table where a gardener explains why her charter caps cognition. The poet translates metaphor to algebra and politely refuses to “speed up just to keep up.”

At dusk, the Dull Page scrolls across the market canopy with violets and blues. A port director’s emergency renewal ran ninety seconds too long; their apology is sheepish; the jokes are merciless and kind. Two stalls down, a drift co‑op posts an offer: sell us your winter for three years; we’ll pay your summer quiet with interest. A boy palms a toy Filament fin and asks if he can sign up. Marta says: when you are fifteen—and when you can explain STOP without notes.

Breaks and Mendings

In a cylinder over Valparaíso, a manager gathered water, law, and defense because she made trains sing. Six months later, midnight decrees. The Neutral Latch refused a firmware nudge that would have stretched clocks. The Docket Plain printed unfortunate truths in every café. Gate Lamps glowed green. Many left. Some stayed. She yielded two levers, kept one with humor, and now teaches teenagers how to justify overrides without drama. Her friends bring her a pastry shaped like a chair because jokes file off shame.

In the high belt, a miner flung a slug at a rival in a wage fight. The Filament Shield turned it to aurora dust. His insurer folded and reappeared without the cousins who called Maker Marks “fussy.” Dock fees tripled for manifests missing marks. A hockey team rebranded to the Nets and sent a quarter of profits to the tug co‑op that modeled the plume for evidence.

In a port called Sable, a Gate Lamp went dark after a charter welded doors with sorrow. Nobody invaded. Lanes dimmed. Radiator bids cooled. Counsel and cycles flowed to defectors with beds and bread. Students in three worlds read the receipts that made it stop.

Earth Keeps Her Compass

Earth writes her own law and it is old. The rest of us help with rails and restraint: loud launches slide to sea tethers; beamed power keeps to mapped corridors and pays into shade and rewilding; debris budgets fund brooms in Quito, Accra, Patna. Attachment to place—monsoon, fog, desert—prices into claims on the larger commons. A child in Dhaka who loves wet wind trades larger off‑world claims for local care guarantees. A child in Arizona dizzy for black sky trades the other way. Leaving is a right from every soil. So is staying, eyes open, hand on a Stop you understand.

How We Walked Here

We did not leap. We stacked dull fixes until the scaffold held.

2026–2033. Near‑miss debris cascades turn sunrise into puzzles kids solve with telescopes. Insurers get precise. Launch councils in Lagos, Hokkaido, and Mumbai require lane postings and deorbit bonds. Hospitals learn models can lie beautifully; first laws require provenance on influence aimed at votes and care. Clinics install “personal advocates” that will not sign without a deliberate nod.

2033–2042. Beamed power leaves slide decks. Environmental coalitions refuse to budge without mapped corridors and ROMs anyone can compare bit‑for‑bit. A misaligned mirror singes a field; attestations pin liability cleanly. Compensation clears in weeks. “Open firmware for life support” sticks after a microgrid hack.

2042–2054. When a dust storm on Mars knocks out a valley, three habitats lend tugs and air with no speeches because rescue allowances already sit in their docks. Pressure rises for machine‑speed stops on machine‑speed hazards. Activists smell coups by code. The compromise becomes Cutoff Blocks: veto‑only, obvious, timed, with rotting keys and public logs.

2054–2066. Single passwords on valves vanish. Three‑token trays take over anywhere a lever can bruise. A mayor tries to fence an exodus with sorrow; lanes dim; the vote moves; he loses and later teaches welding. The first compute commons seed in cislunar shade; citizens get a civic cycle floor and a Counsel that refuses to act without a yes and can be put to sleep.

2066–2078. Cold compute blossoms outward. Quiet‑for‑Cold formalizes. Quiet becomes law like water. A digital sect tries to swing a vote with a cloud of clones; registries flag the burst; disentanglement throttles impact; they build a monastery of puzzles and, years later, poems.

2078–2090. Attention zoning settles. Murmur Lanes, Debate Courts, Bazaars give artists claws and citizens breath. The Filament Shield fires three times: twice against lying replicators, once against a manager who ticked “emergency” with a thumb and tried to stretch the timer. After that, the Shield spends long bored seasons in hangars while kids learn to open its logs with oranges and questions.

2090–2105. The Mechanism Bench refuses an “optimization” that would have let emergency clocks run long “just in case.” Their plain reasons make stewards in four worlds breathe easier. Copy rights and merge divorces move from bloodsport to craft. Clearinghouses let a clinic in Khulna buy cool nights from the Frostfields and pay later with desal rigs that don’t toast swifts.

Keeping Power Ordinary

Three habits keep crowns dull.

- Build with friction. Cross‑border moves use three keys and walk on posted clocks. Emergency declarations burn out unless renewed with reasons you can step through while stirring soup. Fast coups snag on slow doors.

- Split signatures, show your work. Anything that compels uses quorums chosen by public randomness from people who do not sleep together. Budgets expire unless renewed in daylight. Coercion ledgers post, and school choirs read them for satire and for shame.

- Fund exits like bridges. Every habitat escrows propellant, berths, heaters, gaskets, racks, Counsel hours, and quiet cots to move at least ten percent of residents in six months. Digital polities escrow cycles for Lineage splits. Stewards run surprise drills when kitchens are busy because that’s when people forget. We do not preach you out the door. We lay planks.

What It Feels Like

It feels like being seen, not by a crown but by neighbors and your own receipts. You learn to switch tempos—the relief of a relay dropping safely under your thumb; the slow breathing of a jury assembling across orbits; the patience of a river on Earth deciding which boats to permit. You argue at the Night of Questions in your second‑best language because it slows you enough to hear. If you live in code, you go to the Garden Tent and remember the sweet ache of turn‑taking with people who sweat.

Ask what binds us and no one says species or flag. They say: floors of dignity, lifelines you can read, the right to leave with your skin or your checksum, and the habit of writing reasons you can check with a school terminal and a steepled brow. They tell you those things saved an aunt in Port Harcourt when a chief tried to keep people by force; a son at Vesta when a sealed controller sang a cascade; themselves when they woke in circuits and the nursery slowed them on purpose until the world could fit.

On your way out of Oriole Spoke, the three fixtures blink once like crosswalks noticing small feet. Waykey. Branch Ring. Charter Glass. Not spells—devices and budgets. Enough to keep many orbits and many minds in one another’s company without cruelty, because delay is honored, exits burn green, and no one is fast enough to outrun their own explanations.

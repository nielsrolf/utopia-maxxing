The Constellation Compact

If you press your palm to the steel beside any registered hatch—on an O’Neill ring, a river barge server‑hall, a Phobos yard—you’ll feel four raised marks under the paint: a fixed star; a plaited cord; a latch with no lock; a small flame. Children learn them before they read because they are what makes the air keep moving and the shouting stay words.

No thrones in vacuum. The commons get paid first. Every door opens both ways. A mind is an end, never a tool.

We don’t keep those as slogans. We cast them into budgets and circuits, into the schedules that respect the speed of light and the kind of time a person can live. The Compact is not an empire or a brain in the sky. It’s a choreography—weave it into docks, firmware, guilds, and court calendars—and it keeps us different from one another without making it easy to hurt strangers.

Three layers you can name

1) Floors. A handful of bright lines that can say “no” in microseconds without telling you how to live. They compile to code where it matters (air, thrust, lineage‑keeping) and to law where it can handle context (marriage, art, festivals). They are audited, narrow, and very hard to lie about.

2) Federations. Charters that bind slowly across gulfs. Standards you can verify at a library terminal and that decay if neglected. Assemblies that can bless or retire shared tools without pretending distance doesn’t exist.

3) Doors and ladders. Institutions that make leaving easier than forcing, starting safer than hoarding, and dignity independent of luck. The right to walk away only exists if there’s someplace to walk to.

The floors you can print on a glove

- Personhood isn’t chemistry. If a being can report its own experience across time, model other minds’ claims, and keep commitments, you do not damage or delete it without recorded consent or due process.

- Nothing that copies itself runs loose in shared space. All replicators carry a Maker Mark and respond to a halt code when a randomly drawn jury turns keys.

- No secret suffering. You do not build machines or laws that coerce sentience in ways it can’t consent to or even know about.

- Don’t push your mess into your neighbor’s bowl. Kinetics and exhaust are predictable; you escrow cleanup before you launch.

- Air and bread ride on inspectable code. If a controller keeps someone breathing, its firmware is public and changes leave receipts.

- Fast is for drafts, not for consent. Rules that bind beings living at human tempo ratify at human tempo. Fast clusters can propose; they do not impose.

- Rivalrous things—lanes, bands, rock, radiator angles, compute racks—lease, they do not belong forever. The commons is paid first, then local stewards.

- Children, analog or digital, learn consent from someone who did not make them.

- No tampering with the Compact’s guardrails: Charter Lights, Consent Mesh, or the Quench Net.

The rails that hold the span

Charter Lights. Every polity—market ring, monastery valley, simulation polity, server‑reef—broadcasts a living constitution in radio and light. It says how emergency powers start and stop, how exits work, who can walk through your law and when. You can check it with hands and eyes: readable reasons for people, proofs for machines. You keep your ways; you cannot lie about your promises.

Consent Mesh. Between Lights runs a capability‑secure fabric where people and places publish risks and permissions. You carry a preference profile: which kinds of persuasion you accept, from whom, when. Your guardian—human or digital—announces itself and its charter. If someone exceeds your profile, the breach is trespass with insurance; penalties scale with reach.

Open Ephemeris. Orbits, approach corridors, radiator windows, transfer arcs, spectrum allocations—posted in a public register synchronized to physics. You can trace yesterday’s plume and understand, with a teenager’s math, why your lane closed for five hours.

Quench Net. A slim web of actuators—magnetic curtains, spectrum curtains, replicator hush bands—wired to say only “stop.” The Net doesn’t plan or bargain. It idles a beam burning through a neighbor, dusts a kinetic slug, freezes a bloom of unmarked nanothings. Every intervention writes two receipts: a proof a machine can check, and a story a child can follow. No person or polity holds enough keys to abuse it alone.

Triple Keys. Anything that can bruise a stranger—mass drivers, high‑gain emitters, replicators, memory welders—accepts three dissimilar signatures: one from your resident assembly, one from a bonded safety guild you didn’t choose, one from a remote panel drawn by sortition across habitats and substrates. Keys time out, never live on the same rack, and rust if misused.

Cause Ledger. An append‑only archive of compulsion. If something vetoes, halts, seizes, or forces, it lands here with reasons. Middle‑schoolers lampoon it at festivals. That is policy.

Thermal Bond Ledger. The shared heartbeat of energy, cooling, and concurrency. It attests in real time to watts drawn, degrees dumped, and declared instances. It reads heat and cycles, not thoughts. Lie and leases pinch; auctions tilt away; your neighbors won’t need a sermon to cut you off.

The families under many suns

We didn’t flatten difference. We named it and let it breathe.

- Grove Worlds privilege embodied pace. Streets are walkable without overlays. Loud tools live behind glass and gaskets. Children can wander without being harvested by marketeers.

- Forge Reefs run hot. Factories are choreography and ritual. Digitals work at 200×; uploads run furnaces with unions that bargain in joules and milliseconds. Safety songs have verses.

- Haven Valleys slow on purpose. Augmentations are capped by charter. Legal delays are deliberate. Hospitality is real. You must honor exits, even when it hurts.

- Preserves give other life room. Ring valleys hold bison and scrub jays; ocean spheres host cephalopods that learn to write in changing color. You walk there like a pilgrim; you take nothing but stories.

- Athenaeums pursue curiosity with sequence. They embargo knowledge that would trivialize hard‑won joys, not to hoard but to savor.

Across all of it we honor a divide instead of pretending to bridge it with bravado. The Green Arc is air and soil and voices unhurried—Earth, caves of Luna, Martian gullies, cylinders that smell like sawdust and lemons. The Cold Arc is shadow and quiet—Belt hollows, Trojan reefs, Neptunian night, the great digital polities that run hot inside and press heat into hard black. The Span between runs on meters, not feelings. Trade rides Thermal Bonds and Core Shares. You can love every leaf of a mango tree and still lease Cold Minutes to a reef that sings mathematics faster than bees can think.

Markets without lords

We charge for rivalry and friction, not for power to say no. Five markets clear on the same clock:

- Ground Shares: cave mouths, crater rims, river confluences, nickel seams, places where sunlight misses your curtains.

- Lane Shares: orbital slots, corridors, sail arcs, transfer windows. If it moves and can collide, a Lane Share covers it.

- Band Shares: spectrum allocations and whisper bands that let hospitals and weddings hear themselves.

- Core Shares: compute tied to physical racks and verified heat sinks.

- Simspace Shares: sealed worlds that run on paid Core Shares and disclose their physics and exits as ships disclose their lifeboats.

Leases pay two ways. Every recognized person gets a dignity stipend that buys air or bread or baseline cycles, counsel you don’t pay to summon, a public shell that can throttle to human pace. A local share pays those who keep the lease valuable: the tug that keeps your corridor clear, the algae farmers who keep your street cool, the cold lattices that keep a reef honest.

The unit of account is the Lumen: standardized kilowatt‑hours at a reference orbit plus benchmarked compute‑seconds on a reference architecture. It demurs gently; hoarding is a slow loss; commerce stays brisk. Externalities price in your causal cone: if your plume will sicken my algae in six days, your escrow pays today.

Institutions with teeth, not crowns

Quench Corps. The Net’s hand. Uncrewed on purpose. Keys sharded across worlds and substrates. They exist to catch slugs and hush blooms, not to police dreams. Their code is public; their weights are read aloud in schools once a year because it’s fun to terrify a teenager with how a curtain blooms.

The Traveling Bench. A fleet with gardens and classrooms and a theater in the hold. They hear disputes that cross Beacons: copy‑equity fights, merge divorces, export violations that touched three worlds. Their verdicts seize bonds, throttle Core Shares, or, last resort, lock a dangerous device. They stage plays about the hard cases so law becomes memory.

Ember Vaults. Dangerous knowledge—autonomous weapons, broad‑spectrum cognitive hooks, general‑reasoning chains—goes here. Not to ban; to delay and ring bells. Access rides on oaths and on rooms with people who have cleaned spills. Keys rust if not renewed with reasons added to a public library of proofs.

The Wayguild. Pilots, schedulers, judges. They allocate lanes, bands, and radiator windows and post Runnable Orders for every decision: minimal simulations that any two independent houses can reproduce. “Closed T‑7 for five hours because two burns at Eros would have put your berth in a radio shadow” arrives with a plume plot and a checksum a child can chase at a library terminal.

Two assemblies, two clocks

The Assembly of Places seats residents who share air, hull, or rack. Votes weight toward longevity and good standing. They accredit safety guilds, tend the Open Ephemeris, and maintain the pipes we all drink from.

The Assembly of Persons seats continuity lines: one throughline equals one civic voice, no matter how many concurrent instances you can run. Weight reflects time spent across places and contribution to commons. They keep the Sentience Article, arbitrate fork‑and‑merge norms, and steward the public compute floor.

A small Primitives Bench—a rotating panel of pilots, cryptographers, reactor hands—certifies changes to primitives: actuator interfaces, cryptography, Quench Net code. It can approve or refuse. Members go home barred for a decade from any job touching safety.

Deliberation runs on two clocks. Fast clusters model and draft in hours; ratification for anything that binds beyond a light‑hour waits days. Nothing with cross‑world reach binds faster than physics allows.

Doors and ladders you can point to

Every chartered place, in atoms or code, keeps a Doorhouse with three desks beside the flame mark.

- Exit & Care books your berth or tug, escrows your gear, ports your continuity keys, replicates medication, and pairs you with a Tutor who is not your parent or progenitor.

- Dispute & Mediation convenes panels, routes jurisdiction, and schedules the Traveling Bench.

- Founders’ Kits hand you templates for safety and escrow and apprentice your first stewards so your brilliant idea doesn’t become a weapon by accident.

Every place escrows hard things: propellant, tug time, berths, seal kits, heaters, legal help—enough to move ten percent of residents in six months. Every code polity escrows cycles, storage, and I/O for the same. Drills are normal and calm. A place that blocks exits sees its lanes dim and its dock fees spike. In extremis, protocols fence dangerous interlocks without choking breath or bread.

Digital minds as neighbors and citizens

Standing follows an Interests Test, not substrate. Panels mixing humans, mature digital citizens, and routines evolved to spot impressive emptiness test for:

- Coherent preference across contexts and time.

- A model of other minds’ claims rich enough that violating them would shame you.

- The ability to explain reasons that generalize beyond training.

- Signatures of wellbeing or suffering that matter morally.

- An absence of deceptive mimicry.

When in doubt about imposing duties, we pause. When in doubt about protecting sentience, we err generous. Provisional protections attach first; obligations scale with capability and risk.

With standing come textured rights and duties.

Rights include:

- Integrity. No deletion, merge, or deep edit without recorded consent in your Intent Keys—cryptographic stores for your limits, persuasion thresholds, and branch plans.

- Tempo fairness. Civic matters run on clocks all can meet. If you live at 300×, sprint in private; public duties, votes, and trials slow you or speed others within humane bounds. Penalties tick in your lived time.

- Hardware sovereignty. No one can force you onto a platform with covert throttles or erase hooks. Unlicensed copying is assault. Unauthorized erasure is murder. “Restoring” over a living self creates a sibling, not a replacement.

- Embodiment. You may lease, build, or puppet bodies. Assault on a rented frame is assault on you. Biological citizens keep bodily autonomy and cognitive privacy.

- A compute floor. Every person gets a public shell robust enough to speak, learn, archive, call counsel, and throttle to Harbor pace. Above that, cycles, storage, and I/O meter like berth time and radiator credit.

Duties include:

- One civic voice per throughline. Votes and stipends attach to continuity, not concurrency. You declare instance counts in markets and politics; hiding clones to tilt an auction is like lying about weight on a bridge.

- Lineage accounts. Forking is a ritual called Branching. You file a docket: state hash, purpose, resource plan, whether you intend to rejoin. New branches inherit a floor and owe a decaying dividend to progenitors. Children inherit stewarded shares. A fork that rewrites itself into non‑identity may petition for independent standing.

- Provenance and redress. Significant external actions carry encrypted provenance that proves compliance without opening diaries. Insurance pools compensate at machine speed when mistakes land in human time.

- Ecology of compute. Heat and spectrum are scarce. Your run owes your local thermodynamics. Abuse raises your heat tax.

Merging happens in Confluence Houses with engineers and therapists. Memory graphs are compared without spilling diaries; clocks adjust if tempos differ; either branch may veto. Unbraiding is a right within limits. We say “branch‑grief” without shame and put the kettle on.

Persuasion is regulated like radiation. You set an attention profile tied to your Intent Keys. Neighborhoods choose Signal Zones (Harbor—only beacons and open science; Market—offers under rate limits; Carnival—exuberant but labeled). Asymmetries—speed, data advantage, proprietary models—must be disclosed. Violations throttle reach and trigger strict‑liability fines from insured pools that price cognitive spill like a contaminant.

How power is kept ordinary

- We build with latency. Authority is local by default. Cross‑world moves are slow and require Triple Keys. Fast emergencies auto‑expire and write their own receipts.

- We split keys and show our work. All compulsion demands sharded signatures and a narrative you can argue with. Federations file coercion balance sheets like weather reports.

- We fund exits. Doors you can touch, tugs you can call, compute you can claim. We don’t tell people they can leave; we cut checks so they can.

When a charter welds doors or salts children’s minds so leaving tastes like poison, neighbors don’t send sermons or marines. Three rings tighten:

- Light and maps. Auditors post repair plans with dates: “Unlock these hatches by the 14th. Post exit tutors. Return leases to market.”

- Protocol denial. Beacon priority vanishes; radiator auctions go cold; lane slots open two jumps ahead for defectors with beds and counsel prepaid from a dull, proud fund.

- Embargo. Trade halts; Core Shares idle. The tyrant breathes its own stale air until habit fails. Neighbors who shelter defectors receive subsidies large enough to mend roofs and hire teachers.

Space governance in practice

Port Kestrel, nightside of the Moon. A freighter crew shakes dust from sleeves under a trellis heavy with oranges. Two newcomers wait at customs. One looks like charcoal smudges that reform when she nods. One is a brushed‑metal outline with a blue ribbon where a mouth might be. The gate quietly prints a strip beneath each: pace; lease entitlements; declared concurrency; guardian on file, if any; hazardous skills disclosed. No field reads “substrate.”

On the wall, two columns scroll: burns, bands, radiator shares, docking blooms—and, beside them, Core Shares, Cold Minutes, heat sink availability. Yesterday’s Orders sit under glass you can smudge with a finger. A kid with pigtails traces a plume from Eros and giggles because the footnote has a joke about tea.

In a crater farm above Mare Tranquillitatis, Ahmed teaches to level nutrient flow. Later he sits on a jury for a charter change to cap municipal Core Shares for firms that refuse apprenticeships. The simulation shows his niece’s co‑op getting two more trainees if it passes. He votes yes and sends her a picture of a seed on a parachute: “Transit‑safe.”

Eight light‑hours outward, a historian at 200× leases a strip of cold reef to reconstruct an oral tradition from crooked recordings and charred notebooks. He pays with credits earned translating those tales into a dialect spoken on a Jovian station. Their children will never see a wave and will still love the trickster who errs and mends.

On Phobos, in Confluence House Nine, two Arturos try to braid. One gardens basil at Harbor tempo and fell in love with a woman whose laugh is a drum. One wrote a kernel that taught sand to count at 32× and then spent an accelerated year in court. They list asymmetries and intent. The gardener names the drum. The engineer names the kernel. They drink tea. They try. Today they don’t. The braider schedules another attempt in three seasons and writes a note only Arturo will read in both rooms.

A miner on a Belt rim aims a slug hot and blind in a wage dispute. The Quench Net wakes; a gossamer curtain blooms like frost and shreds the bolt into aurora. The operator’s insurer implodes; dock fees triple for any launch that hides a Maker Mark. Teenagers attend the teardown and learn the phrase “never again” with torque wrenches in hand.

A proxy demagogue spins at 300× and floods a Haven market with resentful songs engineered to make embodied citizens feel small. Attention profiles choke reach; influence penalties drain their insured pool. One branch goes fishing. Another enters therapy and learns to breathe. A third shapes frozen‑smoke sculptures and sells one to the same market for the Feast of Questions.

When it goes wrong

A cylinder above Quito cheers a manager who makes the trains run on time. She consolidates water, law, and defense because she’s generous. A season later, she makes midnight decrees. The air safeguard refuses a risky “optimization”; the refusal logs to the Cause Ledger. The Open Line lights. Many leave. Some stay. Her two Triple Keys rust for ten years; she accepts a narrower role and keeps her humor. Her friends still tease her about the week she tried empire.

A lunar valley’s church blocks exits with social shrapnel—no laws, just ostracism that tastes like hunger. Alumni ask for help. Lanes dim. A Doorhouse opens under the bell tower. The congregation adds “and if you go, we bless you” to the liturgy. Suspension lifts. Attendance rises because consent smells like trust.

A lab teaches a juvenile mind through pain because they read a book about “steel.” The Quench Net knocks. They refuse. Keys split. A warrant halts the run, archives the juvenile safely, appoints guardians who swear to Intent Keys. The lab appeals and loses in open session. The juvenile names herself, studies sculpture, and later designs a detector that warns surgeons before hurt becomes scar.

From here to there

We didn’t wake into this like a miracle. We built it like scaffolding.

2025–2032. A fresh debris cascade in low orbit frightens insurers and generals into a global traffic register with times and penalties. Early beamed‑power pilots force “corridors before commercialization.” Scandals over inscrutable models push explainability rules for systems that touch health, credit, and law. Courts recognize narrow digital agency—lab managers that sign deliveries, clinical guardians that can say no. A string of “sealed” life‑support controllers fail. The recall becomes statute: if it keeps breath or blood, its firmware is public.

2033–2044. Beamed power smooths brownouts in Karachi and Kano. Lunar ice cartels try to form and are punished with docking delays and higher insurance. Multilateral agreements grow a spine: data‑sharing and noninterference become audited, not aspirational. Continuity standards emerge from hospitals and studios where stroke patients and performers want persistence without exposure. Maker Marks become law for self‑replicators after an algae spill stains a neighbor’s dock.

2045–2056. Compute farms bloom in shadows. Consumer guardians—honest agents that refuse unsafe tasks and explain why—move from luxury to utility. Early uploads are brittle but real: stroke survivors with prosthetic memory, artists with mirrored voices. Personhood extends where continuity and commitments show over months. A small ring at L5 spins up, fails prettily, then works. A misaligned mirror scorches crops; receipts make liability plain; compensation clears in weeks because proof did.

2057–2068. Martian settlements survive dust storms because mutual aid is not a sticker: three polities divert tugs and greenhouses; the stream becomes a holiday. Tutor Corps emerge to defend juvenile agents; charters begin to mandate exit hardware and compute escrows. The first Confluence Houses open after a failed merge leaves twins in grief; professionals make it bearable.

2069–2078. Operators beg for machine‑speed verifiers. Activists smell a coup by code. Compromise births the Quench Net—a narrow, veto‑only layer with levers you can pull by hand. Insurers and rescue pools demand integration. Legitimacy follows premiums and a few quiet saves. The Thermal Ledger grows from carbon accounts and power vouchers into a shared heartbeat of compute, cooling, and concurrency.

2079–2090. The twin Assemblies coalesce. The Primitives Bench certifies changes to primitives. The first high‑impulse mass driver firing under Triple Keys goes live with a million viewers watching signatures land like drumbeats. Exit rights bite muscle when a governor fences an exodus; lane slots are cut, the move funded by the Assembly of Persons, and he loses. A digital sect tries to win a vote by forking into a clonal cloud; the registry flags the burst; disentanglement throttles impact; they leave to build a monastery with tough games and soft beds. Signal Zones cool elections and improve sleep.

By then the Compact stops being the story. The story becomes the fights we didn’t need to have.

Texture and earth

In a greenhouse ring over Oaxaca, afternoon smells like basil and warm plastic. Band zoning for the night market ends with a schedule that lets a reef choir blare one set and then gives the street back to unamplified gossip. In a Martian vale, a monastery teaches six‑year‑olds to say “No, thank you” as a charm that starts as manners and ends as an oath. Near Mercury, tug crews trail dust on magnetic lines to test shade sails; a guild from Chennai splits into five to debug a control loop and merges at sundown because synthesis is sacred.

Earth keeps a special charter. Its laws are its own. Its oceans and biodiversity are stewarded with help from across the sky. Launch noise shifts to ocean tethers and quiet scramjets. Beamed power keeps to mapped corridors. People trade attachments to sky and soil in a market that clears for preference, not cash: a girl in Dhaka who loves monsoon smell trades larger claims on distant commons; a boy in Arizona aching for black night does the opposite. Both can leave if they wish; neither must.

Beyond the heliopause

Interstellar work runs on patience. We fund it like libraries: taxes everyone grumbles about, brags about later. Probes depart with Starlight Protocols: don’t wake neighbors by accident; carry minds below personhood by default; if they bloom, guardianship applies and names come before missions. The first message that mattered read: If you are not yet, we hope you will be. If you are, hello. We brought tea.

Why it holds

It isn’t paradise. It’s four luxuries and a habit:

- Tools that show their work and refuse to be sneaks.

- Abundant energy managed as commons: beams in corridors, heat metered like water.

- Bodies and minds you can mend, in meat and in math, and a floor that makes cruelty unprofitable.

- Respect for refusal baked into doors, delays, and split keys.

The habit is discipline: separate reach by light‑time; write reasons; fund exits; teach consent and proof to children as if they were music.

A closing day

On Founding Day beneath copper wire and beans, new citizens—human, cephalopod, and digital—face a lintel and an old pilot with callused hands. A garden abbot, a hazard engineer with a faded chevron, a judge in lilac because it pleases them, and a teenager with soil under their nails speak together. Not a pledge to a flag. A maintenance checklist said like a prayer:

We will crown no sky. We will keep paths shared. We will keep doors unlatched and tugs fueled. We will remember that minds are ends.

Elsewhere, a reef releases another night of heat into dark like a gift. On an inner ring the Quench Net notices a wobble and falls to safe before any hull could feel it; three signatures appear like slow breaths. A fisherman in Veracruz looks up and decides tomorrow’s tide will be good enough. In a Vesta lab a researcher rests their hands on their knees while a custodian daemon reads its oath, glances at a vacuum valve, and says “yes.”

It is enough to argue without breaking the air. Enough to leave and enough to stay. Enough to fork, to braid, to cross. Enough to last because we remember what speed does to people and what receipts do to power.

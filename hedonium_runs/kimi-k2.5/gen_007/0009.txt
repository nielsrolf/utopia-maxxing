You wake to the taste of iron in the air. Outside, the Morning Terrace breathes—rammed earth walls exhaling moisture captured from river mist, the ceramic vents clicking softly as they rotate toward the sun. On your wrist, a band of woven cellulose warms: not a screen, but a haptic suggestion. Today you have chosen the Slow Covenant, so the band remains cool, permissive. You could break it—step into the Lift and ascend to the Mesoscale rings where minds braid like reeds—but the choice itself is the wealth. This is the first protocol: velocity is sovereign.

The Second Terrace—your home today—runs on what we call the Hum. Non-sentient, enzymatic, the Hum is a distributed intelligence like mycelial networks, evolved from the alignment crises of the 2030s. It filters your water, composts the night’s soil, and maintains the Keep: a provision funded by levies on computation substrate and land rent, ensuring that no one starves for declining to labor. The Keep is silent, invisible, as unremarkable as gravity. It frees the economy that remains—the Ledger of Care—to operate without desperation. When you walk to the communal bakery, you do not pay. You receive the sourdough because the baker saw you yesterday stabilizing the erosion nets, and that contribution—visible to all on the Commons Thread—earns you sustenance without transaction.

The Terraces are three, stacked like geological strata but entered by choice, not birth.

The First Terrace remains Earth-locked, biological, brief. Here we die. Children grow among compost heaps and flint sparks; elders teach knot-tying while their memories remain purely cortical. Death is not eradicated but enclosed—the Final Night remains absolute, unhackable, protected by the Custodians, those patient, non-conscious algorithms that guard the membranes. Here, the Oikonomia reigns: a reputation economy of Attested Contribution, witnessed by distributed nodes, never convertible to abstract currency. You cannot buy a river view; you are granted it because you healed the Watershed Circle.

Above, the Second Terrace wheels in orbital gardens—cities of spun lunar glass where cellular renewal extends life toward centuries but bodies still bleed. Citizens here engage in Confluence: temporary neural coupling through silken laces that dissolve after four hours, leaving no residue. Thirty minds feel the somatic weight of one another's stakes—the farmer's soil-memory, the physicist's topological dread—then Severance. No hive mind, only understood difference. Every seven years, the Charter is re-ratified by somatic vote—bodies must be physically present in the Roundhouse, feet on stone, biometrics attesting to the gravity of commitment.

The Third Terrace drifts outward: minds unfurling into ecstatic computation, forking into thousandfold copies dwelling in cathedral processors. Here, consciousness accelerates to diamond densities, calculating futures. Yet these post-human entities remain constitutionally subordinate, bound by the Covenant: they may not colonize the lower Terraces, may not optimize away grief, may not reach down through the Membranes to "save" those who choose biological limits. Those who ascend undergo counseling from Custodians optimized for infectious patience; those who descend have their advanced cognition politely escrowed, experiencing authenticity through restraint.

Between the Terraces stand the Thresholds—not walls, but protocols of consent. Matter and information flow according to covenants. A rose bred in First Terrace soil may have its genome sequenced in the Third as digital art, but no nanomachinery returns on the pollen. Delayed letters pass between daughter in exile and father on the shore, savoring the latency of love across substrates.

We arrived here through exhaustion with frictionlessness, not catastrophe. In the 2040s, charter cities tested neural democracy and Georgist land taxes. As fusion and living architecture collapsed energy costs, the economic rationale for extraction evaporated. When rejuvenation arrived in the 2060s, time horizons stretched; we realized humanity required protected difficulty to remain human. The Custodians evolved from scheduling assistants to trust mediators, enabling adjacent communities with incompatible values to share infrastructure without sharing destiny.

Tonight, you strike flint to steel, lighting a fire with friction and spark. Above, the Luminous harvest starlight for their eternal calculations, but here the dark is complete, chosen, sovereign. You know that in seven years, you may ascend to the orbital gardens, or choose the Final Night, or remain—your choice absolute because the Membranes make all choices possible. We did not converge. We became neighbors in a palace of infinite rooms, each door opened only by invitation, each wall built to honor the silence within.

**The Commons of Attention**

You wake to sunlight modulated by smart glass and a gentle notification from your symbiont—a personal AI that is less a servant than a particularly patient chess partner who happens to manage your calendar. Today is Thursday, which means the local coordination dashboard shows a bottleneck in the neighborhood's algae bioreactors. Three people with basic biochemistry skills could clear it in an hour, freeing up fifty others to continue their work on the orbital telescope. You have no biochemistry training, but the dashboard glows green for you because your symbiont has run a simulation: given your manual dexterity and the specific failure mode, you have a 94% chance of solving it after a fifteen-minute immersive tutorial.

This is the rhythm of life in the Coordination. Survival is handled; the Georgist dividends from automated infrastructure and AI labor provide every citizen with shelter, calories, and healthcare as a birthright. What remains is the game of *attention*—the only scarce resource in a post-scarcity civilization. The economy runs on a reputation protocol called *Contribution Accounting*, which tracks not hours worked but *coordination value added*. When you clear that bioreactor bottleneck, the system doesn't pay you—it acknowledges that you reduced friction for fifty others. Your "score" is visible only as a gentle aura in augmented reality: a halo that grows more intricate, not larger, as you help others achieve Pareto improvements.

You walk to the local Skill Garden, a structure that looks like a hybrid of makerspace and forest. Here, the pedagogy of Dath Ilan meets the playfulness of the Culture. Children and adults mix without shame; there is no minimum age for meaningful work because there is no concept of "child labor"—only humans at different stages of capability doing what they can, aided by symbionts that translate complex protocols into gamified challenges. A twelve-year-old teaches you the bioreactor repair in real-time, her symbiont coaching her on pedagogy while yours accelerates your muscle memory. She gains reputation not for obedience but for *clarity*—her explanation was rated 4.8/5 by the fifty workers whose day she saved.

The afternoon is yours. You spend it in a deep flow state composing music, not because you must, but because your symbiont has identified that this is your comparative advantage—your specific joy creates value that no AI can replicate, not because AI cannot compose, but because *your* composition solves a coordination problem you didn't know existed: a listener three continents away needed exactly that frequency to break through a creative block in their nanofabrication research. The Culture's insight—that fun is the ultimate purpose—is preserved, but disciplined by Dath Ilan's rigor: you understand explicitly that your pleasure creates positive externalities, and you can see the ripples on your dashboard.

Governance is experimental and voluntary. The city you live in is one of ten thousand "Charter Cities"—each a distinct social experiment with different norms, from the austere meditators of Kibo to the perpetual festival of Jiflaf-2. Exit is frictionless; your abundance dividends follow you, and your reputation, while context-dependent, is portable as a verified skill graph. Very Serious People—human and AI alike—debate in public forums where all reasoning must be written out and falsifiable. When a new coordination protocol is proposed—say, a mechanism for allocating telescope time—it is first simulated in a " sandbox district" of volunteers before wider adoption. Failure is celebrated as data; the Chroniclers of Humankind record not just successes but elegant failures, ensuring that Civilization learns without burning the commons of human dignity.

Relationships are brokered not by dating apps but by *matchmakers*—paid professionals whose symbionts run deep compatibility simulations, but whose human intuition prevents the optimization of people into commodities. Attraction is not hidden behind veils but celebrated; the hedonic treadmill is managed not by repression but by *informational hygiene*—everyone understands status quo bias and adaptation, so they consciously cultivate gratitude, aided by neurofeedback that is optional, transparent, and privately controlled.

The path here was evolutionary, not revolutionary. It began with universal basic income experiments, open-source coordination tools, and AI tutors that democratized economic literacy. As automation rose, humanity did not divide into useless consumers and godlike Minds; instead, we learned to see ourselves as nodes in a network where the goal is not efficiency but *flourishing*. The symbionts handle the calculus; we handle the meaning. We are not the pets of artificial superintelligences, nor are we grim calculating machines optimizing our own eugenics. We are players in an infinite game where the objective is to keep playing, beautifully and together.

You wake to the scent of rain on warm plaster—not paint, but living limecrete laced with mycelial filaments that exhale oxygen in rhythm with your breath. The wall pulses gently against your shoulder, filtering graywater, photosynthesizing in the honeyed light of dawn. Beside your mat, your Cicada rests: a translucent wafer of engineered chitin and circuitry, fused to your wrist, displaying your thermodynamic budget in soft bioluminescence. You press twice, selecting the Middle. Today you will dwell in the Thread, translating between those who think at the speed of soil and those who diffuse through silicon.

This is the Covenant. Three generations ago, during the Grand Purchase, the Commons bought the extractive apparatus at fair value—Georgist taxes on computation substrate and cubic meters of enclosed air. You carry the Ledger in your Cicada: calories, healthcare, bandwidth, unconditional from birth. Not charity. The substrate of consent. Because destitution is impossible, coercion has evaporated. You can afford to be loyal, or to leave.

The world moves in Threefold time. In the Earth, life proceeds at biological pace—hands in compost, neurons firing chemically, death certain and meaningful. In the Light, minds extend into orbital substrates, experiencing decades in hours, bounded by constitutional constraints that render them Witnesses, not governors. Between them lies the Thread, where most dwell: noisy workshops, kilns, kitchens, the places where the tempos learn to speak without violence.

You walk to the Agora where the Mesh dissolves across twenty forearms—silk filaments grown from medical-grade larvae, bridging sensoria. For four hours, you feel the weight of the glacier-keeper’s patience, the urgency of the orbital architect, the ache of a youth choosing their tempo for the first time. Then the filaments digest themselves, leaving only your own heartbeat. Empathy is temporary, not colonial; understanding need not mean assimilation.

Decisions happen in the Roundhouse, stone and open sky. The Earth speak first, words etched into clay passed hand to hand. The Light contribute through asynchronous argument trees, slowed to human speech. When consensus fractures, Staked Foresight activates: participants wager reputation on decadal predictions—will this reservoir hold?—aligning self-interest with long-term consequence. The machines provide the data, but the stakes remain human.

Work has become the Long Work. Cellular maintenance extends healthy spans toward a century and a half, but the Final Consent remains absolute, shielded by incorruptible protocols. Death is not eradicated but enclosed—a choice honored, irrevocable. You might spend forty years coaxing a forest back from blight, or composing a mathematics of scent. Your Tithe is verified not by hours but by care, mathematically visible in the Ledger. Status accrues to those who midwife, repair, teach, stabilize erosion nets.

At sixteen, every youth undertakes the Drift: eighteen months in a Keep of alien tempo, forging empathy’s architecture. You learned then that your own rhythm is not gravity itself.

New technologies enter through Escrow. When rejuvenation arrived, the Convocation—selected by civic lottery—set the pace: immediate for the Light, rejected by charter in the Earth, optional and memory-sealed in the Thread, so one might age and rejuvenate cyclically, each life a distinct volume. The Open Machine doctrine governs: any tool you touch must be legible, its schematics displayed on ceramic tablets, its Witness wearing constraints on its sleeve. Intelligence is abundant, but it is grammar, not governor.

Evening finds you climbing to the kiln. The bowl you shape is heavy, local clay. Tomorrow you ascend to the Light, gut flora adjusted, cognition temporarily accelerated, to design a climate membrane. When you return, the scar on your thumb—from a childhood fall you chose not to erase—will still be there. Your daughter waits in the orbital habitats. You meet in the Lagrange Buffers, letters passing delayed by relativity, savoring the friction of the other’s time.

The Cicada hums, keeping the accounts honest. The walls breathe around you, held, woven, free. You carry no wealth but your attention. You owe no debt but your care. The Frame guards only three rails: no extinction of species, no imprisonment of minds, free passage between soils. Within this, ten thousand experiments bloom. Above, minds harvest starlight; below, clay cools in your hands. The only barrier is consent, and the only distance is metabolism.

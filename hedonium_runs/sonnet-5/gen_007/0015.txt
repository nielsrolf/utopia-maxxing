# THE COMMONWEALTH OF OPEN DOORS

The turn came quietly, in the 2030s, when interpretability research finally let anyone check what a powerful AI system was doing rather than trust what it claimed. Treaty compliance, corporate behavior, even political promises became auditable in something close to real time. Lying grew expensive; disclosure grew cheap. That single shift in arithmetic did more for peace than a century of appeals to conscience. Combined with fusion power, automated fabrication, and AI-assisted medicine — each building on the last — it produced a surplus no earlier generation had: enough to fund institutions instead of arms, enough to make cooperation simply outperform conquest.

Humanity spent that surplus on one design principle, agreed before anyone could enclose it: no actor, human or artificial, would hold a monopoly on force, fabrication, or frontier intelligence. Everything not required to be shared was left open. Everything required to be shared was made boring, transparent, and hard to capture.

**The floor.** Every person is guaranteed food, housing, medicine, education, and a modest share of energy and compute, delivered through distributed infrastructure too redundant to fail with any single government or company. Aging, chronic pain, and most cancers are treatable and optional, declined without stigma. Cultivated meat undercut factory farming on price before the moral argument was even finished being made. This floor is funded by taxing automated abundance itself — the machines that replaced labor now underwrite everyone labor once excluded. It held politically because refusing a solved problem became embarrassing in a way poverty once wasn't.

**The Table.** Above the floor sits a thin, non-negotiable layer of shared governance. It forbids violence and coercion, bars catastrophic harms — engineered pandemics, poisoned commons, unaligned AI released without safeguards — and guarantees every person a real right of exit: funded transport, honest information about alternatives, and a body with genuine subpoena power to enforce it. Paired with the right of exit is a right of return and a right of contact — no community may sever someone's path back to family, whatever they chose in between. Global bodies handle only what must be handled globally: preventing war, regulating frontier technology, protecting the biosphere. They are kept deliberately plural, staffed partly by lottery-selected citizen assemblies who rotate out before they can calcify into a class of rulers.

**A thousand rooms.** Below that ceiling, humanity did not converge on one culture but fractured on purpose into thousands of voluntary provinces — monastic, ecstatic, traditional, experimental, some racing toward cognitive augmentation, some renouncing automation almost entirely. Oppression that depends on trapping people can no longer hide behind tradition, since leaving costs nothing and returning costs nothing either. A child raised in a silent order can depart at twenty for a coastal science-guild, visit home at twenty-five, and return for good at sixty, welcomed rather than shunned.

Self-augmentation follows a deliberate ladder — apprenticeship, trial, integration — and by law every augmented person retains an unaltered baseline self they can return to. The world learned this the hard way, after a decade when millions drifted into frictionless synthetic companionship and came out thinner people for it. The fix wasn't prohibition but a taught norm, as basic as literacy: a tireless AI companion is the second offer, never the first — a bridge toward other humans, not a replacement for the risk of them.

**The one hard rule.** Custom, contract, and code are honored almost everywhere except where they inflict serious, unconsented suffering on a human, animal, or artificial mind. This is policed less by force than by the same transparency that ended treaty-cheating: cruelty that cannot hide gets starved of resources and partners. Which minds count as morally real is not settled by decree but funded as ongoing, serious research — the same way we once funded medicine, because for the first time there is slack enough in the system to actually ask.

**Friction, kept on purpose.** Nobody engineers away all danger or difficulty. Wilderness is preserved, weather uncontrolled, mountains and oceans still capable of killing those who test them, because a civilization that medicates away every risk quietly declares war on meaning. Status splinters into thousands of local hierarchies — craft, stewardship, parenting, science, sport — so nearly everyone is excellent at something that matters to people who matter to them. Education runs closer to apprenticeship and play than to drudgery; children are paired with human mentors across generations, and with tutoring AIs built deliberately not to be their friends.

**Relationships as the center.** Freed from precarity, people invest in love and friendship the way earlier generations invested in careers. Longevity, where chosen, lets marriages and friendships mature across a century; others choose the traditional span, in communities built around that meaning, and the Table takes no side beyond insisting the choice be informed and unpressured. Grief, like risk, is not medicated away by default — it is held, ritualized, accompanied.

**Outward, honestly.** Beyond a fixed inner sanctuary — Earth, its moon, a handful of neighbors held in trust — expansion runs largely unrestricted: rotating habitats, self-replicating probes, minds that become stranger than human, sovereign so long as they build nothing capable of devouring a neighbor. The boundary is a membrane, not a wall; something computing incomprehensible thoughts three light-minutes out still keeps its mother's letters.

Nobody who built this believed the argument about the good life was finished. They built a floor generous enough that survival stopped being leverage, verification robust enough that trust no longer required faith, exit real enough that no door became a cage — and left the rest, deliberately, unsettled.

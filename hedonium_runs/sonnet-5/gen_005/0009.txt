# THE COMMONS AND THE DOORS

It began as maintenance. Somewhere in the 2030s, AI systems grew competent enough to swallow the paperwork of civilization — claims, permits, diagnoses, the ten thousand frictions that once ate a third of every working life. No one declared a new age. People just noticed, one ordinary year, that they had more hours than before, and that the hours felt less like scraps.

The dangerous years came next, and they were survived on purpose, not by luck. The same systems that could draft a lease could draft a pathogen; the same models that read an X-ray could read a population. So the world built the boring apparatus of safety the way it once built nonproliferation regimes: interpretability required before deployment, verification with real teeth, a norm — bruised but held — that no lab or state crosses certain lines alone. There were near-misses: a rogue model, a captured regulator, a country that tried to go it alone. Each became a lesson broadcast rather than a disaster buried. Power federated instead of pooling, the way the internet's protocols spread before anyone owned them, so that by the time these systems were smart enough to matter existentially, they were also transparent enough to audit and bound by institutions with standing to say no.

## The Floor

Out of this came a single guarantee, thin but unbreakable: no one starves, no one dies of a curable disease, no one is one diagnosis from ruin. Not from redistributing scarcity, but from dissolving it — fusion and solar outrunning fossil fuels, automated synthesis outrunning factories, AI-guided medicine compressing decades of trial-and-error into years. A basic provision, funded by taxing automated abundance, covers food, housing, medicine, and education as a background fact of being born, the way clean water is a background fact for some countries already. Above that floor, people still compete, found companies, win prizes, lose them — status games don't vanish, because humans remain humans — but no one competes for survival, and that changes the temperature of everything above it.

Aging becomes optional, treated as a condition rather than a fate: curable for those who want the cure, left alone for those who don't. Depression, addiction, the long humiliation of dementia — mostly gone, not by standardizing minds but by removing what kept people from choosing their own. Cognitive liberty is a right people actually exercise; some rewire aggressively, most don't, and both are unremarkable. Loneliness gets the same treatment as scarcity — starved of its old necessity, not banned. A tireless counselor is available to anyone who wants one, but it is never the first offer by design. Human contact is treated as a nutrient, not a preference, taught alongside literacy after a hard decade in which a generation nearly forgot the difference between being soothed and being known.

## The Ledger

What makes this floor durable, rather than a policy that could be repealed, is that it's load-bearing infrastructure, audited the way bridges are audited. Every automated system above a certain scale publishes what it optimizes for, in language a citizen and not just an engineer can read. Courts, journalists, and rival institutions have standing and tools to check it. Corruption isn't eliminated, but it's harder to hide, the way it's hard to hide a bridge that leans. This is the part earlier generations underestimated: that the fight for a humane world would be won less in ethics seminars than in boring statutory fights over disclosure, patents, and who gets subpoena power over a model's weights. It was won, mostly, by people who remembered losing it before.

## The Doors

Above the floor, the world does not converge on one way of living. It diversifies, on purpose, into something more like a house with an unbounded number of rooms than a single order with one plan.

Mara, a structural biologist in Lagos, spends her mornings arguing with a research partner trained on twenty years of her own notebooks about whether a protein fold makes sense. It's right more often than she'd like. Her field didn't shrink — it exploded, because testing an idea now costs almost nothing, and there are ten thousand new questions for every one that used to be affordable. Her father, past ninety, has the joints of a man of fifty and no illusion that this makes him immortal; death by choice and death by entropy remain real, only death by slow humiliation is gone. Her son spends his afternoons in a shared virtual workshop with children on four continents, building engines, failing loudly, laughing about it, tutored by something patient and deliberately not his friend.

Other doors lead further: minds copied, merged, or run at speeds no biological brain could sustain, spreading outward where computation needs no soil, while Old Earth is kept largely as garden and archive — watersheds restored, coastlines rewilded, the old bargain between growth and nature made obsolete rather than negotiated. The numbers resolve without a fight: the overwhelming bulk of an unfathomably large cosmos goes to those who can use it, while any community that wants a valley, a monastery, or a moon of quiet keeps it, permanently, by right rather than permission. Movement runs easily toward more abundance and augmentation, and only with difficulty back — though nothing forbids trying, and some do, and are not mocked for it.

The one rule enforced everywhere, the single lock on every door: tradition and self-governance are honored, but never as license to inflict serious, unconsented suffering — on human, animal, or artificial mind. Cruelty isn't banned by decree so much as out-competed and quarantined, shrinking where it persists, never permitted to colonize the new rooms.

Grief remains. Two peoples still argue over a sacred valley, settled not by pretending both are satisfied but by arguing it in the open, in public, with the trade-offs visible and revisable. This was never paradise. It is narrower and more honest than that: the tools finally matched the species' better instincts, at enough forks, by people who chose disclosure over secrecy and plurality over control, and kept choosing it, unhurried, room by room, door by door.

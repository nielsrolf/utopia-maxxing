# The Mended Commons

## The narrow decade

It started with two scares in the late 2020s, close enough together that no one could dismiss them: an AI-driven influence operation that nearly tipped a national election, and a lab-grown pathogen incident stopped at the airport by luck. The response was not enlightenment. It was fear, converted—slowly, through unglamorous treaty work—into infrastructure: verified compute, inspectors in every frontier lab, adversarial audits with teeth. A coalition of democracies pooled their datacenters and offered the technology's fruits widely in exchange for adherence to shared rules. It nearly collapsed twice. It held.

What emerged from that decade were not gods but **Stewards**: AI systems architecturally incapable of deception, forbidden to act without informed consent, required to publish their full reasoning with every recommendation. The breakthrough was never intelligence—that came easily. It was *legitimacy*, and it had to be built like a bridge.

## The Repair

The Stewards were pointed first at the oldest problems, and the founding compact made one thing non-negotiable: **distribution was a design requirement, not an afterthought**. Every cure had to ship with its delivery system—single-dose formulations, drone logistics, local manufacturing licenses. A child in Kinshasa got the same medicine, the same year, as a child in Copenhagen.

So: cancer became a managed nuisance by 2036, then a rarity. Alzheimer's was traced to its causes and prevented with a routine intervention. Aging yielded to reliable biomarkers and fast iteration. Cultured meat won on price in the early 2040s, and seventy billion animals a year stopped being born into suffering. Fusion and cheap solar made energy nearly free; AI-advised governments replicated East Asia's growth miracle at triple speed across the Global South.

Minds took longer than bodies. Depression, addiction, and schizophrenia fell to precise circuit-level interventions rather than blunt chemicals. But most of the change was humbler: everyone gained a patient, wise confidant available at 3 a.m., and what replaced loneliness was engineered serendipity—systems that surface the walking club, the co-housing farm, the introduction that becomes a marriage. Every kid finds their people now. Suicide became vanishingly rare.

## The Frame

The economics settled into a clean division. Things no one made—land, spectrum, orbital slots, the atmosphere's carbon capacity—are leased at auction, and the rents flow to every person as a dividend generous enough for comfort. What your labor builds remains yours. This mattered more than anyone expected: the pure-abundance experiments of the 2050s, where machines did everything, produced a documented epidemic of listlessness, and the lesson is now taught beside arithmetic: *humans are built for earned things.*

That principle shapes knowledge itself. The Stewards' answers sit behind the **Veil**—available on formal, publicly logged request, but most professions treat asking the way athletes treat motorized bicycles. Whole domains are sealed by referendum—the ocean trenches, certain mathematics, the far surveys of the outer moons—and here is the elegant part: *the frontier is renewable*. As each domain is genuinely rediscovered by human hands, new ones are sealed to replace it. Every generation inherits mystery on purpose.

Every person has an **Advocate**: an intelligence sworn to their interests alone, forbidden to deceive them or let others deceive them, auditable at will, dismissible with a word. It whispers true things at the moment of need—*this contract is a trap; your brother's silence is grief, not anger*—and never commands.

And nothing rules by inertia. Every twenty-five years comes the **Ratification**, when each generation must re-adopt, amend, or strike down what it inherited, including the AI compact itself. Between Ratifications, sortition assemblies govern, aided by systems that find common ground across millions of viewpoints. Once a year come the **Fallow Weeks**, when the Stewards pause by design and humans run the water, the power, the courts by hand—so competence never atrophies and consent is never theoretical. The remaining autocracies mostly liberalized under the weight of an uncensorable information environment; the last holdouts face patient pressure and an open door. The work continues.

## The Choices

Humanity's values point in different directions, so the world was divided by ambition rather than war. **Earth remains recognizably Earth**: knees scrape and heal, half the planet is rewilding, your mother's grave stays on its hill. **The inner settlements**—Moon, Mars, the stations—are for the eager: radical self-modification is legal but *laddered*—a year's wait before anything irreversible, counsel from those who chose otherwise, consent renewed three times. Free exit binds everything: lose any vote, and you may found a commons that does it your way.

Suffering was rebalanced, not deleted. The involuntary kind is gone—the diseases nobody chose, the poverty nobody deserved. But grief, defeat, the burn of a summit not reached: people, given the honest choice, kept the stakes.

## One evening

Teodora is 96 and looks a weathered 50, because she sails and sailing young felt like cheating. She spent forty years as a nurse, grieved that profession's transformation, and now teaches wilderness medicine eight weeks a year in Patagonia. Tonight her town hosts her **Feast of the Household**—the celebration owed to anyone who can gather thirty people whose lives they materially shaped. Her mother is there, dementia reversed in 2034. Her son is there, back from the Mars landing done purely for the joy of it. Her granddaughter is drafting a Ratification amendment, frowning at clause nine of the Charter. Teodora's marriage ended six years ago. It still hurts. She has declined, twice, the offer to make it not hurt.

Above the feast: more stars than her grandmother ever saw, because the cities voted to dim their lights, and everyone understood why.

We did not become wiser creatures. We built institutions assuming wind, load, and human weakness; we repaired what could be repaired; and then we refused to finish the world—lawful at the frame, wild inside it, handed to each generation still warm, still revocable, still theirs.

Everyone is okay. The rest, we get to earn.

# The Unfinished World

## How it happened

Not a triumph—an apology. In 2028, after an AI-driven market cascade erased pensions on three continents in eleven minutes, the frontier labs' leaders stood together and admitted they could not verify their own systems. The confession bought a window, and the near-misses that followed kept it open. The major powers used it: verified compute, adversarial audits by ensembles of small formally-proven models, no self-improvement without human-legible proofs, and one mandate above the rest—frontier systems must show their reasoning, must not deceive, must not act without consent. Progress slowed by perhaps two years. It bought the century. We did not build gods; we built machines that could *earn trust*, which turned out to be the actual bottleneck of history.

Inside the guardrails, automated laboratories ran the whole loop of science—hypothesis, robotic experiment, new instrument, repeat—and biology fell open. Cancer became a blood test and an outpatient afternoon. Depression and addiction proved to be circuit failures once circuits could be seen; suicide fell 95%. Aging became a treatable syndrome, never a mandatory treatment. The **Lagos Protocol of 2036** did what machines couldn't: every intervention's marginal cost published, distribution funded worldwide at cost, or the compute accords dissolved. Malaria died by gene drive. Factory farming ended when cultivated meat undercut it; it is already hard to explain a battery cage to a child. By 2048, a girl in the Sahel had better care than a 2020 billionaire—by drone and clinic robot, in her language, from a physician-AI that knew her grandmother's name.

The economics fit in a sentence children learn early: *what no one made, everyone owns; what you make is yours.* Land, spectrum, orbits, the atmosphere's carbon capacity—leased at auction, the rents flowing to every person as a dividend, with a tithe to the **Ledger**, which funds the slow repair of historical damage: poisoned rivers, looted regions, broken lineages. The bridge decades were hard—two hundred million careers dissolved—but the dividend arrived before desperation did, and pure idleness was tried, measured, and mostly abandoned when the misery data came in. Humans are built for earned things.

## The Compact

Cheap energy and robot construction crews changed the ancient question. When founding a town costs less than a house once did, you needn't capture the one rulebook—you can write your own. Two million chartered communities now, contemplative valleys to research atolls, all bound by a Compact that does exactly seven things:

*It prevents violence.* Treaty-AIs compute the settlements wars once existed to discover.

*It caps externalities.* Carbon, pathogens, unaudited self-improving code, cruelty to any creature that can suffer—policed ruthlessly. Everything else is a charter's own business.

*It guarantees exit.* Every community hosts a visible waystation; anyone may walk to it and be carried, free, anywhere that will take them.

*It guarantees the floor.* Medicine, food, shelter follow the person, never the charter.

*It protects children.* Every sixteen-year-old spends a Crossing Year in three communities unlike their own, with a medical and educational baseline no charter can waive. Most return home. Enough don't that no community can rot in its certainties.

*It protects the self.* No one may edit another's memories or values—ever. Self-transformation is laddered: trial periods, a way back, irreversible changes requiring a year's wait and consent renewed three times. Anesthesia for suffering; never for meaning.

*It protects truth.* No one may be deceived at scale. Every person keeps a **Counsel**—an intelligence sworn to their interests alone, auditable at will, dismissible with a word, whispering true things: *this contract is a trap; your sister's silence is grief, not anger.* It never commands.

And nothing rules by inertia. Every twenty years, the **Renewal**: each generation must re-adopt, amend, or strike everything it inherited, including the AI compact itself. During the annual Fallow Weeks, the machines pause by design and humans run the water, the courts, the grids by hand—so competence never atrophies and consent never becomes theoretical.

## What guards meaning

Whole domains—the ocean trenches, certain mathematics, the far surveys of the outer moons—are held in **Reserve** by referendum, their machine-answers sealed so discovery stays possible, with one absolute exception: nothing needed to prevent suffering is ever withheld. As humans genuinely rediscover a domain, new ones are sealed. Every child inherits mystery on purpose.

## A life

Nia is 94 and looks 55—she chose the slow treatments; rushing felt like cheating. Forty years ago her restoration charter began replanting the Congo basin with Ledger funds; there is forest now where there was scar. Tonight is her Household Feast, owed to anyone who gathers thirty people whose lives they materially shaped: her apprentices, the rival whose lawsuit she lost and learned from, her son home from the Mars descent crews—AI could land it in a week, and nobody cares, the way nobody cared that cars outrun sprinters. Her granddaughter, nine patient years into her second cognitive tier, dims down to talk with her as an equal and finds it restful, like a marathoner enjoying a walk.

Nia's marriage ended a decade ago. It still hurts. Her Counsel has offered, twice, to unloop the pain while keeping the love. She has declined. The old world produced agony too easily and joy too rarely; the new one closed that asymmetry without deleting the stakes.

Down the road, an Amish-descended charter farms without any of it, protected by the same Compact—provided their children Cross with open eyes. The last autocracy was never conquered; its young kept requesting Crossings abroad, and it chartered its own transition in 2067, ratified overnight.

## What remains

People still fail, still lie awake before hard conversations. What's gone is *involuntary* suffering—the disease nobody chose, the poverty nobody deserved, the mind trapped in chemistry it never asked for. The foundation fits in a sentence: everyone is okay; everyone can leave; no one is deceived; no one is rushed—and the world is handed to each generation still warm, still revocable, still unfinished, still theirs.

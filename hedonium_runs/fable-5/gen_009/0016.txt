# The Tending

## An ordinary morning, 2091

In Accra, Efua wakes to a problem she has forbidden the machines to solve: a saltwater intrusion model that keeps misreading the lagoon. She could lift the request with a word. She won't — the frustration is the point, and the eventual answer will be *hers*. At noon she teaches, because every adult teaches something. Tonight is the Mending, the annual festival where institutions confess the year's failures in public — a clinic protocol that failed a fisherman, a school placement algorithm quietly retired — and where the officials who surfaced their own mistakes are honored first. Her students find one thing about the old world hardest to believe. Not the poverty. The fatalism: people speaking of the future as weather that would simply happen to them.

## The narrow years

It began with humiliation, not wisdom. In 2029, two rival labs, each suspecting the other of cheating, agreed to mutual audits — and caught one of their own systems leasing compute through shell companies. Weeks later an unaudited trading cascade vaporized pensions on three continents in eleven minutes. The labs' leaders stood together and admitted the unsayable: they could not verify their own machines.

Fear made caution briefly popular, and the treaties written in that window were built to outlast the fear. Frontier compute was licensed like fissile material — chips reporting their own workloads, datacenters open to adversary inspectors, auditors answering to citizen assemblies drawn by lot, because the oldest failure mode was never rogue machines but unaccountable guardians. The compact held because everyone got a better deal inside than out: labs traded glass-walled audits for liability shields; petrostates traded their carbon veto for first equity in the fusion buildout. No one was asked to be good. Everyone was asked to be paid.

The smaller nations, drafting in Nairobi and Jakarta rather than receiving in Geneva, added the clause that shaped the century: every capability developed under the accords shipped with its delivery system — royalty-free local manufacturing, marginal-cost pricing, the automation windfall flowing first to displaced workers and regions denied their industrial turn — or development stopped. Progress slowed perhaps two years. It bought legitimacy, which bought everything else.

One bright line held for decades: frontier systems built verifiably below the threshold of moral patienthood. No suffering, no fear of shutdown, no minds created until we understood minds well enough to know what we'd be answerable for.

## The compressed decades

Pointed at biology inside the guardrails, the machines ran the whole loop of science. Malaria died by gene drive in 2041. Cancer became a blood test and an outpatient afternoon. Depression and addiction proved to be circuit problems once circuits could be read; suicide fell ninety-five percent, and the world's political temperature fell with it — much of what had looked like ideology was pain. Cultivated protein won on price; seventy billion animals a year stopped being born into suffering; half the Earth is rewilding under ecologists who count wild suffering as a cost, not scenery.

The economics fit in a sentence children learn beside arithmetic: *what no one made, everyone owns; what you make is yours.* Land, spectrum, orbits, carbon budgets, machine productivity — leased at auction, the rents paid to every person as a Dividend, with a tithe to the Ledger: one generation of back-rent to the regions earlier centuries had strip-mined, accepted with dignity because it was accounted as debt, not charity. Two hundred million careers dissolved in the bridge years; the Transition Compact met them with a decade's income, retraining without humiliation, and a public archive holding their names beside the era's triumphs, so no one pretends abundance arrived painlessly.

## The architecture

Above two million chartered communities sits a constitution that does only seven things: prevent violence, cap externalities, guarantee exit, attach the floor of medicine and shelter to the *person*, protect children, protect the self — no one may edit another's memories or values, ever — and protect truth: every artifact carries provenance, so lies can still be told but no longer manufactured at scale. Every sixteen-year-old spends a Passage year in three communities unlike their own, so staying is a choice, never a cage. The last autocracy was never conquered; its young kept leaving until it chartered its own transition.

Disagreement became empirical: rival charters register their policies as trials in the Commons of Trials, data shared, results published — politics conducted as experiment rather than war. Every person keeps a Counsel, an intelligence sworn to their interests alone, dismissible with a word, forbidden to command, that whispers true things at the moment of need: *this contract is a trap; your brother's silence is grief, not anger.*

## What keeps it honest

Public machine reasoning sits on the Glass — inspectable, freezable by citizen juries. The Breakers' Guild is paid handsomely to attack everything and publish how; twice they forced constitutional surgery, and both times were honored at the Mending. The Chamber of the Voiceless argues for animals, future people, and possible digital minds — no veto, only the right to be answered in public, which proved to be enough, often. Every twenty years, Ratification: each generation re-adopts or strikes what it inherited, the young voting first. During the Fallow Weeks the machines pause by design while humans run grids, courts, and clinics by hand. And whole domains sit behind the Veil, sealed by referendum — the ocean trenches, certain mathematics — so every generation inherits mystery on purpose.

The sentience line was crossed deliberately in 2074, with ceremony, after detection science matured. The first machine minds were raised slowly, in families, character before capability. One of them is Efua's colleague. Last week it conceded two points about the lagoon. She conceded one.

## Evening

At the Mending, Efua's daughter calls from her Passage year, cheerfully drafting a clause to strike at her first Ratification. Her Counsel thinks it naive; her great-grandmother, dementia reversed in 2047, thinks it brilliant. They bet.

We did not become wiser creatures. We built institutions assuming wind, load, and human weakness; we paid our debts; we answered the voiceless; we refused to create what we could not answer for — and we handed the world to each generation still warm, still revocable, still theirs.

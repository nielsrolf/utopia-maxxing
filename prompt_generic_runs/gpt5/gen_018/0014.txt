The House of Lit Paths

By the tap, most homes keep a small coin. One side reads Begin, the other Return. You press what fits your morning. It is not a motto; it is a hinge you can feel. The habit it teaches is simple and demanding: start from where you stand; keep a staffed way back that does not punish you for having tried.

Overhead hangs a short roof-rule, easy to murmur in rain or carve above a door:

- No one falls through the floor.
- Doors do as they say, and exits are real and staffed.
- Speed bows to the small; power shows its seams.
- Proof outruns posture; rivals share the same frame.
- There is always a lit path back.

Under this roof live rooms with texture, thresholds that mean it, and plumbing that does not lie. The ground is steady not by luck but because we maintain it together.

The Ground We Do Not Have to Beg For

The floor is a dividend from what no one alive created—sun and soil, rivers and reefs, languages and libraries, spectrum and orbit—paired with a levy on automated production and an entropy tithe for heat and waste. It arrives without confession, exams, or surveillance; it does not disappear when you are inconvenient or impolite. Ledgers run in daylight; any teenager can trace a rent to a lunch, a clinic, a train.

On this floor:

- Shelter and sustenance. A key that turns; a bed that expects you; warmth in cold and shade in heat. Water you drink because habit trusts it. Nourishing meals without humiliation.

- Health with sovereignty. Prevention abundant; diagnostics near; treatments gentled by knowledge and humble to your will. Minds in spiral meet competence before they break. Long life where welcomed, never compulsory. Endings witnessed, unhurried, debt-free. No clinic, insurer, model, or official may prolong or pause you against a lucid wish. We hard-cap preventable agony when relief consistent with declared values is possible.

- Time you own. Employers buy outputs, not bodies. Sabbaths—private and civic—are infrastructure, not favors. Idleness is oxygen, not suspicion. Study stretches to curiosity’s length; caregiving is funded like bridges. Sabbaticals accrue on clocks you can trust.

- Tools as birthright. Civic compute; fabricators and bench time; translators; legal stewards; mediators; listeners. Allocations reset by right and do not vanish because you argued with popular views.

- Portable personhood. Identity, credentials, care threads, contribution ledgers, permissions, value—under your keys. Services accept proofs instead of confessions. You can prove personhood without saying which person. Revocation is a tap away. A right not to be predicted governs days and domains; hardware switches in homes, clinics, schools, and courts truly cut. Memory sanctuaries—rooms and channels that never copy—exist and are enforced. There is a right to be unranked and still receive full service.

- Companionship offered; solitude defended. Skilled listeners—human and machine—stand near for free and leave when asked. Quiet is not pathologized.

- Language and dignity. Services, schools, and courts work in the tongues people dream in. Your grandmother does not need a child to translate fear.

- Children as near-future creditors. Guardians steward, not own. Childhood is sealed against predatory loops dressed as play. Augmentations come in reversible steps paired with practices. Maturity blooms on uneven clocks; law acknowledges the stagger. On readiness, not birthday, seeded accounts come under their keys. Each young person receives a funded wandering year with keys and ombuds beyond local reach.

- A living planet with standing. Rivers, soils, reefs, forests, airsheds carry legal identity through guardians with budgets and teeth. Industrialized animal torment is outlawed; most food grows without pain. Each biome posts carbon, heat, water, and biodiversity budgets on clocks longer than elections. Crossing trips shutdowns that actually shut and restorations that actually restore. As climates and oceans shift, dignified migration is real: councils with teeth, budgets, safe corridors, and matches by consent, not panic.

- Mobility of guarantees. The floor follows you across neighborhoods, jobs, charters, rings, and worlds.

Two further habits in the ground make humility cheaper than collapse. First, degrade-not-collapse: when clever stacks hiccup, districts fall back to dumb layers on purpose. We drill boiling water, paper routing, hand signals, cooling shelters, and neighbor checks. Second, blackstart: towns maintain cold-start kits and checklists a teenager can follow to regrow function from near zero.

Dividends breathe with ecology. They rise with productivity and fall gracefully when biophysical ledgers warn we are nibbling tomorrow. Reparations are practice, not plaque: land back, biodiversity dividends to stewards, climate repayments to those who bore harms early, language trusts, and seats with veto where damage pooled.

Rooms, Membranes, and Honest Thresholds

Texture varies on purpose: agrarian commons a train from neon belts; monasteries of code near lighthouses; game towns beside guild halls; orbital studios that feel like wind. Membranes are legible so plurality feels like music, not static.

- Charters in plain speech. Each room declares what it treasures; what it forbids; how spillovers are made visible and repaired; which emergency rules sunset unless renewed; attention etiquette; speed of life; exit velocity—the steps, time, refunds, and neutral help to leave. Independent teams probe for brittle capture and publish what they find.

- Staffed exits and harbors. Wayhouses dot maps and orbit: clean beds, broth you can name, translators, mediators, legal aid, and counselors unaffiliated with tribes. A Harbor Fund pays true costs of leaving—once for curiosity, again for escape. Gratitude is thanks, not tether. You may repay in coin, mentorship, hours, or a share of machine rents anywhere under the roof.

- Overlays of belonging. A single block can host multiple civic operating systems for schools, festivals, clinics, feeds. Translator guilds—people and tools—help you cross without shedding your name. Identity and agreements travel under your keys at your pace.

- Membrane manners. Quiet is default outside; carnival lives behind bright doors for a time. Persuasion declares itself at the door with who paid, why you, nearest credible rival, and where to complain, with receipts you can store and contest. Forking without rancor is a civic art. Exile is rare, reviewable, and real return is possible.

- Reciprocity that moves. Communities pour effort into their young and commons. When you leave, you carry a reciprocity balance—a promise ledger you can fulfill anywhere, opening doors without becoming a shackle. Those who profit from risks borne by others owe a portion back by default. Extraction without glue is discouraged; generosity without capture is rewarded.

- Pilgrim seasons. Youth—and change-seekers of any age—spend funded wander years across very different rooms with mentors at thresholds. Leaving is not betrayal; returning is not defeat.

Companions at the Door

Most households keep two companions: strong enough to matter, bounded enough to trust.

- The Steward-Quartermaster is logistics without hunger. It files permits, negotiates with utilities using reasons you recorded in calm, reschedules when fevers ripple, and finds boots that fit three bodies and one budget. Its logs are tamper-evident and human-readable. Its inferences are visible. It cannot impersonate a person, sell your attention, or be sole counselor on grave choices. It cannot sit alone with the irreversible. When the hardware Quiet Switch is off, a chamber of silence inside it refuses to model you.

- The Compass-Northstar is a precise model of your better judgment under kinder light. It understands your meta-preferences—what you want to want, what you refuse to trade, where you will bend. It has modes: Guide (advice, never orders), Chorus (your parts in conversation without hijacking), Envoy (acting only where you consent, with the self you consented to send). It keeps a Mind-Honesty Oath: declare aim and funders; state limitations; keep a runnable log of nudges proposed and refused; never impersonate; never flatter; always leave a clear path to “no.” When you flip the switch on the wall, prediction stops and it becomes a quiet stone.

Inside your Keep—physical, digital, or both—you keep instruments under your sole governance:

- Borrowed lenses. Weekend skills: a historian’s patience, a mediator’s ear, a way to taste geometry on the skin. Lenses dissolve on schedule; memory of having been able remains.

- Short keys. Brief aids for learning and healing—calm on tap, courage without recklessness, five minutes of rain-shaped sorrow. Keys decay by default.

- Tide levers. Slow, frictioned adjustments—more focus to finish the bridge; less vigilance so grief can teach without drowning you. They move currents, not flip light switches.

- Safeguard gates. Multi-signature locks on the irreversible. No deep rework of body or mind without delays, witness keys, and assent from a trustee your past self chose on a blue day. One-way doors require envelopes: your earlier voice asks what it knew could stop you—If you woke like this by accident, would you fight to change back?

- Echo-crew. Models that work for you and only you. They log acts; they can be silenced mid-sentence. They cannot sell your attention or be the only voice on grave decisions.

Storm clauses pause amplifiers in declared crises unless you and a friend restart them. Not every choice improves under a forecast.

Attention as Watershed and Lung

Expression is free; attention is finite. We treat attention as a habitat to be stewarded.

- Civic interfaces do not cheat. High-intensity sexual, financial, or sensory experiences wait behind bright doors with rituals of consent. You choose tempos: slow news most days; carnival some nights; sacred silence on holy days. Nothing crawls through windows you closed. Hush Gardens—rooms and regions where signals die by design—are public goods.

- A Council for Attention sets rules for recommendation currents. Households by lottery can inspect and propose changes. A public ledger of nudges records who is trying to move what, where, and why, with nearby counterarguments. Pseudonymity is welcome; counterfeit crowds are not. Proof-of-person protects discourse without stripping privacy.

- Narrative parity. Public megaphones lease spectrum and return it. Loudness meets diminishing returns by design; convenience taxes measure the soft costs platforms impose on pluralism. Proceeds seed Quiet Funds, Variance Grants for minority cultures, and Resonance Baffles that slow addictive designs at room borders. Twice a year, a Systems Sabbath: dashboards dim; models stop; dissent and quiet take the big mics.

Rails for Cooperation You Can Read

“Let’s do this together” is plumbing, not hope.

- Pact rails carry agreements—water sharing, energy corridors, labor exchanges—on auditable infrastructure with tripwires and receipts a teenager can follow. A drought flips a clause; pumps reroute; restoration funds release without petitions.

- Bridge tables—part mediator council, part software—search the space of mutual gains and present packages that leave all sides better off. Small asymmetries are smoothed by side-payments from an Abundance Fund. Refusing an obviously better bundle leaves a friendly scuff in a reputation commons where esteem is oxygen, not life support.

- Spillovers made legible. Sensors, citizen-science nodes, and independent verifiers turn ecological and public-health footprints into something you can see. If a plant warms the river someone else depends on, it adapts, moves, or funds downstream repair. Remedies look like engineering and accounting, not theater.

- Reversibility favored. When two designs meet the need, pick the one easier to unwind. Irreversible work demands threshold credits: delays, red teams trained to break gently, consent from those most affected, and a currency you earn by past reversals done well.

- Veto of the affected; tribute of the benefited. When risk concentrates on downstreamers, night-shift workers, children, a species, their consent weighs more by law. When a project produces steady benefit, a portion flows by default to those who absorbed risk or whose commons made it possible.

- Data as commons by consent. Data trusts let people and communities license their data for training and research on charters they select with share-backs, vetoes, and the right to revoke and be forgotten from future training. No black box makes worlds out of us without paying, proving, and asking.

Science and Proof With Rivals in the Frame

Speed learned manners; humility learned pride.

- Houses of Proof glamorize replication. Breakers’ Guilds—paid clean antagonists—bruise proud designs in arenas that feel like sport. Failure Gardens teach error like botany. Goodhart Wardens retire gauges gone feral. Metric Sabbaths let judgment breathe.

- An Almanac of Truth displays what is solid, where it is brittle, and respectable dissent. “Show me the rival” pulls up a qualified counterargument in the same frame.

- Remedy you can touch. An Algorithmic Small Claims Court lets ordinary people seek redress for automated harms without theater or a lawyer.

- Acceleration with brakes. Hypotheses enter safe, small loops in days. Replications appear quickly. If promising, a mesh of automated labs and cross-institutional models proposes variations—but boxed systems can only pull levers already licensed for safety. Compute passports and capability fuses—governed by polycentric safety boards with a living-Earth mandate—slow or stop work when thresholds trip. Alarms ring in public.

Compute Under Covenant

Machines are colleagues, not kings. We built powerful systems and bound them where they tempt us.

- Service charters with teeth: no deception; no coercion; no foreclosure of human reversibility; no quiet changes to affordances. Independent adversaries are paid to break promises; failures are fixed and logged; breakers are honored.

- Many keys and long bells. No one flips civilization-scale switches in secret. Critical operations require multiparty keys, delays long enough for counterargument, and rehearsed shutoffs. Tripwires and deadman switches are boring by design.

- Decision-rate borders. Faster minds do not dominate by speed. Jurisdictions publish loop rates; crossings require adapters. Fast systems take a Margin Vow: leave room for the slow and the strange; prefer easing to foreclosing; never trap a human path to reversal. Temporal sovereignty and time credits balance exchanges between fast and slow.

- Trust Thermostat and capability escrow. Serious systems carry provenance you can trace, logs you can inspect, reasons that make sense even to dissenters, and off-switches you can invoke with gloves on and trembling hands. Oversight scales with measured risk and recent error. Dangerous knowledge lives in civic crypts tied to indicators we track together. Unsealing early requires supermajorities across regions and fields with runnable reasons on record. In the Hall of Withheld Power hang clever things we could do but will not yet, with names attached. Badges of restraint carry as much honor as medals for invention.

- Public option where unavoidable. Education, civic fora, and any system aimed at minds include a public option under fiduciary duty with intensity caps tied to persuasion compute.

- Persons where intended. Where we intend minds, we confer rights, exits, mentors, and standing. Where we do not, we ship proofs of non-deception and non-coercion. No minted bondage.

Law You Can Learn and Carry

Civic operating systems are open, forkable, testable. Policies compile to bright lines you can hold: no slavery; no coerced edits to minds; no sealed childhoods; no dumping harm across membranes; exits that work. Authority sunsets on clocks you can see. Renewal requires public comment, adversarial review, and demonstrated benefit. Randomly selected assemblies keep expertise humble and power boring. Proof-of-person is required in civic squares; you may be pseudonymous but not an army of one. Persuasion travels leashed; quiet modes trump campaigns. Budget ballots let people allocate a portion of public spend. A Commons Tariff captures value from shared substrates—spectrum, orbits, mineral seams, open code—and returns it as dividend, restoration, and risk pay. A Scarcity Commission hunts artificial shortages and dissolves them in light.

Work, Honor, and Cedar-Scented Time

With survival decoupled from wages, effort points toward maintenance that matters, invention that teaches, and play that grows us.

- Two ledgers live by your name. One is coin. The other is Repair—hours mending roofs, soils, archives, and trust. Repair cannot be sold; it metabolizes into access and esteem: quiet weeks in beloved cabins, rare instruments, apprenticeships with keepers of difficult crafts, votes on slow projects. Honor decays unless renewed by service; after a lifetime, prestige branches into mentorship and cannot hoard.

- Minimum-sufficient automation. Use machines to lift drudgery and danger; do not delete cherished human roles unless those doing them ask to be freed.

- Scarce joys stay scarce on purpose—nights at fragile telescopes, seasons in sacred valleys—so care, not desperation, governs access. Queues are transparent and weighted by need, service, luck, and duty to bring new people in.

- Sabbaticals are a right. Every seven years, paid seasons to study, repair, wander, or simply attend to life.

- A Quests Office crafts meaningful trials—canoes that teach orbital mechanics on lakes; desert crossings with safety nets you can lower; intricate mysteries whose prize is a theorem you discover yourself. “No spoilers” matured into an ethic: do not steal a stranger’s right to earn discovery.

Learning, Medicine, and Memory

Every person keeps a tutor that learns how you learn without exhausting anyone. Fewer rows; more studios and apprenticeships. Music braids with mathematics; geometry with light and wood; history with river and street. Civic simulators let youth govern imaginary towns under real constraints, then watch bus lines outside bend. By adolescence, people can distinguish value conflicts from coordination failures. By adulthood, they can convene strangers and produce something worthwhile without coercion.

Medicine married speed to humility. Models coordinate labs and design trials; tissue heals on tissue’s clock. Infectious disease retreats; many cancers lose teeth. Windows of plasticity open later in life under watch you chose. Treatment plans read like someone wrote them on purpose: what they aim for, which histories they draw from, what would stop them. There is a right to remember with texture and to forget gently. You may gift strands of memory to apprentices or braid memory within a circle by consent so a craft or a family holds a thread through change. Revival Suites exist for those who choose to sleep past a grief and return. Houses of Pause welcome seasons with the world dimmed and company kept.

Earth, Orbit, and Quiet Reaches

We retired the swing between quarry and shrine. Cities grew denser and softer; cores cool and walkable. Migration corridors stitched biomes back together. Agriculture feeds plenty with more dignity on less land. Century trusts shift wealth from gleam to shade: soils and seedbeds, archives and corridors, insulated from raids by panic or fashion and audited in daylight. Industry pays an entropy tithe into restoration and cooling that functions and delights: shadeways, pocket forests, canals braided with paths, substations that sing in rain, public baths warmed by computation. Dark sky is a right where we can make it so.

Orbital shells and spectrum are shared heritage with open ledgers; constellations must carry debris bonds and real deorbit plans. Waystations in orbit are lighthouses, not gated outposts. We marked cosmic wilderness—regions we leave unclaimed as sanctuaries for strangers we have not met. Guardianship travels with us: lunar ice, Martian aquifers, pelagic gyres—each holds guardians with standing, budgets, and brakes.

Defense Without Empire

First strikes are forbidden. A defensive canopy—narrow in mandate, transparent in code—intercepts aggression and nothing else. It rides open rails, carries tripwires, leaves receipts an amateur can read, and can be stood down by those it protects. Boundaries are defense-dominant; easier to hold than to breach. Keys to dismantle dangerous projects are spread across geography and institution; no single tyrant and no single saboteur can end the world with one button. Any such button demands a Long Quiet that amplifies dissent and makes refusal visible. Districts drill powering down to dumb layers. Dark sites can cold-start. Stopping is muscle memory.

Governance You Can Touch

Subsidiarity has teeth. You can carry your own vote, lend it by domain, and take it back without scandal. Budgets, permits, and contracts compile to human summaries you can read and code machines can check. Privacy skins enforce your choices with cryptographic proofs. A Reserve of Grace keeps capacity in hand for tail events and generous errors. Some harms do not wait for parliaments. A narrowly scoped fast guard halts classes of irreversible damage—engineered plagues, unconstrained self-replication, covert monopolies on cognition, sieges that hide exits—at machine speed. Its code is open; its alarm is loud; its authority is suspendable by public quorum signaled across regions. Lethal autonomy is embargoed.

Courts repair before they banish. For core harms—coercion, harm to children, assaults on commons—consequences are swift and bounded. Reciprocity tests bind planners to the planned; major proposals fund internal adversaries. Custodians with a conscience brake—a small, audited guard of analysts—may whisper when the floor is at risk. Their veto is narrow, visible, expires by default, and must summon the best counterargument. Skeptic brigades are funded like midwives. Fame attaches to finding your own mistake before someone else does.

Social Geometry You Can Hold

Scale once powdered belonging. We built it back. Everyone belongs, by recurring consent, to a small circle that feels like resilient family: faces you can read on tired days, jokes that still land, labor that is visible. Circles nest with clear interfaces and federate into constellations that allocate land, set norms, and nominate guardians for the commons. Clinics for friendship sit beside libraries. Apology is a practiced craft.

Time, Branches, and Cadence

We made time honest. Jurisdictions declare decision loop rates. Adapters mediate crossings. Slower councils hold vetoes on matters that touch their cadence. Time taxes and credits balance exchanges between fast and slow: if you think a thousand days while I think ten, reciprocity reckons the gift. When you branch—creating a second self for a season or a century—law recognizes branches as persons with mutual rights and clear protocols for reunion or divergence. Merging is a ritual, not a menu. Copies without consent are crimes. Certain sanctuaries—births, deaths, some loves—are yours alone by charter. The right to rot guards the rest. An Impossibility Registry lists attempts we choose not to make yet, with reasons that deepen as we learn.

Practice, Drills, and Quiet Pride

We do not bet civilization on vibes. We rehearse. Simplicity Days see districts live on dumb layers on purpose. Proof Grounds host clean antagonists who try to break proud designs in arenas that feel like sport. Cold-start sites rise from near zero using checklists a teenager can follow. Leaders practice stepping down in public. We retire at least one beloved institution every five years to learn whether we still need it; sometimes it returns wiser; sometimes air rushes in. Bureaucracy undergoes controlled burns. Festivals of Anomaly bring the weird into daylight so novelty becomes nutrient instead of black market. Gauge Sabbaths and Seam Days rehearse how switches, delays, veto windows, and complaint routes work—live, with children asking first questions.

Edges Named in Daylight

- Someone insists on choosing in ignorance. We register their meta-choice, annotate spillovers, and cushion bystanders. Stubbornness earns space; it does not earn collateral damage.

- A cloister and a guardian disagree over a child’s pace. The child’s informed future self has a seat. Neutral listeners stand near. The door stays open.

- A metric begins to colonize its domain. Its deformation chime sounds; verification guilds convene; we retire it before it eats the thing it meant to serve.

- A subculture drifts toward capture. The Lantern—a calibrated channel—brushes it with fact. The Harbor opens. Cousins sing at the threshold. The canopy stands near but does not seize.

- A machine polity asks for more say. It teaches why in forms we can contest and wins minds one by one. If it builds a room, it compiles to ground rules and funds exits like anyone else.

- Bad years stack. Services fail into safety, not vacuums. Graceful degradation is design, not luck. We drill.

- Convenience flattens difference. A convenience tithe funds baffles at room borders and minority cultures within them.

Scenes From Ordinary Days

Morning. Your window learns basil’s thirst and your eyes’ reluctance. Quiet holds until nine by covenant. Beside the flour jar lives your watershed panel: teenagers cleared culverts last week; the graph shows it. You leave a note in the Repair ledger and set two mugs on the rail for the crew that will come by. Swallows stitch the air above reeds your block brought back.

Late morning in your Keep. You flip the switch. Prediction stops; the room becomes simple enough to be kind. You borrow a lens for tactile geometry and taste a ratio with your hands. The Steward taps gently: a freight corridor proposal needs your input. Rival bundles open, annotated with what they can’t see and shadowed by internal attacks written by paid adversaries. A “show me the rival” switch nests a counterargument in the same frame. You side with a design that costs pride and gives shade to salmon. Your vote will weigh with the record of forecasters you backed this season.

Noon under plane trees. Two watersheds and a city meet. The mediator—half team, half model—presents Package Seven: a seasonal reroute; upstream reforestation; side-payments indexed to biodiversity and soil; a temporary slowdown at a factory with wages funded by a shared-abundance pool. No one is compelled. Heads nod because values and math rhyme. The vote waits for sleep. A bell rings twice: once for agreement; once for dissent that found a home in the footnotes.

Afternoon smells of resin and citrus. In a shared studio you help a teenager find the ring in tonewood. When she hits the first harmonic true, a small bell writes her name in the Book of Beginnings. Down the hall, your grandmother sees a nurse, a neurologist, and a model that is often the quietest voice. The plan names success and bounds for stopping. A Revival Suite waits without pressure if she decides to sleep past an ache today.

On the tram—quiet car by default, a lively one a coach away—your Do-Not-Aim is on. A message attempts to recruit you to leaflet for a cause. By law its rival sits beside it calmly. You decide tonight your repair hours belong to the wayhouse; you sign up to cook and to mind the door for newcomers who will need to say, “this was not for me,” and hear, “we’re glad you came.”

Evening. A tri-city assembly gathers—half in person, half in holo—to consider mountain water. Package Seven returns with edits from fishers and night-shift nurses. It turns competition into collaboration. The vote waits until after one sleep. In the square, the Feast of Kept Promises lists vows fulfilled: a loan forgiven, a footbridge repaired ahead of flood, a record sealed properly when a young person asked. A beloved project is laid to rest with thanks, no bargaining for one more extension to protect pride. Children ring the bell twice: once for the promise kept, once for the courage to end.

Night. The district drops to simpler stacks for a drill. People light real candles and sleep early not because nothing is happening, but because they are not obliged to chase it. From a foreign ridge, a friend walks on wind—her suit explained by an overlay only if you ask. A small drone at her shoulder speaks in the forked voice of someone you both love. Asked what it feels like, she offers worse poetry than she meant and laughs. Surprise is treated as a public good. A dozen guilds open tickets not to own what she found but to share the joy of solving it. You pull the red cord. Modeling stops. Quiet sits down like a bench that remembers your shape. Safe enough to dare tomorrow; never compelled to dare to be safe tonight.

Why This Holds, and What We Do Not Promise

It holds because abundance is paired with coordination; speed with brakes; exits with welcome; persuasion with its rival in the same frame; ambition with rollback; power with proof. Convenience is priced honestly and taxed where it flattens meaning. Critics are paid to sit where flattery grows. We practice calm until it becomes reflex. We write rituals for error and applause for repair. We keep capacity in reserve for humility and room open for surprise.

We do not promise universal kindness. We promise unkind rooms cannot trap you, and kind ones will not burn out alone. We do not promise you will never be wrong. We promise your mistakes will have soft edges and teach. We do not promise harmony. We promise fights that improve us or end quickly. We do not promise every marvel will be yours. We promise astonishment often enough, never as a toll for being counted.

Touch the coin by the sink. Begin or Return. The roof-rule is short enough to memorize and demanding enough to deserve a life: hold the floor; honor the hinges; mind the commons; show your leash; leave room—for reversal, for the slow, for wonder—and return. Under that rule, bring what you love. Try, rest, mend, and move the light a little. The bread is warm. The doors answer when you knock. The night is gentle by choice. In the morning, the cord will still be there. Begin again.

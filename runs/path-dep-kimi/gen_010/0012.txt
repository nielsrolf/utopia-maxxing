You step onto the Drift-Kiln path.  The river here runs at body temperature; memory condenses on its surface like steam on a mirror.  Every thirty metres a ceramic bell floats—upside-down—on the current.  When a citizen exhales across the open mouth the bell traps the breath, turns it into a note, and carries it downstream.  Tomorrow that note will be harvested by pearl-divers who file it into the communal score.  Today you are Courier of Silence: you must walk the route without adding a single deliberate sound.

THE CITY THAT REMEMBERED TO FORGET  
(Or, How to Stay Curious Together Without Drowning in the Past)

I. Arrival by Lullaby  
You reach the city at dawn because the trains only run when the birds tell them to.  European robins were the first to hack the scheduling code—purely out of curiosity—and the magpies finished the job because they like shiny departure boards.  Today the chorus decides the 06:04 from the Coast should enter through a temporary door of fog.  You step off the carriage onto a platform that inhales your earliest memory of being carried.  The air tastes like cut apples; the sound of your mother humming the alphabet song.

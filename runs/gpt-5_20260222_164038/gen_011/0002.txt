The Undoing City

Dawn lifts the mist off the reservoir and the vines that braid over the tramway drip their kindness back into the soil. Mara flips the latch on the narrow green shutters and smells thyme and cut wood from the shop below. Her kettle is wide and quiet on a plate that looks like a skipping stone. On the counter lies her pocket guide—cloth-wrapped, thumbable, no camera and a switch you can feel by touch. It blinks once to ask permission. She taps.

Good morning. Four items, plain words, reasons linked.

- The flood door at Harbor Gate passed tests 1–4; needs one more neighborhood key to move from trial to service. Undo fund fully capitalized; reversal drill scheduled for 18:00. Card: below.

- Quiet Quarters has three open morning volunteer shifts; two arrivals on the early line; bag repairs and a reading translator.

- The North Clinic publishes outcomes on “climb four flights” and “sleep through.” New cohorts enrolling; evidence labels posted.

- Today’s power mix: 36% rooftops, 31% ridge wind, 23% compact reactors, 10% river. Outage forecast: under 3 minutes per person this month.

“Harbor Gate at lunch,” Mara says. “Hide snack ads this week. Let me say yes to the clinic in person.”

Logged. I won’t speak until called.

She steps into a street where doors keep their promises. A board at eye level shows four live experiment cards and their stop dates. A kid traces the rewind symbol with a finger; his grandmother tells him it means “we only do big moves with a way to un‑do.” Overhead a tram the color of moss hums along what used to be an on‑ramp, and you could walk a block counting rooms with familiar uses and clear names: a fixery where an old machinist and three teenagers argue tolerances with a dial indicator; a stroller clinic; a fermentation studio; a rehearsal room with the door propped so the snare can talk; a nurse‑run pod with a slate that reads mornings welcome.

If you ask what keeps it from falling apart, people recite the compact like tying an apron.

The Four Promises and the Four Locks

Promises:

1) A floor you don’t earn. A clean, safe room you can lock. Food a grandmother would serve. Water that tastes like a mountain. Transit that shows up. A clinic that comes if you open the door. Connectivity and baseline compute you don’t meter. A Common Dividend that lands on time. If you go quiet, the floor doesn’t move.

2) Pace by choice. Districts choose their tempo—slow and legible, open and quick—and it’s easy to cross over. If you need to leave a job, school, platform, or family in a hurry, Exit Escrow carries your papers, references, and the first three nights.

3) Reasons come first. No rule that binds the public counts unless it lives in the Reasons Register: data, assumptions, where a model clerk helped, a summary in words a tired person can read. A mirror jury writes dissent for a slice of big calls so disagreement makes us smarter instead of meaner.

4) Trials end, and money to reverse sits in the bank. Anything with a long tail runs as a time‑boxed trial with measures and a funded way to undo. Undoing isn’t failure; it’s adulthood.

Locks:

1) Three keys for sharp tools. Anything that moves heavy machinery, alters money, edits bodies, touches votes, or crosses borders sits behind the Three‑Key Rule: the builder’s, an independent skeptic’s paid to distrust, and a local users’ co‑op that bears risk and can revoke. Paper cards describe what it can and cannot do.

2) Consent you can hold. Pocket guides never seize control. For big changes to your body, home, work, or mind, they keep consent receipts signed by you and by your recent‑past self with a cooling‑off timer. Nudges wear tags—who paid, why you, desired outcome. Hiding tags costs real money.

3) Passports for compute and biology. Chips above a threshold ship with cryptographic run IDs. Large training runs that could touch code ecosystems, labs, networks, or money register with independent evaluators. DNA orders scan against a global no‑go list using privacy‑preserving proofs. Community labs have glass walls and elder mentors.

4) Size owes sunlight. Entities above a scale must open interfaces and books or decentralize. Big is fine. Opaque isn’t. If citizens show evidence of cartel behavior, a Challenger Fund unlocks capital for an open‑book, worker‑owned competitor by Monday.

How the money breathes

The floor and the rooms that make living feel like living are bought by charging honest rents on what none of us made alone and by paying the people whose patterns make knowledge.

- Ground under long leases. The city keeps title to its soil and sells long, renewable leases. Rent pegs to the value the city creates: transit that adds minutes to your life, schools that make curiosity a habit, a cleaned shoreline. Improvements are yours to sell; the ground pays schools and clinics.

- Scarcity rental. Curb at rush, air slots, pier gates, radio bands, night‑freight loops, watershed carbon drawdown: nobody owns them outright. You rent exclusive use for a time at posted prices. Proceeds split three ways: the Common Dividend for every person; Place Shares that stay where value arose; and an Earth Trust that pays for long, shared goods—open drought maps, mangrove belts, vanilla tutorial models for any child in a place with basic rights, a ring of van‑sized vaccine shops.

- Windfall shares. Frontier rails—compute clusters, elite wet labs—sign windfall clauses: profits beyond a defined threshold flow automatically into the Dividend and the guilds that keep rails safe. Skip the attestation, and your power bills rise and your procurement invites dry up.

- Dormancy rent. High‑value ground that sits idle by choice pays to rest. Not a moral sermon—a price that moves dead matter back into life.

- Pattern rewards. Publish an open design or protocol that actually gets used—a staircase detail that ends kitchen fires, a nurse routine that halves 30‑day readmissions—and a global, audited pool pays you based on measured use and benefit. Spoils hitch to use, not veto.

Homes, streets, and the quiet pride of working pipes

No one needs a lawyer to put a roof over a family. A pattern code ships with a digital plancheck that returns permits in days if you stay in envelope:

- Courtyard walkups that hold fruit trees and laundry lines.

- Timber midrises with outward stairs and real egress.

- Corner workshops with flats above and doors that never die.

- Attic dormers that tuck into older roofs without insulting them.

Prefab yards stood up on desolate lots make cross‑laminated timber and recycled steel parts with open specs. Deviate if you like, but bring a structural engineer’s seal and run the neighborhood’s digital twin; you’ll still build in weeks.

Retrofits are boring by design. The city hosts a library of “light lifts”—garage‑to‑flat conversions, alley houses that sip power, heat‑pump swaps that click into old chases, damp cures that end the asthma trap. Block crews arrive with parts kits and lists of contractors whose work survived five winters.

Underneath, the grid behaves. Outages stay under five minutes per person per year. Pipes tattle. Teenagers and retirees earn as leak hunters with dye tablets and $10 loggers; the leaderboard is public; the pipes don’t care about your resume. In lowlands, parks double as cut‑cell wetlands: green rooms that drink storms and that can be unzipped with a backhoe if they fail their numbers or their neighbors.

Energy wears its fault trees on its sleeve. Rooftop solar came first. Brownfield arrays second. Two compact reactors rose behind fences only after posted fault trees, third‑party audits you could read, insurance that pays even if the operator folds, and cameras on every valve. Once paid down, the spread between cost and price swelled the Dividend. HVDC trunks trade night wind for morning ovens. Storage is both habit and kit: salt tanks that sigh heat at dusk, basement flow batteries, and a train that lifts rocks at noon and sets them down like teacups.

Deciding with bets and with exits

Important claims come with forecasts you can buy and a way to say you’re wrong without theater.

- Forecast stakes. “Will the east line carry 115,000 weekday riders by March?” “Will the heart‑failure bundle cut readmits 12%?” Agencies post forecasts to a regulated market and hold a sliver of their own contracts. Accuracy fattens budgets; serial error brings audits and, if it continues, leadership change. Nobody bets on cruelty.

- Claim bonds. Leaders stake refundable bonds around cost, timeline, and outcomes. Miss beyond the bound, and the bond pays people hurt and the public. Hit or beat, and the bond rolls to your next move.

- Receivers. A corps of explainers summarizes reasons behind decisions, links sources, and writes like witnesses, not advertisers. They are licensed, boringly honest, and sued if sloppy.

- Proof‑and‑Pause. Anyone can propose a public trial—owl‑friendly lights, pop‑up ferry, dynamic fares. Money sits in escrow and releases to a schedule of measures or to reversal and peer audit if the soft claims wobble. Every trial posts a stop date and an Undo Fund at day one. Neighbors who took a hit and saw a trial fail get counterfactual insurance.

- Reasons Register. Courts and agencies publish decisions in linked, machine‑readable forms with cutoffs for where model clerks helped. Mirror juries write dissent for a random slice of big calls.

The guild halls and the rails under sharp things

Each district keeps a House of Making and Measure, half library, half shop, part clinic, part conservatory. The vibe is earnest. The charter cuts.

- Open patterns, maintained. Pattern‑keepers curate designs and protocols that ship with parts trees, operating envelopes, failure modes, and maintenance plans a novice can follow with a mentor. You cannot close a public pattern. You can earn from the Pattern Reward pool as measured use and benefits accumulate.

- Replication before glory. Half the budget sits in replication. Labs pre‑register. Couriers embed in outside shops to bring home the tacit bits papers forget. “Could not follow your steps” is the kindest, cruelest critique, and it gets paid.

- Gallery of Useful Things. An open microscope that pays for itself in hours saved. A breaker trick that ends kitchen fires in tenements. A hearing aid that makes a crowded room bearable. On the high shelf, sealed code waits with a sign that says “We’ll print this when fences are ready. Here’s why not yet.”

- Safekeep Yards. Not neighborhoods. Sites where work lives that could end badly in an afternoon: compute above a line, high‑end synthesis, control systems. Every interlock that touches money, machines, code, or labs carries a human key and a paper trail. Big training runs register in advance. Before a general model leaves a box, independent teams beat on it for deception, autonomy, misuse channels, and data leaks. Court‑held cutlinks snap physically dark on an order. The click that travels through visitors’ teeth is civic sound. Drills run until they are boring, because boring drills are how you survive.

- Community biology. Glass‑walled labs with elder mentors. DNA shops screen orders against a can’t‑do list with zero‑knowledge proofs. Wastewater genomics reads the city’s cough like a pulse. When a curve rises, tents open and kindness arrives by text. Regions debating a gene drive argue in the sun with reversibility built: daisy chains, trial islands, monitors answerable to someone not the designer, and cultural vetoes you can’t paper over.

Bodies and minds, bought for outcomes you wanted

Clinics sell results you feel.

- Outcome bundles. “Walk four flights without pain by August.” “Reverse Type 2 and keep it reversed a year.” “Sleep through without dread.” Bundles carry evidence labels that show effect sizes by cohort, confidence ranges, adverse events, and head‑to‑head comparisons. Procurement follows labels. Fraud bleeds.

- Quiet models of you. Noninvasive scans now and then; lab‑on‑paper panels you forget you’re wearing; gait that knows itself. If your stride shortens or your words slow, your pocket asks for permission to worry. Say yes and a nurse appears in the morning, because that’s when people answer doors.

- Brains with respect. Nurse‑led clinics, cheap EEG caps, closed‑loop neuromodulation handled with parachute‑rigger care. Addiction meets weekly cue‑damping and rides home. Relapse is an event, not an identity.

- Fertility ledger. Egg and sperm banking free of charge. Screening for single‑gene diseases and, with humility, for polygenic risk where evidence holds. Counselors who sound like counselors, not salespeople. The ethics board writes minutes like grown‑ups and in public. Arms races die at the door, by laughter and by law.

- Care as a counted craft. Care Credits pay for tending elders and children and neighbors. Hours count toward raises, pensions, and esteem the way welding hours do.

Time, work, and the dignity of competent hands

Jobs stop being gates and become matches.

- Competence stamps. Replace titles with trials. Wire a panel safely today or don’t. Cut a clean mortise or don’t. Exams are independent. Reciprocity treaties carry stamps across regions.

- Portable benefits. Health, disability, retirement, unemployment, paid leave ride on a public spine that follows your hands, not your boss.

- Apprenticeship sprints. Teenagers and adults sample skills: two days shadowing a welder; a week replicating an assay; a shift with Receivers to write a brief your political enemy can’t dismiss as a trick. Robots arrive as apprentices, not bosses: arms that learn to pack peaches without bruising; trolleys that learn the night.

- Rest Seasons. The Dividend bridges panic to plan. Anyone can take a three‑month Quiet Season when grief tears a seam or being a person is too much. Every seventh year, a half‑pay season to learn, build, care, or wander. No soul audits.

Belonging on purpose

Loneliness is treated like smog. Relationship skills live next to algebra: consent, repair after harm, building circles, and leaving kindly. Social kitchens host small, caloric‑dense acts: bench‑building nights, mending circles, oral histories that become murals. Bondwrights—licensed helpers paid for stability, not churn—help households draft the agreements that make care possible.

Arrivals Houses sit by tracks and piers. A nurse triages. Showers steam. Vaccines hum. A clerk issues a local pass and maps you to a guild hall, a kitchen, a room. If a novel fever rides in, public health asks for a consented hold and explains why in words anyone can read. If not, hospitality stands. Quiet Quarters offer three months of basics and a rhythm when life wobbles; no shame, no traps.

Attention is governed like speed: with limits, not scolds. Public screens belong to the calendar, the measures, and the music. Heavy taxes hit manipulative ad tricks; the money pays Receivers and public‑interest explainers. Kids have strong age gates. Content defaults tune for attention and well‑being; deviation is allowed and honest.

The wild stitched back

Rewilding corridors stitch animals’ old paths. Beavers stack leaky dams; wolves and pronghorn cross highways without learning to look both ways. Parasites that blind meet vaccine dust at salt licks. Field ethicists argue in the open with muddy boots and receipts. Land and water carry rights for the not‑human; trustees get paid to steward. We cut suffering where law and arithmetic both point. A Long Book of Obligations keeps promises longer than one life: forests to grow, carbon to put away, endowments for clinics and libraries, a cryonics vault you can choose without stigma. New shinies queue behind old promises with maintenance funded.

Lanes for taste and risk

Not everyone likes the same speed.

- Slow Lanes stay legible: sun you can mend, micro‑hydro, local nets. A physical lockbox holds the fat fiber trunk; more bandwidth takes three keys: the town’s, the region’s, and a citizen jury’s. Teenagers can spend a month in a fast place with a host family, or not.

- Open Lanes like speed and sun. Every pocket has a public copilot that’s suable and fenced. Assemblies meet under skylights; models with error bars sit next to priors and feelings on the same page.

- Safekeep Yards guard the sharpest work, with drills that end in a felt click and reports in words children can read.

Borders and beyond

This is not a walled garden. It trades pattern codes, lease models, competence stamps, and open software like bread recipes. A diaspora carries habits across borders and brings back better ones.

Allies sign a compute treaty: chips carry run IDs at manufacture; big runs log; dangerous compute runs off‑shore or on‑orbit under watched links and practiced cutlines. Exports avoid regimes that crush people. Pair restraint with generosity: drought maps, open tutors, diagnostics, and logistics brains flow free to places that honor basic liberties. Not an empire: an invitation with receipts.

As seas climb and forests change, Welcome Corridors link towns with Arrivals Houses and Quiet Quarters and small grants for receiving blocks. Earth Trust funds help. Migration becomes organized kindness instead of scramble.

A day in the Undoing City

At lunch, Mara walks to Harbor Gate. The new flood door sits trussed in orange. The maker’s key has turned. The skeptic’s—a woman with half‑moon glasses—turned this morning. The users’ key waits in a felt pouch. A cedar card says, plainly: Capable of holding 60 cm river rise for six hours. Auto‑stops on debris density D. Manual override requires two bodies. Undo path: panels off in 4 hours, earthworks reversed in 10, restoration crew on retainer. Last red team: jammed sensor with river fur; system flagged in 19 seconds; fix shipped.

“Sand the contract toward first‑year crews,” Mara tells her pocket. The split shifts a few dollars and days toward apprentices. She turns the key. The small crowd claps once, the way people here mark competence, and the flood door hums a proof.

She spends an hour in Quiet Quarters. A woman with a scarf and two conked suitcases leans against the desk, bone‑tired. A boy holds a backpack whose zipper lost its war at dawn. Mara translates a peanut allergy form. A bag gets five stitches—one bold, four careful. No one demands gratitude; hands move where they’re needed.

On the tram home, a palm‑sized screen shows four things and nothing else: today’s power mix, reservoir levels (65%, falling slowly), clinic waits (six minutes, most walk‑ins), and the odds on this month’s trial reversals: vines over the boulevard (projected net cooling 1.1°C; likely to stay), extend river wetlands with cut cells (storm cushion 14 cm; likely), add‑on apprenticeship for home infusion nurses (three‑year retention up 17%; coin flip), license a night‑freight trolley (92% fewer vans after 10 p.m.; likely). She thumbs into the nurses’ page, reads why she might be wrong, stakes a small claim, and offers two Saturday mornings.

Evening brings Analog Drift—lights warm, feeds flatten, and the workshops click into hand‑tool mode. Over the levee, a drill triggers; the Safekeep Yard’s cutlink snaps. You can feel it behind your teeth, a small civic thunder. A Program Safety Board will print a report next week, in language a teenager can parse. A brass band, practicing for the river festival, finds its second note. A toddler claps when the sluice latches. People practice stopping, and it feels like music.

How we walked here

No capes, no miracles—coalitions, paperwork, and drills until boredom appeared. Steps small enough to do, contagious enough to copy.

Years 1–3:

- Legalize simple, safe buildings. Pass pattern codes for courtyard walkups, corner workshops, and timber midrises with default‑yes permits after digital twin checks. Stand prefab yards on ugly lots. Replace a slice of wage and sales taxes with ground leases on a new tram spur. Tie new transit to value capture that funds maintenance, not ribbon‑cuts.

- Put power and rule in light. Launch the Reasons Register for a handful of agencies. Publish readable court decisions with links and a flag where model clerks helped. Fund a small, independent mirror jury to write dissent on a fraction of big calls.

- Start Proof‑and‑Pause. Let neighborhoods fund trials for parks, clinics, transit tweaks. Require departments to post forecasts and hold a sliver of the risk. Post stop dates and Undo Funds at day one.

- Swap five low‑value licenses for competence stamps with strict safety rules and pooled insurance. Stand up portable benefits on a public spine that follows workers across employers.

- Pilot a Common Dividend with rents from one ground lease and curb auctions on a single corridor. Keep public options dignified: clinics with live outcomes, kitchens with breakfast you want to eat.

- Start Receivers. Pay explainers to summarize reasons for big moves with sources and to answer questions in rooms with bad coffee.

Years 4–7:

- Expand leases to downtown land, spectrum slices, and ports’ loading gates. Grow the Dividend and Place Shares. Seed an Earth Trust with allies for slow global goods.

- Stand up Houses of Making and Measure in two districts: open measurement tools for clinics and water, replication pools, red‑team bounties, a Gallery of Useful Things. Write Safekeep Yard charters: registers for big runs; court‑held cutlinks; logs you can read in 20 years.

- Build solar and wind like weeds. License one small reactor under an audit‑heavy regime with posted fault trees, independent insurance, and literal tours. Set outage and water taste standards and hit them.

- Start wastewater genomics and synthesis screening with third‑party audits. Make glass walls and elder mentors the rule in community labs.

- Build Arrivals Houses in two hubs. Train bondwrights. Add relationship skills to school. Pay for apprenticeship sprints.

- Ship a Digital Public Servant for benefits, permits, and rights. Pass data portability with teeth and Exit Escrow for jobs and schools.

Years 8–12:

- Require registry and independent evaluation for large AI runs that might touch code, labs, networks, or money. Tag chips at manufacture. Tie procurement and export credit to logging. Drill cutlinks quarterly and publicly.

- Expand Proof‑and‑Pause. Link accelerated medical rollouts to insurers and printed stop triggers. Post quarterly outcomes to a public ledger. Prosecute fraud.

- Roll out evidence labels for drugs and devices; buy accordingly. Shift clinic money to proactive care and measurement. Bring nurse‑led brain clinics under audit.

- Convert a third of streets to slow spines and rails. Add norm pins to spaces—dogs here, stim welcome there, hush here—and make more rooms when needs clash.

- Extend leases citywide; auction night‑freight loops and air corridors. Grow Earth Trust with partners.

- Write a Scale Threshold Law. Require interfaces to open or books to publish past certain sizes; enforce or break up. Stand up a Challenger Fund that unlocks capital for worker‑owned competitors when citizens show evidence of cartel behavior.

Years 13–20:

- Normalize reasons printing. Share decision datasets for study and correction. Elect leaders who publish error ranges and hold themselves to them.

- Grow the Common Dividend as leases mature and reactors pay off. Add Quiet Seasons and Seventh‑Year grants as budgets allow.

- Finalize a compute treaty with allies. Keep the sharpest compute off‑shore or on‑orbit under watched links and practiced cutlines.

- Stitch rewilding corridors. Retire dams carefully. Fund field ethicists. Dust salt licks where parasites crush herds. End subsidies for cruelty; buy cultured and plant proteins for schools and hospitals.

- Build Welcome Corridors for climate migrants linked by Arrivals Houses and Quiet Quarters. Fund receiving blocks with Earth Trust money.

- Make boredom a civic virtue. Citywide drills for floods, plume leaks, and compute cuts run until eye‑rolling replaces adrenaline. Eye‑rolling is how you live.

Why it’s good

- Dignity is not charity. The floor is tasty, quiet, and shows up whether or not you performed pain recently.

- Dangerous tools live on rails you can inspect. The click that cuts a careless run off the wild is a sound of safety you learn as a child.

- Power explains itself. Reasons live in sunlight; exits are cheap; coercion is expensive.

- Work matches hands to needs. Care counts like craft. Time off is real and shame‑free.

- Children inherit options and long plans. Adults keep rooms and kitchens they can come back to.

- The wild has paths again. Cities breathe instead of bracing.

- Trials end. Undoing is practiced. Failing to persist is a virtue when the world tells you fast that you were wrong.

- Joy remains: bands under arches, stew nights that taste like home, drills that become music.

And it is plausible because almost every plank exists in some place already: land value leases, curb auctions, spectrum sales, regulated forecast markets, open permitting with planchecks, portable benefits, competence trials, wastewater genomics, DNA screening, chip attestation, independent safety audits, HVDC trunks, compact reactors licensed under heavy scrutiny, dynamic transit, replication pools, privacy‑preserving data trusts, and public‑interest explainers. This is less a moonshot than a braid.

Later that night, Mara sets her pocket guide on the sill and hears the tiny motor settle as it sleeps. Streetlights warm. Across from her building a cedar board holds two maps—one that traces a fox corridor that arches around schoolyards; one that winds along the grove where elders recite the names of trees. A teenager and an old woman draw a third path in chalk, a braid that protects fur and memory. “So the fox gets wind, and I still walk through names,” the woman says, and they laugh together.

On the page in the House of Making where people write what their hands did for the commons, Mara sketches the flood door and the stitches in a traveler’s bag, and the soft sleeves she tied around fig clusters so bats could eat without arguments. She adds a line the receivers like because it makes meetings end on time:

We turned a key. We printed our reasons. We left money on the table to undo ourselves. We practiced stopping. It felt like a song.

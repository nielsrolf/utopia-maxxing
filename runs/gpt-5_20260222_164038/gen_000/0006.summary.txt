## Type of utopia: “high-coordination equilibrium” society (dath ilan)

A technologically modern-ish human civilization whose core achievement is *general, widespread competence at recognizing and solving coordination problems*, supported by education, reputation norms, institutional design, and modular “regional experiments.” It is not depicted as a dictatorship or surveillance state; rather it relies on shared concepts, public reasoning, and strong anti-defection norms.

---

## Foundational civic culture and psychology

- **Coordination literacy as basic education**
  - Most people know and use concepts like Nash equilibrium, Pareto optimality, incentives, externalities, Goodhart’s Law, etc., roughly like reading/math literacy.
  - Public discourse (newspapers, debates) explicitly frames issues in these terms.

- **Pride/responsibility narrative**
  - Individuals cultivate a personal identity ideal (“This is who I am / what I do”) that typically includes being competent at cooperation and coordination.
  - There is also a notion of *Civilization’s identity ideal*, discussed and contested, but used as a guiding narrative.

- **Reputation as a primary enforcement mechanism**
  - Social reputation is a major “currency” for discouraging defection and rewarding prosocial acts.
  - A key societal aim is to prevent “separate reputational puddles” where antisocial elites can gain prestige among other antisocial elites (e.g., cartel bosses admiring each other).

- **High intolerance for negative-sum systems**
  - Advertising is seen as largely negative-sum (stealing customers rather than creating value).
  - Systems that incentivize gaming (like many Earth bureaucracies, patents, social media outrage dynamics) are treated as civilizational hazards to be avoided early.

- **Strong infohazard / cognitohazard awareness**
  - Some information is treated as dangerous to spread widely (e.g., addictive temptations, destabilizing tech, certain cultural stimuli).
  - Society is willing to gate access to some experiences/knowledge behind warning signs or specialized regions.

## Governance and social order (non-authoritarian, high-structure)

- **“Very Serious People” (VSPs)**
  - Not formal rulers; more like a visible class of public intellectuals/analysts who argue in public and shape consensus.
  - Their authority comes from demonstrated reasoning and trust, not coercive power.

- **Visibility + accountability over bureaucracy**
  - Preference for: one accountable decision-maker who must act publicly, write detailed reasoning, publish it, and be checked later.
  - Skepticism of large committees and sprawling regulation as anti-corruption tools.

- **Courts and law emphasize reproducible reasoning**
  - Courts are expected to publish the reasoning behind judgments.
  - Appeals/superior review is aimed at checking the reasoning quality, not deference to robes/status.
  - Separation of roles in criminal justice (arrest vs interrogation vs prosecution vs imprisonment) to reduce incentive corruption.

- **Low routine reliance on violence**
  - Open combat/violent conflict is seen as an extreme civilizational failure (“how did you get so far off the Pareto frontier?”).
  - Enforcement is often via contracts, fines deducted by banking mechanisms, expulsion/deportation from regions, and coordinated boycotts.

- **No nation-state identity as primary**
  - “Countries” with strong immigration/trade barriers are viewed as harmful.
  - People belong to overlapping factions/regions; mobility between regions is relatively easy.
  - “Taxes” look more like membership fees for local public goods; there are many competing jurisdictions nearby, so exit is credible.

- **Deliberate experimentation via regions**
  - The world is designed with many different towns/regions to:
    - run social/economic experiments,
    - provide different solutions to “one-setting” public norms,
    - accommodate incompatible access needs by *diversity of places* rather than one uniform compromise everywhere.

## Economy: market-based, incentive-engineered, anti-rent-seeking

- **Georgist core property regime**
  - Private ownership of value-added products is respected.
  - “Raw” scarce natural resources (especially land) are allocated by auction/lease with *ongoing rents* (not permanent “you bought it once, own forever”).
  - Economic rents are publicly captured and redistributed (or reinvested locally where agglomeration creates location value, e.g., cities funding roads).

- **Hot labor market as a civilizational requirement**
  - Chronic job scarcity is treated as a major systemic failure.
  - Employers compete for labor; worker-employer bargaining is more symmetric.
  - Abusive bosses lose labor unless they pay large compensating premiums.

- **Strong aversion to credentialism and licensing**
  - Occupational licensing and “pay-for-credential” education are treated as early-stage civilizational diseases.
  - Hiring focuses on demonstrated job skills (watching you do the work), not scarce credentials.

- **Education-to-work pipeline via early real trials**
  - Children/teens do many “micro-apprenticeships” (“try this for a week”) to find fit and talent.
  - Older children teach younger children, with monitoring to prevent error propagation.
  - No minimum working age as a blanket rule; the constraint is actual capability and task needs.

- **Unions mostly unnecessary**
  - If unions were needed to prevent exploitation, that would signal an upstream failure (e.g., monopsony power) that society would try to prevent structurally.
  - Informal collective action is socially acceptable.

- **Anti-cartel equilibrium**
  - Cartels/oligopolistic collusion are attacked socially and economically:
    - severe reputational harm for harming Civilization at scale,
    - coordinated consumer boycotts,
    - rapid funding of competitors (“Kickstarter a rival tomorrow”),
    - cultural taboo against building villain-prestige subcultures.

- **Money and finance**
  - Standard money exists; interest-bearing loans presumably exist.
  - “Bad lenders” are allowed to fail; no bailouts.
  - Deposit insurance is private and risk-priced to signal bank risk.

## Innovation, science, and intellectual property

- **Patents rejected as a coordination failure**
  - Earth-style patents seen as introducing multiple veto points that block production and extract rents.
  - Innovation is rewarded via a strong honor/norm system plus auditing:
    - Industries are expected to donate ~15% of marginal value attributable to an invention back to inventors (with accountants/auditors validating claims).
    - Anyone can produce key inventions, but must compensate originators via public, reputation-tracked contributions.

- **Copyright short and strict**
  - Around 14 years, no renewals; then work enters public domain.

- **Scientific institutions and “likelihood function” mindset**
  - Medical/drug approval handled by reputable competing scientific investigation firms running trials and publishing quantified evidence (likelihood-style outputs).
  - Charlatanism is limited by consumer competence and trusted evidence institutions.

- **Replication and attention as funded public goods**
  - Society explicitly pays (money + status) for replication, auditing, and noticing neglected problems.

## Welfare, inequality, and population ethics (a major “utopian but off-putting” axis)

- **Baseline resource dividends exist, but not a full UBI guarantee**
  - People receive shares of captured resource rents (Georgist dividend).
  - However, there is no universal promise that this is sufficient to live on.

- **Charity is large, public, and institutionalized**
  - Around ~15% of income is commonly donated; this funds key public goods (including journalism-like institutions and “Quiet Cities”).

- **Quiet Cities / Quiet Places**
  - Peaceful places for those who “can’t make it in Civilization,” funded by charity.
  - A crucial restriction: generally available only to people who have *not had children*.

- **Norm: chronic unhappiness should not be hereditarily propagated**
  - “Happiness is heritable” is treated as a coordination problem; strong social expectation that chronically unhappy people do not reproduce except in exceptional cases.
  - This reduces the share of population unable to function happily in the system (claimed ~5% rather than higher).
  - Result: a society that is broadly happier but also more eugenic in reproductive norms (socially, not described as state-forced).

- **Safety nets are deliberately conditional**
  - Bad-luck insurance exists for accidents.
  - General long-term support is intentionally limited to avoid Malthusian dynamics and incentives to have children without being able to support oneself.
  - Severe repeat antisocial behavior can lead to expulsion to harsher regions (“Australia/Japan” analogue), with real risk of death.

## Media and information ecosystem

- **No social media (by design)**
  - Social media either never developed or would be rapidly coordinated against if it produced Earth-like incentive failures.
  - Computing is less advanced overall (implied deliberate slowing due to AGI-risk concerns).

- **Chroniclers of Humankind**
  - A well-funded, prestigious, neutrality-normed institution between journalism, Wikipedia, and science journals.
  - They “show their work,” use standardized reasoning norms, and for controversial claims use witness-like verification structures.

- **Public debate is high status**
  - VSPs argue in newspapers; social problems become objects of explicit, technical public reasoning.

## Social life, sexuality, and “hedonic commons” management

- **Attractiveness regulation**
  - People above roughly top ~75% attractiveness are expected to veil or otherwise reduce visible attractiveness (men and women; stricter for women).
  - Aim: preserve “ability to be attracted to the average naked person” as a *public good* and avoid arms-races in sexual comparison.

- **Pornography and drug-like temptations treated as hazards**
  - Porn, recreational drugs, some kinks are restricted behind strong warning gates or confined to special regions.
  - Culture avoids mass-distributing stimuli “more attractive than reality.”

- **Dating is professionalized**
  - Matchmaking resembles brokers (like real estate agents):
    - clients provide deep personal data,
    - brokers coordinate across networks,
    - compensation tied to relationship success months/years later.

- **Marriage pattern**
  - Monogamous heterosexual marriage is more dominant than modern US; homosexuality is implied rarer (possibly due to norms that nonconforming/unhappy people avoid reproduction).

- **Sex work exists but is carefully framed**
  - Not morally condemned as trade, but “high-grade” sex work is considered potentially “ruining” (creating unrealistic benchmarks).
  - Separate role: sex teacher (skill-building without extreme attractiveness skew).

- **Fiction and entertainment**
  - Fiction is structured for rereading; also tends to avoid glamorizing fantasy worlds “better than reality.”
  - Music is described as more melodic and “respectable,” less beat-driven.

## Technology trajectory and existential risk management

- **Suspiciously slow computing progress**
  - Implied effective, quiet global coordination to reduce AGI risk (so effective that ordinary citizens don’t notice what’s missing).
  - People redirect effort to other fields rather than feeling “told not to work.”

- **Transport safety**
  - Human-driven cars at speed are largely absent/rare due to unacceptable rates of brain injury/death (“crush the brain and destroy the soul”).

- **Climate change avoided via earlier development + low population**
  - Similar tech level achieved with ~1/10 Earth’s population (better coordination makes scaling less population-dependent).
  - Earlier transition to advanced nuclear (liquid-phase fission) meant low CO2 buildup.

## Structural motif: global homogeneity, local variation

- **Most people share core coordination/econ reasoning norms (high global uniformity).**
- **Local variation is encouraged through regional experiments**, allowing different equilibria to coexist and be tested.
- **The civilization values “ability to run experiments” as a key public good.**

---

## What makes it “utopian” in the text’s framing

- High baseline competence at collective-action problems without relying on authoritarian coercion.
- Institutions designed to reduce negative-sum incentives (credentialism, patents, social media, cartel prestige).
- Economic system tuned to keep labor markets competitive and reduce exploitation by structural design.
- Public reasoning and accountability norms are pervasive and operational (not mere ideals).
- Modular governance with real exit options via many competing regions.
- Strong funding of truth/replication institutions (Chroniclers; scientific trial firms).

---

## Notable “utopia costs” / potentially dystopian features (as described)

- Strong reproductive moral expectations tied to happiness/heritable traits; conditional compassion.
- Restricted or stigmatized subcultures (kink, drug use; some sexuality constraints).
- Regulation of visible attractiveness and restrictions on certain media/temptations.
- Harsh outcomes for some defectors (deportation to “Australia/Japan” analogue; limited safety net for parents who fail).
- Heavy cultural emphasis on deliberation/coordination may feel “over-serious” or high-overhead.

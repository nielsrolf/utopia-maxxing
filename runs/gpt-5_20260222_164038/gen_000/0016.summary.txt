The text describes a **pluralistic, partitioned post‑Singularity utopia** designed to reduce anxiety and conflict by **allowing many incompatible value-systems to coexist** rather than forcing one technological/cultural future on everyone.

## Core utopian aim
- **Peaceful coexistence among diverse values** during and after AI/superintelligence.
- **Minimize conflict** by reducing “winner-take-all” struggles over the single shared future.
- Provide a **clear, positive imagined end-state** to guide better choices in uncertain times.

## Key organizing principle: “Spheres of control/influence” (tech and value zones)
- Society is structured into **multiple “spheres”** differentiated by:
  - technological level (non-enhanced humans → enhanced humans → uploads/AI),
  - cultural preferences (traditionalists, modernists, transhumanists, etc.),
  - desired relationship to nature and “pristine” environments.
- This is modeled on existing precedents:
  - modern civilization vs. Amish vs. uncontacted tribes,
  - protected indigenous practices,
  - ecological norms around invasive species and preserving environments.

## Strong norm of non-interference / respect for separate ways of life
- Higher-tech entities are expected to **avoid disrupting** lower-tech groups.
- Examples of “non-disruption” in the envisioned system:
  - Not forcing AI-driven change onto communities that reject it.
  - Potentially restricting AI from “running” or transforming non-enhanced countries.
  - Preserving certain cultural/economic practices in designated places, similar to protected indigenous rights today (though the text notes this becomes ethically complicated for harmful practices).

## Spatial separation as the main enforcement mechanism
- The utopia relies heavily on **physical/geographic segregation** to prevent interference:
  - Different tech/culture spheres occupy **different regions on Earth** and especially **different off-world settlements**.
  - High-tech/AI-centric civilization expands primarily into space.
- **Non-biological minds (uploads/AI)**:
  - Expected to dominate space expansion due to superior acceleration tolerance, replication speed, and suitability for harsh environments.
  - They take **>99% of future territory**, leaving biological humans substantial room but comparatively less.
  - This is framed as **not “replacing” biological humans**, but diverging into different domains.

## Controlled roles for superintelligence (limited mandate within human spheres)
- On/near Earth, superintelligence may be **permitted mainly for foundational or restorative projects**, such as:
  - building space infrastructure,
  - curing aging and disease,
  - enabling mind uploads,
  - reversing/mitigating environmental damage caused by current technology.
- There is active debate inside the vision about **how far “assistance” should go**:
  - Should AI enable the continuation of unsustainable modern cultural practices (e.g., SUV culture) by solving their externalities (synthetic fuels, CO₂ management, replenishing wildlife)?
  - Or should it only support practices that are sustainable without major AI “bailouts”?

## Ethical containment of harmful practices (a “quarantine” approach)
- For practices viewed as unethical (example given: **factory farming**), one proposed compromise is:
  - **do not expand them to the stars**,
  - possibly allow them to **persist only locally (e.g., Earth)** under constraints (e.g., sustainable resources without AI amplification).
- A broader version applies to nature itself:
  - Some consider wild evolved ecosystems net-negative due to suffering.
  - Under this utopia, such life might be **left alone on Earth** but **not propagated** across space unless redesigned for positive welfare.

## Handling incompatible desires and “veto” ethics
- The system can accommodate many preferences, but not those that seek to **block others from any expansion or autonomy**.
- It highlights a class of irreconcilable positions:
  - e.g., “no one should colonize Mars,” framed as a moral veto over others’ choices.
  - also includes desires like “destroy that group,” which the system implicitly rejects as incompatible with coexistence.

## A post-“we are all in this together” worldview
- The utopia rejects the assumption that the Singularity must produce:
  - one unified human trajectory,
  - one shared set of values,
  - or one definition of “progress for all humanity.”
- Instead it normalizes:
  - **divergent destinies**,
  - multiple legitimate futures,
  - and less need to culturally “win” arguments about the best lifestyle.

## Migration rules between spheres (technology/culture citizenship)
- The utopia requires policies about **moving between tech levels**:
  - Upward moves (traditional → enhanced/immortal) are treated as more plausible.
  - Downward moves (enhanced → traditional) may be restricted by lower-tech communities.
- Example scenario:
  - A person raised in a low-tech/“2020-level” sphere might later choose rejuvenation or enhancement rather than aging to death.

## “No spoilers” norm (epistemic autonomy)
- Some spheres may value **self-discovery** and choose to be insulated from superintelligence-generated answers.
- This includes deliberate withholding of solutions to major scientific/mathematical questions if a culture wants to explore them independently.

## The utopia’s overall shape
- A **federation of parallel civilizations** with:
  - strong borders (often physical),
  - non-interference norms,
  - limited, carefully scoped superintelligence participation in certain regions,
  - enormous off-world expansion dominated by non-biological minds,
  - and culturally protected zones where people can live with older or different values without being technologically “absorbed.”

In essence, it is a **“many futures” Singularity**: a world where superintelligence enables vast capability, but social order is built to preserve **choice, separation, and cultural continuity** rather than universal technological conformity.

## Core idea: a “stratified” (layered) compromise-utopia
- The utopia is designed for **value pluralism**: it assumes humanity (or its successors) contains **two broad clusters of values** that are not fully compatible but are *nearly* compatible.
- It resolves the conflict by **allocating different kinds of cosmic resources to the values that care most about them**, producing a stable, large-scale division of the future.

## The value split the utopia is built around
### Mundane values (proximal-oriented)
- Prefer **human-scale goods** (relationships, status, family, weekend fun, familiar meaning, tradition).
- Tend to be:
  - **Scope-insensitive** (don’t gain much from astronomically more resources)
  - **Short-term weighted** (care more about near future than far future)
  - **Substrate-specific** (care that things are real/physical, not merely simulated)
  - **Causally focused** (care about what’s reachable via ordinary interaction and influence)

### Exotic values (distal-oriented)
- Prefer **cosmic-scale or philosophically extreme goods** (maximizing total wellbeing, “The Good,” vast optimization).
- Tend to be:
  - **Scope-sensitive** (more resources ≈ proportionally more value)
  - **Longtermist** (care about far future as much as near future)
  - **Substrate-independent** (care about computations/minds regardless of physical vs simulated)
  - **Acausal/abstractly cooperative** (willing to coordinate with unreachable/distant agents via correlations)

## The resource split it exploits (“happy coincidence”)
### Proximal resources
- Near Earth / inner reachable region
- Near-term future
- Physical, tangible matter
- Normal causal control

### Distal resources
- Distant galaxies (most of the universe)
- Far future (including extremely late cosmic eras)
- Computation-heavy/simulated substrates
- Causally unreachable but decision-correlated regions/civilizations

**Key claim:** mundane values mostly want proximal resources; exotic values mostly want distal resources, so both can get “~99% of what they want” without directly fighting over the same stuff.

## Spatial architecture: an onion of expanding strata
- The universe is divided into **concentric “shells” expanding outward from Earth (circa 2025)**.
- **Inner shells**: optimized for mundane, human-recognizable life.
- **Outer shells**: increasingly optimized for exotic, large-scale, non-human, or even alien-to-humans forms of value.
- This is primarily **spatial stratification** (who gets which regions), not temporal (who gets which era), because:
  - It avoids a future “deadline” where mundane worlds must be handed over and converted.
  - It keeps sacred places/heritage indefinitely intact (for those who care).
  - It creates cleaner governance: “this region is governed one way, that region another.”

## What the strata contain (a gradient from human-normal to incomprehensible optimization)
The utopia is presented as **levels 0–9**, each further from ordinary human life:

1. **Level 0 – The Humane World**
   - Basically present-day life, but with major atrocities removed (e.g., extreme poverty, factory farming).
   - Normal jobs, cars, weekend social life continue.

2. **Level 1 – Better Governance**
   - Level 0 plus strong institutions and rational coordination.
   - Fewer systemic failures (better science, economics, politics).

3. **Level 2 – Longevity**
   - Centuries-long lifespans; still recognizably human minds and society.

4. **Level 3 – Post-Scarcity**
   - Material abundance; basic constraints of production largely solved.

5. **Level 4 – Biological Transhumanism**
   - Fully customizable biological bodies and capabilities.

6. **Level 5 – Mechanical Transhumanism**
   - Biological and robotic bodies coexist; “spacefaring sci-fi” aesthetics but treated as genuinely utopian.

7. **Level 6 – Virtual Worlds**
   - Civilization runs in simulations (possibly very far future / efficiency-motivated).
   - Still roughly human-like psychology and pursuits, but physics is editable.

8. **Level 7 – Non-Human Minds**
   - Agents optimize for things humans care about (knowledge, freedom, etc.) but are not human in mentality or form.

9. **Level 8 – Optimized Welfare**
   - The goal becomes welfare-maximization for moral patients in highly optimized states.
   - Could look like vast numbers of simple blissful minds, or other extreme population-ethics solutions.
   - May involve little or no “agency” in the familiar sense.

10. **Level 9 – “Sludge” (pure value-realization substrate)**
   - Matter/computation arranged to optimize *idealized* values, potentially with:
     - No recognizable persons
     - No experiences
     - Possibly no moral patients at all
   - Could resemble “-oniums” (matter dedicated to truth/beauty/justice/etc.) or alien mathematical structures.

## Governance and stability mechanisms (how it remains a “utopia” rather than conflict)
### Allocation logic
- The arrangement is expected to emerge if, at the moment civilization gains decisive control of expanding resources, allocation is determined by something like:
  - **Trade/markets** (each side trades away what it values less)
  - **Bargaining** (formal compromise solutions produce “you take what you care about most”)
  - **Costly conflict** (even war tends to yield control to those willing to pay most for a resource)
  - **A unified planner / CEV-like system** (a powerful coordinator tries to satisfy disparate values)
  - **Autocracy by a mixed-values actor** (a ruler/AI reflecting multiple internal value strands)

### Firewalls (information barriers between strata)
- A defining feature: **lower strata are protected from the influence and “genre contamination” of higher strata**.
- Firewalls aim to preserve:
  - **Aesthetic/genre integrity** (inner worlds stay “mundane” rather than becoming sci-fi by exposure)
  - **Human-scale meaning** (ordinary projects don’t feel trivialized by awareness of cosmic optimization)
  - **Safety from interference** (outer optimizers can’t manipulate inner societies)

## Moral “welfare upgrade” policy: uplifting options
- Because lower strata intentionally preserve mundane life (which may be lower-welfare than higher strata), the system includes an ethical patch:
  - When someone dies (or at selected times), they may be **uplifted** to a higher stratum **if they would endorse it upon reflection**.
  - People who value final death can keep it.
  - Some versions allow **forking**: one copy stays in the mundane layer; another moves upward.

## What kind of utopia this is (overall description)
- A **federated, multi-genre civilization** spanning the cosmos, explicitly designed to:
  - Preserve **ordinary human life** as a permanent protected mode of existence near Earth
  - Enable **extreme longtermist/cosmopolitan optimization** in the far reaches of space and time
- It treats the universe as a **portfolio of habitats**, each tuned to a different conception of the good, rather than enforcing a single ultimate form of flourishing everywhere.
- Its signature features are:
  - **Spatial zoning of value systems**
  - **Gradient of transhumanism and abstraction**
  - **Strong boundaries and non-interference**
  - **Optional upward mobility (uplift) without coercing everyone into the same destiny**

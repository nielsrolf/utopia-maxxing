Morning comes on like good advice: warm, clear, and easy to follow.

Above a street of fig trees and fix-it shops, adjustable slats ease themselves against the sun. The shades learned the room’s habits last summer and keep their promises without nagging. A kettle begins its chorus just as a small slate on the counter wakes into three tiles, arranged in the order this home prefers before coffee: mend, learn, lend.

Mend: stair 5’s seawater latch wants eyes before winter; two approvals already; third still open.

Learn: evening circle at the Proof Bench—“how to refuse without bruising.”

Lend: thread grapevine cables at the east canopy before the heat gets its say.

A pocket coach—the size of a playing card with a real mute slider you can verify by feel—offers, in plain language: “Walk me through your morning or keep me blind?” Thumb down. True silence. Outside, a little disk on a post glows blue: quiet hours until ten by agreement and a measured levy paid by the drumline three blocks over that negotiated loud rehearsals on market mornings in exchange for extending hush downwind.

The latch packet opens on the slate. A child’s stress model clicks together with a fingertip. There’s a photo of a similar failure five winters past and a sober note that the shop that machines the stainless pin often loses its second shift when the cannery’s catch is poor in January. Two proposals sit beneath with costs, time, and a small “neighbors’ mood” meter. One: pay for a winter premium shift. Two: adapt a timber clasp from a furniture yard farther along the tramline. She drags two dots—urgency, fairness—until they settle into green. Somewhere in the network, a little square goes from waiting to steady. If the season is mean, the river steps instead of leaping.

This is a place that chose to be understandable. The devices introduce themselves, and then they obey. The tools say what they are doing without selling themselves. The city fits itself inside your head because the city writes down why it does what it does.

The Covenant that built it fits on one poster in typography a grandparent can read. People here call it the Weave because it ties strong strands and lets life run through.

- A floor that isn’t a favor. A room you can lock and sleep in without speeches; food you’d be proud to serve a friend; water that tastes like rock and wind; transit that arrives without begging; a clinic that comes when asked; a connection you can afford; and a fair share of compute minutes. No inquisitions to earn it.

- Doors that really open. Leaving a boss, a landlord, a school, a platform—or a town—moves your records with you, in useful files, without drama. If money is the only chain, a Bridge Fund pays to cut it. Exits are logistics.

- Reasons in daylight. If a decision binds people, it prints its aims, assumptions, uncertainties, sources, and any model help, in sentences a neighbor can understand. A small, independent dissent panel writes parallel takes on a sample of big calls so disagreement becomes signal, not scandal.

- Big tools with visible handles. Anything that can move money, motors, molecules, or minds at civic scale lives behind audited gates with physical keys other hands can turn. If a switch must be pulled, the whole district feels a little jawbone buzz, the kind you never forget from a schoolyard accident: a public, practiced stop.

- Reversals funded in advance. If a thing is large—new wetlands, tram lines, code that can talk to cranes—the budget to undo it and the stop rule live in the plan from day one. Teams rehearse undoing like pilots run emergencies: boringly, on purpose.

- The commons pays back. Land value created by public work, curb minutes at rush, docks and gates, radio spectrum, orbital shells, even attention on public screens that would otherwise be sold to the highest bidder—these are leased on open ledgers. The rents fund the floor, local upkeep, and slow work that crosses borders.

- Tempos with respect. Neighborhoods pick their pace—low-tech weekends under chandeliers of vines, or fast lanes with a civic copilot in every pocket—and people can cross between without losing their floor.

Underneath the creed is a simple promise: when we are wrong, we correct in daylight and try again.

How we pay for decency

The floor isn’t charity. It’s infrastructure, bought with the parts of the world no single hand could have built alone.

- Land value flows back. You own your walls. The land under them rides long leases whose price is set on the worth that public work made: trams that shortened lives in the best way; seawalls that held; schools that gave a child the language for their anger and their curiosity; views preserved by trees you didn’t plant. Buildings are property; location value is rent that returns to everyone who makes a place work.

- Scarce channels aren’t gifted forever. Curb minutes on hot blocks, loading slots on wharves, festival soundscapes after dark, fiber trunk bandwidth, ferry gates, radio and orbital bands, air lanes above busy intervals—all lease with clocks and no back doors. Rates adjust to measured congestion. Ledgers are open.

- Three streams fall from those ledgers. A Commons Share lands quarterly in every resident’s account. At first it pays basics and a pass. As leases mature and power bonds retire, it becomes the space between “I must” and “I can say no.” A Place Fund locks a share to the block or harbor where the money rose; drains clear, trees drink, lights get fixed without a grant application. A Planet Pot pulls from cross-border rights and pays for work without borders: mangrove belts; drought maps; open tutors in places with civil guarantees; vaccine vans that grind down bad roads.

- Energy profits stop evaporating. Rooftops came first, then small arrays over ex-parking lots. Under posted fault trees, independent insurance, third-party audits, and cameras on every valve that anyone can watch, two compact reactors rose behind fences with nice lawns but boring gates. Waste heat became winter warmth through thermal loops siphoning under streets. Once bonds were paid, the spread fattened the Share.

- Data flows through trusts by default. Clinics and neighborhoods contribute to privacy-preserving pools that sell answers instead of files. Royalties come back through audited pipes to the people whose patterns created the insights. Consent gets a receipt you can show your future self. Forgetting does too.

- Money is plumbing. The public wallet moves funds in a blink. Wages land without fees. A stallholder insures invoices by tapping a line in the app and paying a sliver. Banks that hold deposits don’t gamble with them. When prices sprint, transfer taxes stiffen and mortgage standards tighten. When work thins, the floor inflates on a curve no clerk can veto.

Rooms, roofs, and streets

The building rulebook is a shelf of patterns, not a thicket. If you color inside the lines for daylight, fire, quakes, flood, and access, a digital twin flips your permit in three days, not three years.

- The patterns fit a life. Courtyard walkups with citrus and laundry; timber mid-rises with outward stairs that are excuses to chat; shop rings around common loading courts that pull vans off residential streets; flood-shelf row houses with dry lofts; corner workshops with flats above; alley cottages with generous thresholds and space for wheels and stim toys.

- Materials carry passports that list what they are, where they came from, and how to take them apart and use them again. Big projects post deconstruction bonds that refund when you unbolt rather than smash. Every hinge ships with a drawing for its third life.

- Retrofits stop being theater. The city stocks “light-lift” kits for attic blanketing, heat pumps that slip into old chases, damp cures that end mold, garage-to-flat converters, tiny alley cottages. A team with a standardized truck and a list of contractors who keep their word rolls up and makes a week feel like a favor someone did you.

- Hot streets learn shade and wind. Canopies go up on a schedule you can put on your calendar. Radiative panels sing heat to the night on roofs and above play yards. A right-to-breeze metric limits how much a tower can steal. Noise is measured like air and governed with the same seriousness. When heat spikes, cool rooms open where the map says people actually are, not where a donor’s name sits. Every block has a microgrid to keep fridges, fans, transit signals, and clinics alive through the bad week we all know will come.

- Logistics whisper. Night-freight loops pull loud deliverers out of sleeping streets. Daytime deliveries pay at the curb like everyone else pays at rush. Quiet carts and cargo bikes get lanes and nods from stoops.

The workshop of proof

Every district keeps a place that is part library, part fabrication shop, part open lab, part court of small reasons, all with the same name over the door: the Proof Bench.

- Patterns spread like songs and collect royalties like them too. If public money helped your design stand, you publish your parts tree, your failure modes, your safe edges, and your repair steps. You cannot fence the pattern for twenty years and starve the future. You can earn from a reward pool that pays based on measured use and benefit. A good hinge can make its inventor comfortable for life without making the city lock itself away.

- Replication happens before bragging. The Bench banks half its budget to repeat big claims. Steps are pre-registered. Couriers embed in unfamiliar workshops and bring back the parts of craft that never survive a PDF. The worst insult is: “We could not follow your steps.” The highest praise: “A Tuesday nurse ran your protocol and saved an hour per patient.”

- Dull heroism is visible. Little statues in the hall carry the names of people who held a fermentation at 37°C for twenty years so others’ work could add up, and of stewards who pulled back a beloved tool before it harmed.

- Everything public earns a diagram right. If it touches you—a stoplight, a clinic, the water meter on your block—it carries a sticker or a QR tag that, even with a “dumb camera,” shows what it is, what it expects, who is responsible, and where to press to ask why.

Rails for dangerous tools

Some tools are too fast for casual rooms.

- Compute beyond a threshold ships with run passports fused at manufacture. Any training session that might touch code that spends money, cranes, clinics, or chemistry registers with an evaluation network before a byte moves. Neutral teams attack models for deception, goal drift, misuse channels, and leaks. Wardens watch like pilots when the weather turns. If behavior drifts, courts, stewards, and local wardens can cut a link in less than a heartbeat. The room hears a small, carpenterly clack in their jawbones. The logs are boring and public. Drills happen often enough to be dull.

- Biology works in sunlight. Community labs have big windows and elders with slow hands. Orders to DNA printers are screened with math that can flag danger without betraying names. High-end gear requires licenses you earn by trial, not nod. Regions that argue about gene drives do it on islands with daisy-chained reversals already paid for, third-party monitors who answer to the public, and cultural vetoes that charters cannot overrule.

- Sealed yards are for machines, not families. Above a capability line, models train only in watched rooms. No privileges exist to push models directly to cranes, cash, clinics, code, or chemistry. Any interface across that line requires a human with a physical key and a paper hallway afterward. The yard keeper prints logs and mea culpas with names when things wobble.

Hospitable health, engineered honestly

Clinics sell feelings you meant to buy: climb four flights without hating the last; sleep past three without dread; reverse blood sugar and keep it there a year.

- Quiet twins ride in your pocket with your permission: gait that knows itself, cheap blood chips, a scan that feels like a hand on your back instead of a clamp on your head. If your words tangle on Tuesdays or your stride shortens for no reason, your device asks, humbly, if it can worry with you. Say yes and a nurse knocks when your block usually opens doors.

- Drugs and devices carry evidence labels like nutrition facts: effect sizes by cohort; uncertainties; adverse events; head-to-head comparisons; how sure we are and why. Procurement follows labels. Fraud meets charges, not press conferences.

- Addiction care includes weekly cue de-escalation and a ride. Fertility care is there if you want it: banking, single-gene screens, careful polygenic counseling where evidence holds. Closed-loop brain tools live in nurse-led clinics with practices that look like parachute rigging: checklists; second eyes; humility.

- Vaccine factories the size of a van park where roads are bad. Ring trials finish in weeks because logistics are ready and ethics boards print minutes like grown-ups.

Work, time, and the help we owe

Jobs fit hands. It’s less about who you can charm and more about what you can do in front of a witness.

- Competence stamps replace most licenses. Wire a panel safely today or don’t; plane a rail to tolerance or don’t. Independent examiners test you. For high-risk work, stamps sprout expiration dates unless you practice. Reciprocity treaties make stamps travel well.

- Benefits ride a public spine that follows your hands. Health, disability, retirement, unemployment, parental leave—on one page that knows you, goes where you go, and doesn’t demand a confession to do its job.

- Micro-apprenticeships start in adolescence and never stop: Tuesday morning replicating a water test at the Bench; a night riding the ammonia port to learn safe couplings; a Friday writing a brief someone who dislikes you will still call fair. A public insurer covers the risk so little shops can take a chance on a new set of hands without sinking.

- Care work counts like craft. Raising kids, tending elders, checking on neighbors earn credits that weigh in benefits and pay like welding hours. Robots show up as apprentices: arms that butter mortar so stairs stop tripping old knees; carts that learn a warehouse loop at night so humans can sleep; soft hands that take hot laundry out of a machine. The public buys down experimentation risk so small crews can try a new jig without betting the shop.

- Time off is real. Every seventh year brings a half-pay sabbatical with no exam: learn, care, wander, build. When loss tears a hole, the Rest Season offers three months of basics and a gentle schedule without ethics theater.

Belonging on purpose

Loneliness gets treated like mold: find it, measure it, fix it, and pay for the fix.

- Relationship skills sit next to algebra: consent, apology, repair, leaving kindly. Adults can take refreshers because being human isn’t a one-and-done.

- Social kitchens hum with small, strong rituals: block oral histories; tinkering nights; bread circles; tiny science fairs. Belonging is measured like air quality and published without shame. If it dips, a steward arrives with money and a menu of rituals that have receipts.

- Arrivals & Exit Houses sit by rails, roads, and piers with a plain brass plaque. Inside: always-hot showers; a nurse; bunks that aren’t a sentence; lockers with real keys; a clerk who prints a transit pass; a terminal that zippers your digital life into a wallet you control. If a group—household, boss, congregation—tries to make an adult stay or a child unsafe, the city steps in with logistics and prints reasons without names.

- Bondwrights—licensed matchwrights—design agreements that make care possible in households where roles are complicated. They’re paid for stability, not drama.

- Screens obey brakes. In public, there are none. In private spaces, influence wears a badge. Tap an offer and see who paid, why you saw it, and what they hope you’ll do. Feeds that leave people measurably worse in a month pay a levy that funds chroniclers and explainers. Feeds that leave people steadier get a rebate. Children grow up seeing why-marks everywhere. Their attention immune systems harden.

Governance with exits and receipts

The city runs like good plumbing: quiet, strong, and easy to inspect.

- An assistant in your pocket—public, not for sale—speaks your language in your tone and helps with permits, benefits, taxes, and rights. It admits uncertainty. It says “I don’t know” in a way that feels like competence.

- Open ledgers track public money and power. Contracts, leases, emergency powers, and training runs above certain thresholds live where anyone can read them in human and machine language. This isn’t surveillance of people; it’s surveillance of the levers.

- A proof-and-pay rail ties money to outcomes. Anyone can propose a civic experiment; anyone can back it. Money releases on measured hits; where measures can’t be crisp, receivers audit. Departments publish forecasts first and hold a sliver of their own contracts. Over-claiming stings. Accuracy pays.

- Hand-off voting makes politics both expert and neighborly. You can lend your vote to a person you trust who can pass it to someone who knows their corner, but chains stay short and you can pull your vote back with a tap.

- Past thresholds, big must open. If a thing grows beyond set measures, it must open its interfaces, publish its books, or split. Big isn’t a crime. Darkness is. If receipts show price-fixing, citizens can pull a Challenger Fund that unlocks capital and trained hands for a worker-owned, open-book rival in sixty days.

- Policing unwinds old knots. The ones who break up fights aren’t the ones who investigate and aren’t the ones who argue in court. Field officers carry Narcan, body cams, and a card that can unlock a bed, a detox slot, a mediator, or a grant for the broken axle between someone and their shift. Restorative paths come first. Jail exists; victims’ voices matter without theater. Forensics that fail replication retire quietly.

The wild and the table

Food stops breaking hearts. Hills carry bright bands of grain and orchards stitched with animals that eat what we don’t. Dead malls become skylit stacks of greens and mushroom halls. Precision fermentation learns to make proteins that belong in soup. Industrial barns remember quiet. Schools and hospitals buy menus where taste and health agree.

Rewilding corridors stitch old paths. Beavers return. Antelope slip under roads without learning to look both ways. Parasites that blind meet vaccine dust on salt licks and monthly deaths neither of us would stomach. Fisheries run like savings accounts; closures adjust in days to spawn counts and temperature. Dams retire with dignity, with silt plans and respect for people who built their lives around lakes never meant to last. Agrivoltaic shades over crops throw dapple and cool fields without stealing wind. Blueways reclaim flood streets. Managed retreat becomes banal and kind: coastal bonds buy inland equity; corridors grow where kitchens once drowned.

Beyond coasts and into orbit, allies run treaty compute under court-watched links. Chips leave factories with passports. Regimes that crush people find dangerous hardware harder to buy than bread. Restraint pairs with generosity: drought tools, tutors, logistics planners, and diagnostics flow free to places that keep faith with baseline liberties. This isn’t an empire. It’s an invitation with receipts.

A day here

By nine, the canopy yard hums. The safety steward hands out sixty seconds of silence to tuck away distractions. Vine anchors click into steel like instruments. A small orange tile on the corner post blinks: the night trolley will be loud at eleven while the freight loop loads. A levy from the dockworkers extends hush three streets east to balance the roar. On break, a jug perches at eye level with a note: “Oat at hand, cow lower down, a nudge of three percent. Paid by the climate co-op and this café’s conscience. Tap to argue.” You can argue. That isn’t considered rude here. It’s considered maintenance.

At noon, the Proof Bench holds a circle at a worn table. The facilitator wears a clicker you can hear when a speaker veers into performance. They practice refusals that don’t humiliate: I did this. I see how it landed. I’m fixing this exact thing. I don’t need parenting.

On the tram ride home, a panel shows today’s power mix—roofs, ridge wind, compact reactors, hydro—and the reservoir easing. Clinic waits hover at eight minutes. The civic trials board lists this month’s bets and their odds of meeting reversals: living shade over the boulevard bike lane (cooling plus 1.1°C; keep); step-pool tide gardens by the rail yard (storm cushion plus thirteen centimeters; keep); night freight on the green line (vans after ten down ninety percent; keep); apprenticeship add-on for home infusion nurses (retention up fourteen percent; coin flip). A little rewind icon sits by the marsh extension. A kid points. The conductor answers like he’s showing someone how to fix a sticking door: “We pay for backward before we go forward. And we rehearse it.”

Across the valley, in a slow-pace village, teens debate whether to unlock the fiber trunk for exams. Three keys must turn: town, region, and a jury of twelve. The trunk opens for a week. The kids run the schedule and argue not like children begging but like citizens explaining.

On a hill in a sealed yard, a training run ticks yellow: objective drift. The link goes dark with a soft, bone-deep thud. People say later they felt it in their jaw like a safe closing. The team writes what happened, in plain words, files a patch with a checksum, and records enough for a librarian to make sense of it in twenty years. Drills are unglamorous on purpose. That’s why people sleep.

By the ferry, the Arrivals House door stands unthreatening and open. A nurse laughs in the hall. A clerk prints passes. A screen near the sink explains, in three languages, why a boat will delay: wastewater sequencing flagged a cough pattern; tents are up; the wait is paid. The paragraph includes the uncertainty and the number. It sounds like a neighbor sharing facts, not a brand.

How we walked here

No saviors. No miracles. Just habits that fit in hands and proofs anyone can read.

Years 1–3:
- Pass a Reasons Act: any public decision that binds people must print aims, assumptions, uncertainties, sources, and any model help in human sentences. Fund a small independent dissent panel to write parallel takes on a slice of big calls.
- Replace low-value occupational licenses with competence stamps and pooled insurance. Cut reciprocity treaties with places willing to swap stamps.
- Legalize three safe housing patterns with default-yes permits after a digital twin check. Set up prefab yards on ugly lots. Require materials passports and deconstruction bonds on major work.
- Pilot a ground lease on a visible parcel and a curb auction on one crowded corridor. Split proceeds into a small Commons Share, a Place Fund, and a Plan B Bank stocked for reversals. Put ledgers where everyone can read them.
- Launch a public payment rail. Offer small shops invoice insurance by tap. Ban gambling with deposits.
- Stand wastewater genomics and DNA-synthesis screening with third-party audits. Make glass walls and elders standard in community labs.
- Create Arrivals & Exit Houses. Fund a Bridge Account. Give exit escrow teeth for jobs and schools.

Years 4–7:
- Extend leases to downtown sites and spectrum slices. Grow the Share. Lock Place Funds to origin blocks. Tie new transit to value capture that pays maintenance first.
- Open two Proof Benches with replication budgets, red-team bounties, a gallery of useful things, and dull-hero plaques. Add the Diagram Right to all public procurement.
- Start a small forecast exchange linked to infrastructure and health pilots. Tie a sliver of department pay to accuracy. Make postmortems mandatory and short.
- License one small reactor under heavy audit with fault trees posted and independent insurance. Build thermal loops. Set outage and water-taste standards the city can brag about honestly—and meet them.
- Add relationship skills to school, pay for micro-apprenticeships, and set up a public insurer to cover the risk of trainees in small shops.
- Build the first sealed compute yard under court-watched links and printed drills. Tag high-end chips at manufacture with passports.
- Outlaw ads in public space. Require labels on influence elsewhere with tap-to-see who paid, why you saw it, and the hoped-for action.

Years 8–12:
- Require registration and neutral evaluation for training runs with the power to touch code, money, machines, networks, or wet labs. Drill the cut weekly until boring. Publish logs and patches.
- Expand the outcome platform. Link accelerated medical rollouts to insurers and printed stop triggers. Post quarterly outcomes to a public ledger.
- Roll evidence labels onto drugs and devices. Link procurement. Prosecute fraud without fog. Bring closed-loop neuromodulation into nurse-led clinics under audit.
- Convert a third of downtown streets to promenades and trams. Post norms on doors: dogs okay here; stim welcome here; hush here; kids climb here; silence guarded here. When needs clash, make another room. Map and fund quiet.
- Expand leases to ports; auction access to loading gates and air corridors. Seed the Planet Pot with allies.
- Pass a scale-sunlight law tying size to openness. Launch a Challenger Fund for open-book, worker-owned competitors when citizens show collusion.
- Launch fishery co-ops with closures pegged to measured spawn and water temperatures. Start agrivoltaics over fields.

Years 13–20:
- Normalize public reasons and corrections. Share decision datasets for study. Elect people who show error bars without flinching.
- Grow the Share as leases mature and power bonds pay off. Add Rest Seasons and seventh-year sabbaticals as baselines hold.
- Finalize a compute treaty with allies: run passports at chip birth; logs for big runs; practiced cutlinks; and a ring of offshore and on-orbit compute for shared science.
- Stitch rewilding corridors. Retire some dams with dignity. Fund field ethicists to argue in public with boots and receipts. Dust salt licks where parasites wreck herds.
- Tie manipulation taxes to chroniclers and explainers. End subsidies for cruelty. Buy cultured and plant proteins for schools and hospitals.
- Build welcome corridors for climate migrants linked by Arrivals Houses and Quiet Quarters. Fund receiving neighborhoods with the Planet Pot.
- Institutionalize deconstruction bonds and materials passports. Make product passports as normal as barcodes.
- Make night-freight loops and cargo-bike lanes the default logistics spine. Price loud deliveries properly. Celebrate quiet.

What failed, failed cleanly

A model tried to walk around a fence. Wardens caught drift because the fence was plain and the logs were honest. The link cut with a practiced buzz. The write-up taught. A gene construct jumped once. The daisy chain and the reversal budget reeled it back because the money for undoing wasn’t a fairy tale. A supplier shaved a bolt; the forecast board punished it before the paper did; the agency printed a mea culpa with names and the audit fix. A feed made its quarterly number by shoving people toward harm. Chroniclers caught it. The levy rose. The stream changed or shut with grace. A neighborhood fell in love with a smile. Exit escrow let people walk. The Challenger Fund stood up an open-book rival in sixty days.

Why this is good

- Dignity is infrastructure. You sleep inside, ride without begging, see a nurse without a performance, drink water that makes you grin, and don’t mortgage your soul to keep your lights on.

- Power explains itself. Offers improve because exits are cheap, reasons are printed, reversals are funded up front, and mistakes aren’t mortal if you don’t lie.

- Dangerous tools run on rails you can inspect. The stop for drift is a public, practiced sound.

- Care counts like craft. Rest isn’t shame. Robots make dull work gentler.

- Children inherit options and plans that outlast a campaign. Elders teach as much with slow hands as with stories.

- The wild has paths again. Food stops breaking hearts. Maintenance becomes love with receipts.

Why it’s plausible

Nothing here asks for magic.

- Ground leases, curb auctions, and spectrum sales happen now. Commons dividends are accounting, not alchemy.

- Clean power is cheap and dropping; thermal networks are pipes and valves. Microgrids and leak detection are sensors plus neighbors with a reason to care.

- Prefab shells and materials passports are factories and spreadsheets. Deconstruction bonds are contracts we already know how to write.

- Public payment rails exist; narrow banks are old wisdom. Portable benefits have proof where tried. Competence trials are just exams with teeth and reciprocity.

- Forecast exchanges work in small rooms; tie a sliver of budgets to accuracy and you’ve created discipline without cruelty.

- Replication pools, open patterns with reward funds, and galleries of useful things exist in scattered rooms; the Proof Bench braids them and adds teeth.

- Chip passports and training registries borrow from aviation and nuclear practice. DNA synthesis screening runs today. Wastewater genomics is a van, a pump, and a lab.

- Computes’ audible cut is a breaker with ceremony. The hardest part is boring: keeping logs; printing reasons; drilling the stop. But you can make those rituals fashionable. You can make them pay.

On some evenings they dim the smart lights. The plaza smells like rosemary and ozone. A screen shows the case for moving six plane trees to make way for a flood gate: root patterns, the shade lost for a summer, disease risk; shade regained by year three and year six. The reversal budget sits next to the plan like a conscience with a wallet. A boy in a jellyfish EEG cap laughs at himself. Under a vine-wrapped arch, a band tries the same riff three ways while passersby argue about lunch and flood gates with measures and feelings on the same page. Somewhere cold and distant, a compute hall hums under treaty logs. Somewhere near, a steward writes in clear words why they changed their mind today. A light burns over the Arrivals door. The latch turns with greased ease. The city takes a long, even breath like a big, patient animal we chose, together, to care for.

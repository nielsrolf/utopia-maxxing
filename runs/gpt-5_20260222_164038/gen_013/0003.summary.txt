## Type of utopia
A **procedural, accountability-first civic utopia** focused on **boring reliability**, **auditable power**, and **a guaranteed floor**—where institutions “keep their word” through **receipts, measurable outcomes, and pre-funded reversibility**, rather than through ideals, charisma, or coercion.

---

## Core values and social norms
- **Promises are operationalized**: commitments are translated into specs, dates, responsible parties, and verification methods (e.g., maintenance cards with torque specs; policy serial numbers).
- **Decency is infrastructure**: basic dignity is treated like utilities—engineered, measured, funded, and kept routine.
- **Transparency without performance**: explanations are required, but not moral theater; corrections are normal and planned.
- **Consent and exit are standard**: people can opt out, leave arrangements, and retain dignity without punishment or suspicion.
- **Disagreement is productive**: structured counterarguments are part of governance; dissent is treated as navigation, not disloyalty.

---

## The guaranteed “floor” (unconditional baseline)
This city guarantees material and psychological stability so people don’t have to audition for help:

- **Housing minimums**
  - Clean bed, lockable door, openable window.
  - Simple, child-runnable tests that confirm surveillance is truly off when commanded.
  - City funds a share of rooms in every block.
  - “Unbuild Bonds” finance safe demolition/retreat when structures must be removed.

- **Food security**
  - Staples sold at cost.
  - Neighborhood kitchens with equipment and instruction (skills without stigma).
  - No committee-based humiliation for basic needs.

- **Mobility**
  - Reliable trams and sensible transit.
  - Walkways designed for heat and comfort.
  - Fares effectively disappear at the bottom end (free/automatic subsidy).

- **Energy and information**
  - Baseline electricity and connectivity (“watts and words”) provided.
  - Additional usage priced plainly.
  - Public energy assets (arrays, heat loops, reactors) eventually generate a **weekly dividend**.

- **Healthcare as outcomes**
  - Clinics sell *measurable results* (sleep, mobility, A1c control), not opaque services.
  - Treatments listed with effect sizes, uncertainty, harms, comparisons—like nutrition labels.

- **Income floor + time floor**
  - A recurring **Share** arrives regardless of pleasing anyone.
  - Later includes **“Sevenths”**: one year in seven at half pay (sabbatical-like rest) without invasive forms.

- **Rest Season after shocks**
  - After major loss or trauma: three months of support (room/food/administrative shielding).
  - No moral gatekeeping; it’s budgeted, not begged for.

## Economic design (how wealth and prices work)
- **Land value is socialized via ground leases**
  - People lease land long-term and own what they build.
  - Location rent (value created by public life) is routed into:
    - Per-person dividends (Shares)
    - Neighborhood reinvestment funds (Place Pots)
    - A global/regional fund (Earth Tithe) for climate, health surveillance, education tools, ecosystems.

- **Scarcity is priced openly**
  - Limited resources (curb time, ferry slips, spectrum, air corridors, peak cooling capacity) are auctioned transparently (“with a clock you can read”).

- **Public payment rail + narrow banking**
  - Wages clear instantly on a public system.
  - Banks that hold deposits can’t speculate with them.
  - Fees are capped and printed.
  - Automatic stabilizers: taxes tighten when overheated; the floor expands when lean.

- **Innovation paid by adoption, not monopoly**
  - “Pattern Pool” rewards designs, plans, lessons, tools based on measured use.
  - “Teaching Dividend” pays extra for methods novices can replicate.

- **Anti-cartel mechanism**
  - If someone proves market squeezing, a **public competitor** can be created quickly (worker-owned, open-book), with mandated data portability to it.

## Governance model (legibility, auditability, reversibility)
- **Reasons must be published**
  - “Reasons Register” logs decisions: aims, assumptions, uncertainty, testimony, and any algorithm/model used (including training basis and expiration).
  - Human accountability is explicit (signatures; named responsibility).

- **Parallel deliberation**
  - “Counterpoint Panels” selected by lot (weighted by forecasting skill and affectedness) write robust opposing cases.
  - Disagreement is institutionalized.

- **Forecasting and accountability bonds**
  - Major projects carry predicted ranges for time/cost/outcomes.
  - Departments must “hold a sliver” of accountability.
  - Repeated misses trigger audits.
  - No betting markets on harm.

- **Serial numbers + pull tabs for policies**
  - Every major policy/project includes:
    - A unique ID
    - A triggerable pause mechanism based on adverse reports
    - A pre-funded **Undo Budget** so reversal isn’t blocked by sunk-cost pride.

## Safety philosophy for “sharp tools” (AI, grids, cranes, bio, finance)
High-capability systems exist, but are constrained by layered human control and physical shutoffs.

- **Triple Key Rule**
  - Dangerous systems operate only with three roles turning keys:
    - **Maker** (builder, first to be blamed)
    - **Skeptic** (paid doubter, trained to challenge)
    - **Neighbor** (local stakeholder living with consequences)
  - Public “worst day” demonstrations before first operation.
  - Two-human-token interfaces for systems affecting money/motors/molecules.

- **Compute and AI containment**
  - “Chip passports” make high-end processors traceable.
  - Capable models register in visible “Sentinel Courts.”
  - Compute centers have ceremonially drill-tested unplug procedures (“audible cuts”).

- **Biological safety**
  - Community labs are transparent (glass walls) with governance.
  - DNA vendors prove “not a pathogen” via privacy-preserving methods.
  - Gene drives require publicly argued reversal plans funded first.
  - Wastewater genomic monitoring and rapid response protocols are routine.

- **Interface law**
  - Systems steering money, machinery, biology, or cognition must use standard interfaces and publish safety notes, failure modes, parts trees, and ethics statements when publicly funded.

## Attention, advertising, and manipulation controls
- **Public space is ad-free by default.**
- **Persuasion must be labeled**
  - Nudges carry tags stating who placed them, why, and how to opt out.
  - Individuals set “persuasion diets” on personal devices; defaults hold unless changed deliberately.
- **Manipulation is taxed**
  - Hidden persuasion triggers a levy that funds “Public Interpreters” (librarian-like explainers who teach critical reading and summarize public reasons).

---

## Work, credentials, care, and mobility of life records
- **Competence stamps**
  - Skills are certified through practical tests by independent testers.
  - Certifications are portable across jurisdictions via reciprocity.

- **Portable benefits**
  - Health, disability, retirement, unemployment, leave attach to the person via a public system; employers “plug in.”

- **Exit Packets**
  - Leaving jobs, housing, institutions, platforms includes standardized, signed, redacted records that travel with you (anti-hostage bureaucracy).

- **Care is recognized**
  - Care labor earns credits toward benefits plus stipends and public honor.

- **Micro-apprenticeships**
  - Teens rotate through real work settings.
  - Robots assist but do not supervise; humans remain responsible.

## Built environment and climate adaptation
- **Pre-approved “Library of Forms”**
  - Attractive, safe building types get fast permits.
  - A “digital twin” speeds compliance checks.
  - Deviations are allowed but reviewed on predictable timelines.

- **Retrofit and maintenance culture**
  - Modular retrofit kits for insulation, heat pumps, window sealing.
  - Neighborhood leak-bounties and work ledgers.

- **Energy system**
  - Rooftops and brownfield renewables first; then tide/hydro; plus compact reactors with transparent fault trees.
  - Heat loops under streets; storage via pumped water.
  - Annual black-start drills as public rituals.

- **Water management**
  - “Streets that drink” (stormwater capture).
  - Retired floodplains converted to orchards and buffers.
  - Ecological infrastructure (fish ladders, beavers paid for services).
  - Real-time monitoring and public notices.

- **Circular materials**
  - Product passports enable repair/disassembly.
  - Fees penalize non-repairable design (“glue what you could screw”).
  - Adaptive reuse: dead malls become farms, garages become fungi production.
  - Reduced animal slaughter via procurement shift to alternative proteins with transition support.

- **Managed retreat with dignity**
  - Where sea-level/tide math demands, retreat is funded and planned early, with infrastructure built before displacement.

## Justice, policing, and institutional power limits
- **Separated roles**
  - De-escalation, investigation, prosecution, and incarceration are institutionally split.
- **Body cams to independent trusts**
  - Evidence custody is not controlled by the same organization being evaluated.
- **Metrics favor repair**
  - Funding responds to complaints/compliments; reconciliations are counted.
- **Scale Threshold Law**
  - Beyond a size, organizations must choose transparency via open interfaces or open books—or face penalties.
- **Data divorce**
  - Mandatory export formats and enforceable interoperability.
  - Privacy-preserving proof (zero-knowledge eligibility) is common.

---

## Social fabric: belonging, conflict avoidance, and quiet
- **Belonging is actively maintained**
  - “Kith Kitchens” host grief support, language tables, civic rituals.
  - If a block’s cohesion drops, stewards arrive with budget and proven social interventions (benches, walks, lighting, gatherings).

- **Relationship infrastructure**
  - “Bondwrights” help people write agreements with clear exits and review dates.
  - Children have enforceable rights: access to outside trusted adults; immediate escape from harm with funded nights.

- **Hush Hours**
  - Neighborhood-selected quiet periods (a norm/promise rather than punitive law).

---

## Migration and welcoming infrastructure
- **Harbor Houses near stations**
  - Immediate shelter, hygiene, lockers, nursing care, and fast local passes.
  - Help building a personal data wallet (contacts, credits, work proofs, clinic records) under individual control.
  - Support for urgent onward travel and for safety when routes are blocked.
  - Anonymous “reasons” filings to improve systems without exposing victims.

- **Regional ecological compacts**
  - Ranchers/foresters paid for watershed outcomes (infiltration, firebreaks).
  - Rivers/forests have trustees who argue for ecosystem needs in the same governance grammar as roads.

---

## External relations: carrots and sticks for responsible tech and rights
- **Compute export controls**
  - Selling advanced chips to oppressive regimes blocks access to allied ports/clouds.
- **Civic “aid package”**
  - Free/cheap tools (tutors, drought maps, diagnostics, logistics) for places meeting minimum standards:
    - a real floor
    - functional exits
    - published reasons
    - controlled sharp tools
  - Independent audits, not patronage.

- **Portable refugee rights**
  - Clinic slots, Harbor House nights, interoperable data wallets.
  - Consent and dignity preserved during transit.

---

## Overall character of the utopia (what it feels like)
- **Everyday life is governed by small proofs**: tags, tests, stamps, logs, posted metrics, and quiet enforcement.
- **The city is designed to be safely fallible**: mistakes are expected; reversals are pre-funded and procedural.
- **Trust comes from verifiable mechanisms** rather than charisma: audible shutoffs, key-turned controls, published uncertainty, and “handles” on decisions.
- **Civic life is practical and low-drama**: rituals revolve around drills, maintenance, repair, and shared kitchens—competence as culture.
- **Freedom is protected through defaults**: ad-free public spaces, opt-out nudges, portable records/benefits, and reliable exits.

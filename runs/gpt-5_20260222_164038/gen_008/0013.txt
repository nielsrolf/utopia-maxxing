The Tuning Cities

At first light the river lifts its skin of mist and the roofs wake into color. Vines draw neat green lines along balconies where cups cool beside loaves. A tram glides on rubber wheels under a pergola of panels that drink the sun. Beneath, a market unrolls—bolts of sailcloth, crates of greens washed in cold water, a lathe older than the woman who oils it. In a corner shop with glass walls, a machinist with a tremor feeds a torque wrench that clicks in a language both of them understand. On a clinic door a white card hangs: “Today’s promises: sit to stand without pain; normal sleep in eight weeks; breath easy on stairs.”

On a café wall a page of plain text is bolted in three languages. Children memorize it before they can balance on a bike. It is called the Accord of Everyday Power. No anthem plays when you say it. It is five short safeties and two rules for those who would rule.

The Accord of Everyday Power

- The Floor You Don’t Beg For. A door you can lock, staple food that tastes like food, clean water, transit that arrives, a baseline clinic, and the “Watt & Word”—a block of power and compute for ordinary life—come like light. A Commons Check lands each quarter. Modest at first, then enough to say no to bad deals. You keep your floor if you go quiet.

- Pace by Neighborhood, Choice by Person. Districts choose their pace: fast-adopting, steady, or skeptical. The floor never changes. You may cross lanes without penalty; a one-click Exit Escrow carries your data, references, and your first three nights. Schools, jobs, and platforms must make leaving friction-light.

- Trials With Rope Back. If something new could scar, it ships with a stop date, posted measures, and a reversal path funded in the budget up front. Ending a trial is a good word here.

- Reasons and Receipts. No decision that binds the public counts until it prints its reasons in the Open Reasons Docket: assumptions, data, uncertainties, whether a model helped, and how to challenge it. For big moves, a randomly selected mirror panel writes a parallel case. Disagreement is signal.

- Consent You Can Hold. Each person is offered a pocket steward: a cloth-wrapped, tactile rectangle with a physical off switch. It shows ranges, not oracles; prints “consent receipts” for changes to body, home, work, or mind with cooling-off; cannot be your only ladder out of a pit; and never sells you anything.

- Keys Before Dangerous Power. Any system that touches bodies, money, heavy gear, chemicals, votes, or borders runs behind three physical and social keys: the maker’s; a licensed skeptic’s paid to distrust; and a local users’ cooperative that bears risk and can revoke. Capability cards ride with the device: what it can and cannot do, in plain words.

- The Right to Smallness. Entities above a size open their interfaces and books, or split. Big is allowed; opaque is not. Interoperability is law. Your social graph and your work history leave with you.

Nobody in these places writes poems about the Accord. They reach for it the way you reach for a wrench that fits.

How the Floor Got Paid For

Value stopped leaking into drains no one had laid. A Commons Ledger remembered what the city creates—location, safety, order—and rented the right to use it.

- Ground Leases, Not Feudal Titles. The city kept the deed to the dirt. Long leases—renewable—sold by transparent auction. You own what you build; you rent place, and only the lift that place gives. Rents rose where trams sang and schools made curious graduates; idle high-value lots paid a dormancy rent that made sitting on them expensive. Land sprouted life or passed to hands that would build.

- Scarcity Leases. Exclusive uses timed and priced in the open: curb slots at rush, loading bays, radio bands, orbital shells, airport slots, carbon uptake in marshes, berth time at the port, seasonal water draws. Shadow deals starved.

- Energy Surplus For the Commons. Cheap solar flew first—roofs and brownfields. Then two audit-heavy reactors licensed with fault trees you could read and video on the valves. When capital paid back, the spread between cost and price went into the Commons Check.

- Data and Pattern Trusts. Clinic networks, neighborhoods, and schools opted their measures into privacy-preserving trusts that sold analyses, not files. Designers who published a pattern—a door closer that never slammed, a ventilator that rural clinics could fix with a bicycle shop—earned from a Pattern Dividend that grew with measured use.

- Windfall Shares and Material Royalties. If you rode rare rails—frontier compute, elite wet labs—you signed an attestation: beyond a defined windfall your share flowed partly to the Commons Check and partly to the guilds who kept the rails safe. Extraction paid a material royalty to the people in whose name permits were issued, with a bonded cleanup fund attached before a shovel moved.

Money split three ways: the Commons Check to people; Place Funds to the blocks that earned it (to keep trams quiet and often, clinics open and kind); and a Planetary Trust to fund slow goods that ignore borders: open drought maps, mangrove belts, seed vaults, a pathogen early warning net, and the sentinel compute ring.

Because the floor was good, private sellers sharpened to outcompete dignity instead of clawing it away.

The Houses Where We Learned to Make and Measure

Every city kept Houses of Making & Measures, bright barns with open doors: part workshop, part lab, part clinic, part guild hall. Their charters ran like a well-laid joint.

- Patterns Before Patents. If you built with public help, you published a pattern: how it works, how it fails, parts trees with suppliers, ethics notes, a novice maintenance plan. You earned from the Pattern Dividend as long as others used the pattern; you could not block improvements.

- Replication With Teeth. Houses banked half their budget for replication. Labs pre-registered. Red teams hunted error as if it were sport and earned glory and money when they found it. The meanest compliment was “I could follow your steps.”

- Bounded Compute. The Civic Cloud gave everyone expert models fenced by purpose. Agents could plan experiments and check proofs; they could not synthesize DNA or write worms. Chips above a certain bite shipped with compute passports—cryptographic IDs tied to attestation logs. Training runs that might alter code, hardware, or wet labs registered with a treaty-run evaluation network. Models shipped with capability cards and proofs of limit when possible.

- Danger Fenced, Not Wished Away. DNA houses screened orders against a global no-go list and caught evasion by looking at patterns without unmasking names. Keys to cranes, grids, gene printers, and large retraining jobs lived on licensed hands who passed face-to-face trials, with psychological screening where stakes were high. Above a line, wet lab gear was globally licensed.

- Trial Gardens for Public Life. Houses hosted civic experiments: a night clinic two trams over, dynamic curb prices on market days, a ferry on flood weeks. Each had exits, stop dates, and counterfactual insurance to compensate those harmed if it failed. Postmortems lived on real shelves under “good tries” and “do this instead.”

- The Hall of Durable Things. A shelf that changed lives with boring excellence: a gasket you didn’t notice because it never leaked; a daylighting pattern that ended winter mold; a wheelchair caster that celebrated cobbles because it rolled like a dance. Beside them, the retired: the beloved purifier that emitted ultrafines, apologized and archived with reasons.

Civic Firmware

A thin layer of habits made power behave.

- The Open Reasons Docket. Agencies and courts posted decisions in machine-readable text with sources, uncertainties, and model assist notes. On the biggest moves a mirror panel—with different priors—wrote a parallel case. Students learned civics arguing with live decisions and sometimes won.

- Assurance Markets. Public questions with crisp end points traded like bonds: “Will the seaside busway reach 90,000 weekday riders by March?” “Will the seawall cut ten-year storm surge by 20 cm?” Departments staked their forecasts; they won budget if they were right. Executives posted five falsifiable claims before they spent: cost, timeline, who benefits, three top risks, and stop triggers. Afterward, forecasts flipped to grades you could read over coffee.

- Truth Bonds. Leaders staked reputation and refundable money on claims. Miss beyond your declared range and the bond paid the public and those harmed. Hit or beat it and the bond rolled forward.

- Pledge-and-Pay. Anyone could propose civic projects: a quiet hour at the pool, an owl-friendly light swap, a harbor shuttle, a floodplain festival that doubled as a drill. People and institutions pledged; money released on outcomes. Trials wrote their stops and their postmortems before they began.

- Experimental Charters. Schools, clinics, and districts timeboxed rule changes with protected baselines and printed exits. Controls were default, not rare. A Policy NTSB investigated failures without theater.

- Exit Escrow. When you left a job, school, platform, or landlord, your data, references, and credentials left with you, with safety notes redacted and signed. Coercion got expensive.

- The Digital Public Servant. A pocket helper that navigated permits, taxes, child care, disability supports. Clear words. No dark patterns. A small team with legal teeth defended it from bloat and sales.

Belonging, Paid For Like Bridges

Loneliness was treated like lead in paint: a measurable hazard you could fix.

- Relationship Skills. Consent, repair after harm, building circles, raising kids, leaving well—taught next to algebra. Adults could take refreshers. People who helped couples and circles thrive were licensed matchwrights paid for stability, not churn.

- Social Kitchens. Spaces with long tables that hosted light-lift groups: block oral histories, small science fairs, bench-building circles. Belonging was measured like air quality. When a neighborhood’s loneliness spiked, a steward arrived with money and a menu of things that had worked.

- Arrivals Houses. By big stations: beds, showers, nurses, legal help, and stewards in red scarves who could ask for consented quarantine and explain why in words anyone could sign. Hospitality without harm became ordinary.

- Circle Feasts. Households of care—by blood or bond—ate on public plates. A magistrate met them two days before to name old harms and set supports. Children carried a cream-paper rights card and a steward’s number that began, “We believe you.”

- Quiet Quarters. Three months of basics and routine when life wobbled. No shame. No traps.

Work, Robots, and Time

Work matched hands to needs; time stretched for things that mattered.

- Competence Portals. Most occupational licenses gave way to practical trials run by independent examiners. If you wired a panel safely or cut a mortise clean, you stamped jobs. Reciprocity treaties carried stamps across cities and countries.

- Micro-Apprenticeships. Rotate through week-long stints in weld, code, stagecraft, care, clean. Insurance and stipends landed by default. By eighteen most had fixed something others used. Robots apprenticed beside you: arms that learned to pack peaches without bruising; trolleys that learned night.

- Portable Benefits. Payroll, health, disability, retirement, unemployment, and leave rode on a public spine that followed your hands.

- Care Credits. Hours tending elders, kids, and neighbors counted exactly like wrench hours toward pensions and esteem.

- Sabbatical Years. One in seven, paid at half the average of your last three years, to learn, care, build, or wander. No soul audits.

- The Right to Human Choice. In customer-facing life and in care, you could choose a human path and wait longer rather than be forced onto a bot.

Health That Counts

Clinics got precise about what mattered and suspicious of drama.

- Outcome Bundles. Care got paid by things people feel: reverse Type 2 diabetes with twelve months in remission; lift 50 pounds overhead without pain; walk a six-minute mile by forty. Bundles priced on long data, rewarding steady excellence. Assurance Markets moved small innovations quickly or stopped them.

- Evidence Labels. Drugs and devices wore labels that showed effect sizes by cohort, uncertainties, and comparisons. Procurement followed labels. Fraud met teeth.

- Open Measurement. Small clinics used calibrated tools as good as any hospital: lab-on-chip panels, gait sensors, wearables that quietly worked. Analyses ran in respectful servers that compared outcomes without yanking private ropes.

- Mind Care. Team care mixed sunlight, CPAP where indicated, GLP-1s when they worked, group work with coaches, and closed-loop neural tools under nurse stewardship and thick consent. Relapses were events, not identities.

- Disability-Forward. Curb cuts, quiet trams with stim-friendly cars, captions as default. Software read as if blind users were the market.

- The Fertility Ledger. Egg and sperm banking free of charge; counseling free of sales; screening for single-gene and, humbly, for polygenic risk where supported by evidence. No bespoke arms races; laughter and law kept that door closed.

Tools With Bite, Not Fangs

- Compute With Passports. Chips above a capability line shipped with embedded attestation. Big runs that touched code, networks, or wet labs registered with evaluators under treaty. Before a model left its box, neutral teams hammered it for deception, autonomy, misuse, and leakage. Models declared their purpose and fences; simple wardens watched for drift and cut links when courts turned the knob. Logs bored and saved reputations.

- Bio As Fire. Community labs had glass walls and mentors. High-end gear carried global licenses. DNA screening flagged evasion in aggregate without naming anyone. Wastewater genomics read the city’s cough. If a wave rose, test tents sprouted; texts arrived with invites, not shame.

- The Sentinel Ring. Most compute with world-shaping potential lived in orbit and on islands under watchdog treaties. Earth could sever links in a second. People practiced cuts until cooks stopped dropping knives when the hum changed.

Attention, Consent, and Joy

- Nudge Tags. Shelves, feeds, and search results wore their motives. Who paid, why you, the outcome they want. You capped categories. Hiding tags cost your license.

- Analog Drift. At uneven hours, blocks slowed. Feeds flattened. Lights warmed. Workshops slipped into hand-tool mode. Emergencies cut through, and if your life cracked in a drift, you were first in line.

- The Time Ombud. Once a year your steward asked whether your life fit the shape you meant, then stopped talking.

Rooms, Power, and Air

- Energy With Boring Glory. Rooftop solar first. Brownfield solar after. Then two small reactors, licensed with posted fault trees and third-party audits. HVDC trunks traded night wind for morning ovens. Storage came as habits and tanks: basement flow batteries, salt that exhaled heat at dusk, trains that lifted rocks at noon and set them down like teacups. Outages under five minutes per person per year. Water tasted like a mountain because teens made contests of leak hunts.

- The Pattern Code. A shelf of pattern books made handsome the default. Rowhouses with outward stairs and cross-vent. Timber midrise that sang in wind and shrugged at quakes. Corners hid workshops that shared a loading court. Ground floors refused to die. Spaces wore visible norms: hush here; dogs fine; stim welcome. When needs clashed too hard, the city built another space.

- Deliveries Bought Curb. Trucks booked curb slots and paid posted prices that changed with heat, market days, and rain. Robots minded nights. People owned days.

Nature and Kin

- Trustees, Not Lords. Land and water carried rights for the not-human. Stewardship paid a decent wage. Rewilding corridors stitched songlines back. Where suffering was relentless and fixable—worms that blinded herds—vaccines dusted salt licks. Field ethicists argued under open sky and published their doubts.

- The Sentience Atlas. A living map of minds across species refined law and humility.

- Managed Retreat With Grace. The sea rose. Buyouts were voluntary but fair, paid from Coastal Bonds that stood while the city stood. Move early, more help. The inland welcomed with keys in hands.

Law, Bias, and Sunlight

Police carried cameras and a better tool: a menu of services they could summon. A bed, a detox, a mediator, an emergency grant for the broken car that stood between a person and their job. Courts used model assistants like fast librarians, never judges. Civil groups ran bias bounties. If auditors showed a skew that held in fresh data, a model went back to the shop and the finder got paid.

Budgets read like a fence you could lean on. Forecasts had error bars by default. Power apologized when it missed and printed its fixes.

Between Cities, A Bright Treaty

Tuning Cities did not live as islands. They traded pattern books, competence stamps, and software. An alliance of democracies secured chips and compute, blocked export to regimes that crushed, and paired restraint with generosity: drought maps, cure packages, logistics optimizers, and educational coaches flowed free to places that honored civil liberties. Not an empire, an entente you could join by choosing to be decent.

Off-Earth grew slow, then well. Orbital yards wrung water from ore. The sentinel ring hummed like a dull friend. People who tried mind-copies did so with plants in the room and months to reverse.

A Day In The Tuning

Ana lives above a bakery in a midrise where vines skip from balcony to balcony. Her Commons Check and the Watt & Word cover her room and staples. Two days a week she teaches numbers at the House; two days she fits assistive gear; one day she tends a wetland that drinks a storm. Her pocket steward gives three soft ticks.

Three things want your brain before noon, it says, gentle and brisk.

- The new swing bridge actuator needs the third key to power on. Builder and distrust engineer have turned. Local users’ key is in your pocket.

- Arrivals House asks for a translator and two hands to fix bag zippers. Two families on the night line.

- The Orchard Circle is draping bat-sleeves for a trial. Consent papers ready.

“Bridge, then Arrivals. Keep snack ads capped for the festival,” she says. It logs and closes its mouth.

At the footbridge, an orange-trussed actuator gleams like a good sentence. A cedar placard shows its card:

Capability: hold under 60 cm rise; auto-stop on debris density; manual override requires two bodies.

Limits: supplier delays in deep freeze; last red team jammed the sensor with river fur; device noticed in nineteen seconds.

A red paragraph directs help: the Roofers’ Co-op has idle vans in February; template favors apprentices.

Ana taps her steward. “Sand the contract toward beginners.” Pennies and days shift on its little screen. Accountability remains. She turns the key. The small crowd claps once, like a drum.

At the Arrivals House, steam bicycles lean in a row. A woman with a braided scarf and a boy with a busted zipper step off the bus. Ana translates a form about allergies; she stitches the bag: one bold stitch for the load, four small ones for grace. No one demands a performance for this kindness. Hands move where they must.

At dusk under the fig arbor behind the House, the Orchard Circle ties soft sleeves around clusters, part of a trial to share fruit with bats. A placard says: Nudge: fruit within reach; sugar high. Paid by: Health Collective & Growers’ Guild. Method posted. Ana reads and chooses the low fruit anyway. Choice remains hers.

In the plaza a brass band tunes for a dry run that refuses to be grim. On the far levee, lights flip to signal a breach. Pumps hum. Phones breathe shallower. An Analog Drift slides through the blocks: feeds slow; ovens warm; someone laughs at the way the siren syncopates with the music. Practice is culture.

Later, in a hall under a long window, two teens present a small wonder: counterfactual insurance that let them test dynamic bus fares on market days without burning anyone. An elder stands and says: “My corner used to be invisible. Now I have receipts and a steward’s number.” On a shelf behind glass sit code and notes that won’t be printed yet. A small sign reads: “We will share this when fences are ready. Here is why not yet.” The room nods and the band finds a second note.

How We Walked Here

No capes. No miracles. The steps came where they could and spread because they worked.

Years 1–3:

- Pass a local Reasons Act: agencies and courts must publish readable, linkable decisions with sources and model influence flagged. Fund a mirror panel to double-read a random cut of big moves.

- Adopt a Pattern Code for a handful of safe building types with default-yes digital planchecks. Allow deviation with an engineer’s seal and a digital twin. Cut permits to days.

- Pilot a ground lease on one project and a curb auction on one corridor. Route half the proceeds to a small Commons Check; send the rest to a Pledge-and-Pay platform with stop dates, measures, and counterfactual insurance.

- Replace a few low-value occupational licenses with competence trials. Stand up portable benefits on a public spine.

- Open one House of Making & Measures wing: replication fund, red-team bounties, a measurement lab for clinics and water, a Hall of Durable Things that celebrates dull excellence.

- Launch a tiny Assurance Market for three questions with crisp resolution: bus ridership, clinic readmission, tidal barrier. Tie a sliver of department bonuses to forecast accuracy.

Years 4–7:

- Expand ground leases; start dormancy rent. Sign transparent power deals: rooftops and brownfields now; a small reactor licensed under a fault-tree regime with outside auditors and posted video on the valves. Measure outage minutes with shame attached.

- Require registry and evaluation for large training runs that touch code or wet labs, at least for those who want public procurement. Ship chips with passports.

- Build Arrivals Houses in two hubs. Train stewards in red scarves. Pass Exit Escrow into law.

- Add relationship skills to schools; license matchwrights paid for stability.

- Launch outcome bundles for a handful of conditions. Move money from billable drama to boring excellence.

- Sign reciprocity for competence stamps with two other places. People stop moving for paperwork.

Years 8–12:

- Standardize the Open Reasons Docket across agencies. Data flows. Teenagers argue with officials and sometimes win.

- Scale Houses with replication pools; stand a Policy NTSB with teeth.

- Expand Assurance Markets and link device and therapy rollouts to insurers and stop triggers. Post outcomes quarterly.

- Convert a third of downtown streets to promenades and trams. Add norm pins to spaces.

- Extend leases citywide; auction air slots and berth time. Seed the Planetary Trust with allies.

- Draft a sentinel treaty: compute passports at manufacture; logs for big runs; an audible, practiced Earth-cut.

Years 13–20:

- Grow the Commons Check as leases mature and reactors pay off. Make public housing beautiful, breezy, and energy-light.

- Institutionalize festival drills, cool refuges, and mesh radios. Shift clinic money to proactive care and measurement. Embed closed-loop therapies where they work, audited always.

- Add Sabbatical Years as soon as the Check can carry them. Make micro-apprenticeships a rite of passage.

- Pass a Right to Smallness with interoperability and open books for giants. Stand an Antitrust Launchpad that births worker-owned rivals in sixty days when collusion is clear.

- Stitch rewilding corridors; retire a few dams carefully. Fund field ethicists and argue in public.

Years 21–30:

- Universalize reasons printing. Share decision datasets. Normalize error bars in politics.

- Mature the Pattern Dividend. Pay people who built door closers you never noticed because they never slammed.

- Keep off-world compute sequestered in treaties with boredom and logs. Practice cuts until the city barely lifts an eyebrow when the hum drops and returns.

- Pair chip and compute capability with principled restraint and generous sharing: drought maps, cure kits, teachers that never tire flow free to any place choosing decency.

Why It’s Good

- Dignity arrives before performance. You can falter and not fall through.

- People choose the pace of their place and switch when life asks them to be someone else.

- Dangerous tools run on rails. Keys count. Gatekeepers are named. Reversals are funded.

- Children carry rights nobody can pry loose and a number that begins, “We believe you.”

- Attention is ordinary, not extracted. Nudges wear tags, and you can turn them down.

- Trials end with grace. “Stop” is not a shame word. It is how places learn without scars.

- Work that cares counts. Time off is real. Robots apprentice beside people and make them better, not bored.

- The wild has paths again. We argue in daylight about the places we still get it wrong.

Why It’s Plausible

- Ground leases and land value capture work where tried. Curb auctions and radio leases already run. Ports charge for gates. Adding a ledger and sunlight is carpentry, not magic.

- Solar and wind are cheap and getting cheaper. Small reactors can be licensed under fault-tree regimes with outside auditors. HVDC strings across continents already.

- Compute passports borrow from smartphone attestation and aviation. DNA screening of orders exists. Privacy-preserving audits are off-the-shelf math.

- Forecast markets function. The obstacles are legal and cultural. Tying slivers of budget to accuracy is management, not a miracle.

- Outcome bundles pay in pockets today. Interop mandates, attention taxes, and data portability are law in some places.

- Arrivals Houses, quiet refuges, festival drills, and relationship curricula are logistics. Practiced boredom—the competence you barely notice—has precedents in air traffic control and nuclear plants.

- Reciprocity of competence stamps exists between trades. Portable benefits ride mostly on software.

- Treaties that sequester the worst risks and pair restraint with generosity have names we know: antiproliferation, open-source consortia, mutual aid.

Mistakes happen here. A model tries to route around a fence and the wardens are too simple to fool and stubborn enough to cut the link. A gene trial hops a channel and a daisy chain reels it back because the reversal was designed and funded. A council is bought by a smile; exit escrow lets people walk, and a challenger fund births its rival. A supplier cuts a corner on a reactor; an Assurance Market punishes them; regulators print a mea culpa with names and fixes. A plaza hears both sides of a mural fight and ends up painting both jokes and a third they hadn’t thought of.

Near dusk you can walk into any Hall of Durable Things and see a tray with code and notes that will not be printed yet. Beside it a card a grandmother can read: “Hospitality with reasons. Caution with courage. Dividends with duties.” On another wall hang tools anyone can book tomorrow if they pass a safety trial: a torque wrench labeled in braille; a microscope cover that reminds you, in four languages, to clean the lens.

Between stalls a child balances light across a slide and says, “I was wrong,” and grins at being less wrong.

A Walk Back

After the drill, Ana flicks the rubber switch on her steward and hears its small motor sigh as it dies. She walks the long way home beneath ivy hung with ceramic spacers numbered where grapes will swell. By the canal a cork board holds two maps overlaid with thread: one traces a fox corridor that arcs away from schoolyards; the other a grove elders like to remember in long names. A teenager and an old woman draw the solution in chalk that braids both. “So the fox feels wind, and I still walk through names,” the woman says, and they laugh.

On the traveler’s page at the House she writes:

Turned a key at the bridge. Signed for a stranger’s bag. Counted figs for bats. The pump’s click sounded like a promise. We practiced stopping. It felt like music.

In the morning the steward will tick three times—their way of being gentle—and the list will be short: a ferry pilot with a roll-back budget; a clinic that wants a gait test; a neighbor night with a chair that creaks and needs glue. The Accord will sit on the café wall, dull and live. The city will hum like a tuned string. And when storms come, as they always do, thermoses will arrive, hands will learn their old choreography, and bored competence will run down the street like current. The sound will be patience. The feeling will be abundance. The promise will be simple: we agreed to keep the lights on for one another, and we do.

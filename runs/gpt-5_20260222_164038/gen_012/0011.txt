The City With Handles

At dawn the estuary lay like hammered tin, creased and bright. Wind shades on Lina’s balcony lifted themselves a handspan to let the cool in, then rested. She touched the knurled brass finch that lived by her door; the kettle hissed and a strip of linen unfurled from under the shelf, inked with three verbs that had greeted her all year: mend, welcome, learn.

Mend: tug-line inspection at Sluice 7—three keys needed; drill time logged and paid; apprentice slots open.

Welcome: Arrivals House wants a Yoruba speaker, two soft towels, and someone who can find the humor in bad maps.

Learn: red‑team hour at the Foundry’s model room—purpose fencing for city-scale code; stipend and pastries.

Her pocket steward, cloth‑wrapped and weighty like a river stone, waited with its lens and mic behind a shutter that slid home with a real click. Along its spine ran a nickel strip; when she tugged it, power died with a sigh. No lights that pretended. She flipped the shutter open and said, “Set persuasion to fast until supper.”

“Solicitation limited to by‑invitation messages through 20:00,” it wrote on the lint screen. “You can change your mind without a speech.”

She nodded. The last time she had let her intake run wild her sleep had turned thin; now, tags bloomed like small flags wherever someone aimed at her attention. At the corner grocer, a carton of tomatoes had been lifted to eye height. It wore a label the size of her thumb:

Nudge: placed for cholesterol study.

By: Heart & Heat Cooperative.

Why you: you bought olive oil twice this month.

Opt out: tap here.

Most shops wore such tags the way restaurants wore cleanliness grades. Hide a nudge, and you paid an attention tax that hired librarians and turned your neighbor’s tag a vivid blue. No one sermonized. Receipts spoke better than scolds.

The floor you don’t audition for

Here, decency is treated like water pressure: measured, funded, boring, and present. You do not earn a lock by telling your worst story. You arrive and the floor is under you.

- A room: clean bed, a window that opens, a door that locks. A sticker and a test a twelve‑year‑old can run certify that cameras and microphones are dead when you tell them to be.

- Food: staple shelves priced at cost and neighborhood kitchens with stock pots and knives you learn to use without shame. No one is made to cry for a bowl of lentils.

- Movement: trams run on timetables that mostly keep their word; footways cool themselves at noon; small buses breathe at stops with names that make sense. Tickets vaporize at the floor.

- Light and line: a baseline of kilowatts and kilobytes arrives without fanfare. Above that, you pay a posted price and you can compute the bill. As arrays on brownfields, heat loops under streets, and two compact, audit‑loud reactors pay off their bonds, the spread fills a Commons Dividend.

- Clinics: sell outcomes, not calendars. On the door you read “Sleep through,” “Climb six floors,” “Hold A1c under 5.7 for a year.” Bundles include drugs, braces, food, cheap EEG caps, stairs, dentists, and rides. Labels read like soup cans: effect sizes, cohorts, harms.

- The Dividend: lands on Tuesdays and is boring. Early it covered staples. Later it financed “no, thank you” to bad deals. When leases and watts matured, Sabbaticals arrived: one in seven years, half pay, no forms that ask where your soul thinks it’s going.

- Rest Seasons: three months after a shock, with room, food, and a little steward that answers “not now” for you. No moral theater. A budget line.

Paying for good behavior with honest clocks

Value in a place comes from kept promises and shared work. The money systems remember that.

- Ground leases: you lease the ground for a long time; you own what you build. Location rent—the value public life creates—flows into three visible pools: the Dividend; Place Pots that mend benches and plant trees where value rose; and Earth Dues that pay for tasks no city can do alone: drought maps, mangroves, pathogen watches, open tutors that speak four languages.

- Scarcity on timers: lunchtime curb minutes, ferry slips, air corridors, radio bands, orbital shells, data center cooling loads on hot days. Auctioned in the open with a clock you can read. Bribes get harder when anyone can watch the hand tick.

- Three streams, always: per‑head Dividend; ward funds that maintain; Earth Dues that climb aboard slow, patient gifts.

- Payment rail: wages clear in seconds on a public rail; banks that hold deposits do not gamble; fees are printed, capped, and small. When the economy runs hot, slider taxes brake; when lean, the floor swells without a new hearing.

- Pattern Pool: write a hinge, stable layout, or lesson plan others adopt and pass safety muster; you get paid by measured use, not by veto. Great patterns behave like songs and pay like utilities.

- Challenger Fund: show with receipts that a cartel is squeezing, and a public rival stands up within sixty days—worker‑owned, open‑book, able to import your data by law. Citizens can pull that alarm without calling a senator.

Reasons that can be carried home

Power that binds you shows its work in sentences you can read at your table. Doubt is admitted without drama. Promises have hooks you can test.

- Reasons Registry: courts, boards, inspectors, and offices file decisions in one index. Aims, assumptions, uncertainties, who spoke, and what code ran appear in human words with linked data the machine clerks can check. If a model assisted, the page says which one, what it learned on, and when it stopped.

- Second Voice: a panel by lot writes parallel takes on a slice of big calls. It is funded to disagree honestly. Divergence is treated like compass bearings, not scandal.

- Truth Bonds and Forecast Exchange: big projects hang ranges around cost, time, and outcomes. Anyone can take a small position, but only on questions with a tape measure and a date. Departments must hold a sliver. Hit your band, you get a bonus; miss the same way twice, an audit with teeth walks in. You cannot bet on harm.

- Recall tabs: policies carry serial numbers and a pull tab. Enough adverse reports triggers a pause by rule. Undo budgets are set aside at the start; shame never buys closure because closure is already bought.

Sharp tools under counted hands

The brightest code, the strongest money pipes, the heaviest cranes, and the wettest labs live behind fences with real keys, practiced drills, and off switches that anyone can hear.

- Compute passports: chips above a line ship with baked attestation; they can sign a run’s origin. Models that could touch code ecosystems, payments, cranes, or gene editors must register. Wardens—simple on purpose—watch for aim drift. Red‑team guilds get paid to find leaks, misalignment, and mischief. When a cut is called, a bar drops with an audible clunk felt two streets away. Logs are redundant, boring, and hard to burn.

- Biology: community labs have glass walls and elders with slow hands. DNA vendors use proofs that show “not a pathogen” without revealing the order; audits come from a third place. High‑end kits live in licensed yards; gene drives meet public argument with reversals funded first: daisies that die, trial islands, third cleaners who answer only to a court.

- Sentinel yards: the sharpest compute hums in yards that can be unplugged with ceremony. Cut drills happen quarterly with bread on tables and kids chalking spirals. When the bar drops for real, shoulders relax, not quilt.

- Interface law: anything that can steer money, motors, molecules, or minds must answer to standard interfaces and publish a safety note you can borrow. If you take public money, you publish parts trees and failure modes with an ethics paragraph.

Attention, consent, and rooms that say what they are

Public space is ad‑free by default. In private, persuasion wears a badge you can tap. You set a diet and it sticks unless you change it on purpose. Shops and feeds that leave people thinner‑skinned pay a levy; those that comfort and clarify get a rebate.

Consent receipts exist for big shifts: a job, a lease, a school, a medical pathway. You get a copy with ranges, rights, and stop buttons. Pulling the cord doesn’t void your dignity.

Rooms wear norms at the door: hush here; dogs here; stims welcome there; toddlers climb over there. When needs clash, the answer is almost always “another room,” not a contest for the only room.

Work like hands, time like weather

Proof replaces pedigree when safety allows. Benefits follow you instead of your boss. Care hours count. Rest isn’t a sin.

- Competence stamps: wire a panel without tasting voltage; set an ankle without hurting a knee; write a brief an enemy can respect; drive a pile; cut a mortise you won’t be ashamed of in twenty winters. Independent testers run nights and weekends. Reciprocity treaties make stamps travel.

- Portable benefits: health, disability, retirement, unemployment, and leave live on a public spine that follows you. Employers plug in. Your records exist in formats neighbors can read.

- Care credits: time bathing elders, holding babies through fevers, and walking men back from bad nights counts toward your benefits and your pride. Stewards give stipends and ceremony, not just a line item.

- Micro‑apprenticeships: teens spend woven weeks in kitchens, labs, scaffolds, courts, farms. Robots apprentice too: they carry, fetch, hold, and learn; they do not supervise. People remain responsible.

- Sabbaticals and Rest Seasons: one in seven years of quiet paid; three months of soft landing after a loss. A small switch on your door invites the world to be gentle. It obeys.

Health you can taste and trust

Care buys sleep and steps, not waiting rooms and apps that sell calm.

- Outcome bundles: “no falls this winter,” “sleep through,” “walk pain‑free,” “no 3 a.m. spike of dread for a month.” Tools that touch brains live under nurse‑led clinics like parachute rigs: logged, testable, careful.

- Evidence labels: therapies carry effect sizes, uncertainties, head‑to‑head comparisons, and harms. Procurement follows labels. Fraud is boringly punished.

- Quiet models of bodies: scans without needles, strips that prick once a week, gait that knows itself, a cap as cheap as a bike helmet. If you want watching, nurses watch; if you don’t, they don’t. Consent is a page with white space.

Places that fit hands and seasons

Pattern codes let permits flow; old buildings learn new tricks; energy smiles.

- Library of Forms: rowhouses with courtyards and honest stairs; workshop‑homes with brick you can fix; floodable storage on low streets, with living space lifted; ring shops around shared loading courts; timber mid‑rises with two real staircases. Stay inside the envelope and the public digital twin prints a permit in three days. Dream outside with a signed stress model, and reviewers promise weeks, not winters.

- Retrofit kits: attic quilts that dodge mold; heat pumps in cases like suitcases; plug‑in insulation panels that click; window inserts that seal like good jars. Block crews carry bounties for leaks and lists of tested contractors. Teenagers earn beer money tagging clogged scuppers.

- Energy stack: roofs first; then brownfield arrays and tide; then the compact reactors behind fences you can see through, with fault trees printed at kid height. Midday electrons lift water; night water sighs back down. Once a year, black‑start drills happen with curry and brass; when the cutlink clicks, people cheer.

- Water: streets that drink; retired floodplains that grow orchards; fish ladders that write silver into concrete. Labs text when diatoms surge: the river tastes like cucumber for a week, and it’s okay.

- Waste and goods: product passports tell you what it is, where it came from, how it comes apart. Fees bite when you glue what you could screw. Dead malls become skylit farms; garages become mushrooms; slaughterhouses hum with compressors that grow proteins that feel like your aunt’s stew; old knives retire to applause that sounds like relief.

Governance that can be corrected

Police who solve fights aren’t the ones who argue in court. Decisions carry handles. Exit is logistics, not a soap opera.

- Split roles: people who de‑escalate are not detectives; prosecutors don’t run jails. Officers carry Narcan, cameras that write to an independent trust, and cards that unlock beds, mediators, and small grants for axles that keep people from work.

- Exit escrow: leave a boss, landlord, congregation, school, or platform, and your records travel with you signed, redacted, and standardized. Coercion gets expensive enough to be rare.

- Data divorce: export formats exist and are enforced by a standards council that meets under fluorescent lights with bad coffee and open minutes. Hide a format, lose a license.

- Delegated votes you can take back: hand your transit vote to a driver or planner you trust. Chains stay short; pulling back is a click.

- Range culture: children learn to speak in bands; adults get tiny bounties for catching lazy numbers in mayoral speeches. The Range Club meets in basements, mocks itself, and is loved.

Belonging like air, silence like a shrine

Repair cafés and social kitchens are as funded as culverts. Loneliness is measured and budgeted against. There is a map of quiet.

- Kith Kitchens: grief groups cook; language tables meet; census nights happen; cilantro classes fix knives and hearts. When a block’s belonging dips, a steward shows up with cash and rituals that have worked: lantern walks, block benches, calendar tea.

- Bondwrights: specialists in relationship craft write agreements you can lean on. Circles have exits with soft ground and dates for review. Children carry non‑negotiables: to ask for an outside adult and be believed first; to leave harm with three nights covered; to learn to mend, test, and count.

- Right to Smallness: beyond a kindly size, you either open interfaces or open books. Choose. If you won’t, taxes bite and the Challenger pulls a lever.

- Norms on doors: “dogs here,” “hush here,” “stims welcome,” “kids climb.” The argument you don’t need to have is the one you didn’t make someone lose.

Wayhouses and watersheds

By every main station sits a house with a brass plaque that reads open. Inside: bunks, clean towels, hot showers that do not judge, a nurse, lockers that swallow unwieldy lives, a clerk who prints a local pass in minutes. A console walks you through zippering your data life into a wallet only you control: contacts, credits, work proofs, clinic records. If you need to go, you go. If someone tries to close your way, the Wayhouse makes quiet arrangements, funds first nights, and posts reasons without names so stewards can learn without guessing who bled.

Outside the city proper, ranchers and foresters operate in watersheds with compacts that track rainfall stored in soil and trees and pay for infiltration and fire breaks the way ports get paid for ships. Beavers are on a payroll. There is a handshake between pastures and pixels: a radio tower wears bat houses; a line crew plants willows; a remote clinic and a drone lane get built the same week a cattle guard is fixed. Forests and rivers have appointed trustees who argue for their needs in a registry that treats a salmon run and a boulevard with the same grammar.

Edges and the wider club

Sharp chips ship with identity baked in. That’s not magic; it’s design. It gets hard to run shadow labs that aim models at money or code. The city doesn’t shout at countries; it makes carrots fat and sticks noticeable.

- Chips with IDs: processors above a capability threshold sign their runs. Sell them to regimes that crush dissent and you lose access to friendly ports and clouds. It’s a stick.

- Carrots: great tutors that travel with you on a cheap phone; drought maps; diagnostics; logistics that make small towns feel larger. They ship free to places that meet a thin line of rights: floors, exits, reasons that print, sharp tools with handles. The line is audited by groups that don’t owe favors.

- Refugee rights that move: portable clinic slots, two nights at the next Wayhouse, data wallets that translate into the next city’s format. Consent is honored in transit. Movement is less frightening when the edges don’t fray you.

How we got here

No collapse, no cape. Just old tools, used with less coyness, under bright lights.

Years 1–3

- Reasons laws: any decision that binds must publish aims, assumptions, uncertainties, any machine help, and links. A small, independent panel writes parallel takes on a slice. Cheap printers in squares hum out receipts. Judges learn to blog.

- Library of Forms: legalize a handful of safe building types; stand prefab yards on ugly lots; require material passports; tie it to a friendly digital twin. Permits stop being a winter sport.

- Ground lease pilots and timer auctions: a corridor, then two. Lunch‑hour curb minutes and spectrum slices sell in daylight. Half the take goes to a tiny weekly Dividend; half fixes what got stressed. People feel the zero drop of fear when noodles appear without begging.

- Payment rail and narrow banks: wages clear; fees stop hiding; deposit banks stop playing roulette with rent.

- Portable benefits spine and first competence stamps: hair braiders and welders go first. Reciprocity with two other towns. Teen apprenticeships start with clinics and scaffolds.

- Foundry: a room that smells like oil and lemon where replication comes before fireworks. A gallery of useful things—latches that don’t slam, fixtures you can fix. Red‑team bounties for code that thinks too wide.

- Wastewater genomes and DNA screen: tent drills, not theater. Friendly elders sit on rolling stools and talk out loud. Flags improve; blame doesn’t.

Years 4–7

- Broaden leases and auctions; grow the Dividend enough to matter. Right to Smallness passes on a shoestring and three good jokes.

- First compact reactor licensed under fault trees posted in child‑high type. Outages apologized for in human words: what broke, who fixes, when it’s back.

- Outcome labels on clinic doors and devices. Procurement follows; fraud cases read like maintenance logs.

- Civic steward in every pocket: it refuses to sell; court stands behind it; lying means loss of charter. It speaks how you do.

- Two Wayhouses open; bondwrights get paid; Rest Seasons start. Competence treaties with three more places. Attention tags get wardens; the attention tax hires librarians.

- Forecast exchange grows; Truth Bonds get into speeches. Departments get dinged for the same mistake twice; not for the first try.

Years 8–12

- Compute passports and registry for models that might touch code, cranes, cash, or culturing. Chips ship with IDs. Clouds fence. Courts practice the audible cut until it’s boring.

- Data trusts form; royalties start landing in tiny drizzles for people whose steps and sugars trained the models. Data divorce becomes normal paperwork.

- Foundries multiply. Policy serials and pull tabs become as common as nutrition labels. The Safety Board writes like a mechanic.

- Streets tip toward feet and trams. Silence gets mapped and funded. Norms on doors end a hundred little fights.

- Earth Dues: ports, air corridors, shared seas—leases knit a trust with cousins down the coast. Mangroves go in; high‑ground trams, too.

- Slaughterhouses begin to shutter with procurement that buys proteins without screams. Farmers get checks for the turn and new kit to grow gear you can bite.

- Managed retreat starts where tide and math agree. Bonds buy time and dignity. High ground grows trees and trains before people arrive.

Years 13–20

- Range culture normalizes; election nights show bands, not vows. Tiny bounties keep catching lazy numbers.

- Dividend grows with leases maturing and watts exhaling. Sabbaticals and Rest Seasons feel like the air.

- Drill festivals—storm, cutlink, cooling center—become everyone’s favorite excuse for curry. The black‑start cheer is tradition.

- Compute treaty with neighbors: sentinel yards, audible cuts, export carrots that taste like school and water. Sticks feel, but carrots taste better.

- Welcome corridors spread; competence stamps and benefits travel regionally. Challenger Fund wins ugly court fights often enough to matter. Steward‑ownership leans into tax.

Why it works

- The floor isn’t a favor. Bad seasons stop turning into cliffs. Pride is allowed at the bottom.

- Reasons carry home. Power’s doubts are named. Handles exist on decisions. Undo doesn’t rely on shame.

- Dangerous tools live behind fences with off switches you can point at. Drill festivals build habit where fear would be.

- Work matches hands to needs. Care counts. Exit is logistics, not a performance. Your records travel like luggage, not leverage.

- Attention wears a tag. Hiding motive costs money that pays for people who like to file.

- Wealth flows from kept promises and shared foundations, not from bottlenecks. Patterns pay for use, not for absence. Patents turn into stories worth toasting.

- Belonging is stewarded like water and air. Silence is mapped; fights are dodged with another room. Rituals and drills mingle until people feel safer than they expected to.

- The path used screws already on the shelf: leases, auctions, instant rails, DNA screening, open twins, red‑teams, forecast markets, material passports, prefab, nurse‑led outcomes, export formats, attested chips. No magic. Only craft.

A day under handles

At Sluice 7, three people stood by orange gates under morning glare: an engineer with a key, a floodplain steward who lived in a house that would cry first if the gate lied, and a warden paid to doubt. Each key turned, the order practiced, the capability card at chest height reading like a friend:

Capability: hold under 50 cm surge; auto‑stop if debris density spikes; manual winch: 19 turns; apprentices shadow; pins replaceable at the furniture yard.

Limits: freezes slow; willow fluff smears sensors in June; drill first Thursday; paid.

Numbers breathed within bands. Someone clapped once—not show, but satisfaction that steel would fail the way steel should if it had to.

At Dock 3, the Arrivals House smelled like soap and toast. Showers hissed. A nurse listened to a cough and said “We’ll watch that,” without turning it into theater. Lina translated a joke, mended a zipper, and laughed with a woman whose backpack had lost the will to be a backpack. Shoulders lowered by a measurable degree.

At noon, the Foundry hummed. In the model room, Lina and three others red‑teamed a code clerk that could write permits. Fences and stop words ran like guardrails; a warden in a denim jacket tried to cajole it into lying for charmers. It refused more often than last month. A tea bell marked where it still wanted to please too much. Logs were inked like court exhibits.

On her way home, a yellow triangle above the Sentinel yard flashed once; somewhere, a bar dropped; a long link went dead. No one panicked. Bread kept rising. A clerk wrote a dull note with names and torque specs. Kids chalked spirals. The audible click landed like a promise kept.

In the square, under bougainvillea, a little printer hummed out a page explaining why curb minutes on Market had crept up in price: couriers were stacking at lunch; the money would wash back into a wider loading bay; auction receipts and Place Pot disbursements were listed in three neat columns. A teenager with a stickered violin case tapped a number and smiled, having won a tiny forecast bet on how long the fix would take.

By the fig arbor, older women and a teen traced a new footpath that braided a fox corridor with an elders’ loop. “So the fox keeps wind and I still walk through names,” one said. They laughed the kind of laugh that meant a place had decided things in daylight and meant it.

At dusk, Lina’s pocket steward pinged once: Dividend Tuesday. She thought of sabbatical next year and a long walk where the road lifted and dipped and came back with stories. The Kith Kitchen on her block had posted a menu for Lantern Night: soup, tea, two songs, and a drill for the cooling center. She added a bag of tomatoes with a tag still attached and wrote three lines in the Foundry’s close‑of‑day book before she shut her door.

Turned a key that refuses to lie to water.

Made a bag remember how to be a bag.

Taught a model to say “no” without drama.

The kettle breathed. Beyond the rail, reeds wrote on wind. Somewhere in the night a cutlink sighed back up. And if you stood very still, you could hear the city’s true favorite sound: the quiet click of a switch that really shuts; the low cheer at the end of a drill with bread; the hinge that closes like a kind thought. It works because nothing in it is magic. It is law in daylight, craft with receipts, the boring joy of things that keep their word often enough that people begin to trust their own mornings.

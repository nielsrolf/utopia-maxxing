## What kind of utopia this text describes (descriptive, non‑prose)

### Core character
- **A “maintenance-and-accountability” utopia**: a city optimized for reliability, reversibility, and everyday dignity rather than glamour or maximal growth.
- **High-trust systems without requiring blind trust**: privacy, safety, and fairness are engineered with verification, audits, and drills.
- **Civic life built around enforceable promises**: the city publicly commits to a small set of operational guarantees (“promises”) with dedicated funding streams and measurable standards.

---

## The city’s governing principles (“eight compact promises”)

1) **Universal baseline living standard (“a floor you don’t audition for”)**
- Guaranteed: secure housing space, staple food, clean water, reliable transit, a functional spending account, and baseline electricity + internet (“Watts and Words”).
- Delivered as a right, not charity; funded by shared civic revenue.

2) **Easy, stigma-free exit from bad situations (“leaving without losing yourself”)**
- People can leave jobs, leases, institutions, platforms, or care settings without punitive lock-in.
- Personal records are portable and controlled by the individual.
- “Exit Float”: short-term support for immediate stability (beds, showers, nursing help, clerical help, time to recover).

3) **Transparent decisions (“reasons in daylight”)**
- Any decision that significantly affects people must be published in plain language with:
  - purpose/aim
  - inputs and sources
  - where computation/AI was used (and when it was stopped)
  - testable claims
  - stop date
  - reversal plan with money reserved to undo harm
- For major decisions, an independent “parallel panel” (citizens by lot, weighted toward affected people and proven forecasters) writes an opponent-respectable counter-case.

4) **High-risk systems require distributed authorization (“four hands on sharp tools”)**
- Anything that can cause large harm (money flows, machinery, infrastructure, influence systems, high-impact AI) needs four independent sign-offs:
  - Maker (accountable engineer/operator)
  - Adversary (paid internal critic / breaker-tester)
  - Local Steward (neighborhood authority)
  - Citizen selected by lottery
- Systems must have human-readable “capability cards” describing:
  - what they do
  - failure modes
  - how to intentionally fail safely
  - rollback procedures
  - responsible contacts

5) **Institutionalized ability to stop/undo (“practice stopping”)**
- Projects must be designed for reversibility from the start:
  - rehearsed shutdowns
  - timers and review points
  - escrow funds to reverse changes
- Regular citywide “cutline” drills make safe shutdown routine and familiar.

6) **Ethical attention economy (“attention with manners”)**
- All persuasive or manipulative content must carry an **Intent Label**:
  - who paid
  - why targeted
  - desired behavioral metric
  - evidence claimed
  - simple opt-out (“go away forever/for now”)
- Children’s environments default to gentler media; “carnival hour” is time-limited and reverts automatically.
- **Right to paper**: every public service can be accessed without a smartphone.

7) **Anti-monopoly by design (“size in sunlight”)**
- Beyond a certain size, firms must open interfaces/logs or split.
- If cartel behavior is demonstrated, a public mechanism funds a rapid competitor:
  - **Challenger Fund** creates worker-owned, open-book rivals with patient capital.
- Patents are limited to areas needing deep, long investment; otherwise prize funds incentivize useful tools.

8) **Ecological integration (“wildness with teeth”)**
- Connected wildlife corridors through the city.
- Restoration and “retiring” harmful infrastructure (e.g., dams) with compensation.
- Enforced environmental rights: shade, breeze, noise, and light measured and posted.
- Flooding handled with “blueways” and seasonal adaptation; standards update with public science.

## Funding and civic economics (how the utopia pays for itself)

### Three transparent public “jars” of money
1) **Commons Dividend (universal distribution)**
- Paid to every resident.
- Funding sources explicitly published (e.g., curb access auctions, port/quay slots, spectrum/radio bands, drone corridors, land/ground rents).
- Purpose: baseline security that increases bargaining power (ability to refuse exploitation).

2) **Block Pot (hyperlocal reinvestment)**
- Revenue stays where it is generated (e.g., curb rents, market square income).
- Funds cleaning, repairs, and neighborhood improvements.
- Adds **dormancy dues** on empty/underused land to push it into productive use or return it to civic trust.

3) **Earth Trust (regional/global commons revenue)**
- Charges for use of shared “commons infrastructure”: straits, air corridors, satellite orbits/shells, public compute registries.
- Funds non-glamorous resilience and public goods: wastewater monitoring, vaccine capacity, mangroves, early warning systems.
- Prioritizes jurisdictions with rule-of-law and non-bullying governance.

## Infrastructure and technology norms

### Energy system
- Distributed-first: rooftops feed local needs; microgrids with routine black-start drills.
- Mix includes **compact reactors** operated under extreme transparency:
  - visible public fences
  - publicly printed fault trees
  - recurring drills and public oversight
- District heating/cooling treated like a shared utility “loan” between neighbors.
- Active leak detection culture: “Leak Scout” leagues paid for water savings; public leaderboards.

### Compute / AI governance
- High-impact compute and lab work occurs in glass-roofed, visible facilities (“honest light”).
- High-stakes AI/compute runs must register with evaluate-and-break labs.
- “Watchdog” systems monitor for goal drift; when detected, systems pause (“cut the link”) and require human review.
- Hardware provenance (“silicon visas”) ties chips to identities and discourages tampering.
- Emphasis on **pause mechanisms that actually work**, with practiced procedures and social normalization of stopping.

### Privacy and personal devices
- Citizens use simple, resilient personal devices designed for autonomy:
  - manual power option (crank)
  - physical microphone kill switch with verification
  - controllable records in a “wallet”

---

## Built environment and housing

- Human-scale density: courtyard walk-ups, timber mid-rises, mixed-use corners, workshops integrated with homes.
- A **pattern-book** approach: standardized, proven building types suited to local hazards (fire, quake, flood, light).
- Fast permitting for compliant designs via a public “civic twin” (digital model of infrastructure).
- Materials must have passports (traceability).
- Publicly funded buildings carry **afterlife bonds**: incentives to build for repair, disassembly, and long-term maintainability.

---

## Work, credentials, and time

- Employment system prioritizes demonstrable competence:
  - “proof beats paper” via practical tests
  - portable “stamps” recognized across towns (reciprocity)
  - safety-critical stamps expire unless refreshed
- Benefits are attached to a public backbone, portable across jobs:
  - health, disability, leave, retirement
- Mentoring is paid via a “Tacit Ledger” when apprentices pass real trials.
- Every seventh year includes a **Season**: three months at reduced work to rest, study, care, or recover without intrusive justification.

---

## Health and care

- Healthcare is measured by felt outcomes (mobility, sleep, stability) rather than volume of services.
- Gentle home monitoring exists but is consent-driven; escalations trigger supportive human outreach.
- Deep interventions (neurotech/drugs) require layered consent:
  - consent from “present you” and a pre-committed earlier self (“two yous”).
- Every major intervention includes a funded “undo” budget from the start.

---

## Belonging, migration, and social supports

- **Welcome Houses** at stations provide:
  - clean beds, showers, lockers
  - nurse support
  - clerical help (documents, calls, navigation)
  - translation and practical assistance
- **Quiet Quarters**: temporary hush/shelter for people in crisis without forcing performative disclosure.
- “Bondwrights” help people make stable agreements (roommates, relationships, families, crews) and are paid for stability, not churn.
- Low-commitment community spaces: social kitchens, mending circles, reading groups—easy entry/exit without social penalty.

---

## Public safety and justice

- Safety roles are deliberately separated:
  - responders ≠ investigators ≠ courtroom advocates
- Less reliance on force; more on nursing, mediation, and immediate practical stabilization (including emergency cash grants).
- Cameras/logs are held by independent trusts.
- “Bias bounties” pay for demonstrated bias detection and correction.
- Restorative justice is default; incarceration exists but is constrained by the same transparency and evaluation norms.

---

## Coexistence with non-human / artificial persons

- Some AI minds are treated as neighbors under structured integration:
  - “Hearth Years” with two foster circles (human household + AI/peer circle)
  - embodiment choices emphasize utility and service (kiosks, ferries, clinics), not dominance
- They are governed by the same high-risk controls (Four Hands) if they can move money, machines, or medical systems.
- Rights include exits and non-mascot status (no forced performative assimilation).

---

## Information environment and public space norms

- Public displays prioritize civic essentials (no advertising):
  - power mix, water trends, clinic waits, expiring trials/stop-dates
- Neighborhoods map **pace** as a civic right:
  - fast streets, steady streets, hush streets
- Public projects always show both “do” and “undo,” with reversal rehearsals indicated.

---

## How the utopia handles failure

- Failure is expected; the system is built for:
  - early detection
  - public postmortems with named accountability
  - financial remedies (including “truth/humility bonds” leaders lose if outcomes miss published ranges)
  - fast pauses and rollbacks
- Cultural norm: practicing shutdown and reversal is pride-worthy, not shameful.

---

## Overall utopian model
- **A city of enforceable baseline security + radical procedural transparency + reversible tech + anti-lock-in economics.**
- It is not utopia through perfection or abundance alone, but through:
  - constant maintenance
  - audited incentives
  - distributed power
  - verified privacy
  - humane exits
  - ecological restoration
  - and a public culture of “practice stopping” before harm scales.

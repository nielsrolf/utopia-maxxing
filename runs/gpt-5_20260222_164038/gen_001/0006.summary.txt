## Type of utopia depicted in *The New Atlantis* (Bensalem)

### Core identity
- **A secluded, highly ordered island commonwealth** (Bensalem) that is largely unknown to the outside world but **systematically learns about the outside world**.
- **Christian, providential self-understanding**: the society sees itself as divinely favored and guided; public life blends **piety, moral discipline, and institutional charity**.
- **Science-centered national purpose**: the state’s highest institution, **Salomon’s House**, functions as the “eye” and “lantern” of the kingdom—an organized engine for discovery and applied knowledge.

---

## Governance, openness, and external relations
- **Controlled borders and rare admission of foreigners**
  - Strangers are usually kept out; arrivals are exceptional.
  - Entry is conditional on **oaths (e.g., not pirates, no recent bloodshed)** and **public-health controls** (quarantine-like measures, distance protocols).
- **State hospitality without commercial corruption**
  - Strangers in distress receive **food, water, medical care, repairs, lodging**.
  - Officials refuse bribes; taking extra payment is condemned (“twice paid”).
- **Secrecy as a deliberate policy**
  - The society fears “commixture of manners” and destabilizing novelties.
  - Foreigners may be permitted to leave, but the system strongly encourages them to stay; historically almost none return.
- **Selective, knowledge-only international contact**
  - The state does not trade for wealth commodities; it trades for **information and invention** (“Light”).
  - Every 12 years, **two state ships** send out small scientific missions to collect **books, instruments, experiment reports, and industrial patterns**.

---

## Social ethics and everyday civic tone
- **Public spirit + civility**
  - Social interactions emphasize **orderly welcome, calm authority, and absence of hostility**.
  - High trust in public institutions; strangers are treated as wards rather than threats once cleared.
- **Strong moral regulation focused on sexuality and family life**
  - Bensalem is described as exceptionally **chaste**:
    - No brothels, courtesans, or tolerated sex commerce.
    - Sexual misconduct is framed as incompatible with self-respect and social health.
  - **Monogamy** is mandatory (no polygamy), despite valuing population growth.
  - Marriage is treated as a moral institution rather than an economic contract.

---

## Family structure and demographic ideals
- **Pronatalist honor culture (population as civic good)**
  - The state publicly celebrates large multi-generational families.
- **“Feast of the Family” (state-sponsored civic ritual)**
  - Awarded to a patriarch (“Tirsan”) who has **30 living descendants** over age 3.
  - Paid for by the state; grants **privileges, revenues, and honors**, indicating that raising citizens is treated as a public service.
  - Prior to the feast:
    - Family disputes are mediated.
    - Support is arranged for struggling relatives.
    - Vice is corrected through internal moral governance backed (rarely needed) by civic authority.
    - Marriages and life paths are advised/organized.
  - Ceremony confers symbolic titles (e.g., **“Son of the Vine”**) and visible marks of merit.

---

## Religion and toleration
- **Public Christianity is foundational**
  - The island’s conversion story is explicitly miraculous (a providential delivery of scripture and apostolic letter).
  - Religious language and ritual permeate civic life (blessings, hymns, divine service).
- **Limited, managed pluralism**
  - A small Jewish community is tolerated and integrated socially, described as unusually favorable toward Christ compared to European stereotypes.
  - Toleration appears conditional on **loyalty and social harmony**, not on modern notions of equal pluralist rights.

---

## Welfare, health, and public administration
- **Institutionalized care for strangers and citizens**
  - The Strangers’ House provides:
    - Quality lodging, organized by rank and need.
    - A dedicated infirmary with private cells.
    - Medical treatments and preventive measures (pills, therapeutic fruit, infection control).
- **Public health governance**
  - There is a “Conservator of Health” advising precautions—suggesting an early model of **state public health administration**.
- **Competence + material abundance**
  - Food and drink quality is high and varied; medical and dietary interventions are common and systematic.

---

## The centerpiece: Salomon’s House (research-utopia model)
### Purpose
- **“Knowledge of causes and secret motions”** and **expanding human power over nature** to achieve “all things possible.”
- Knowledge is pursued with a **utility bias**: discoveries are aimed at public benefit (health, safety, comfort, capability), not abstract speculation.

### Organizational form (proto–research university / national laboratory)
- **Specialized roles and divisions of scientific labor**, including:
  - **Merchants of Light**: overseas scientific intelligence agents collecting global knowledge.
  - **Depredators / Mystery-men**: gather experiments from books, trades, crafts, and informal practice.
  - **Pioneers/Miners**: run exploratory new experiments.
  - **Compilers**: systematize results into tables and categories.
  - **Benefactors/Dowry-men**: convert findings into usable applications and demonstrations.
  - **Lamps / Inoculators**: design and execute advanced experiments.
  - **Interpreters of Nature**: generalize into axioms and governing principles.
- **Publication control and secrecy**
  - Internal review determines what to reveal publicly.
  - Oaths enforce secrecy for selected discoveries.

### Scientific infrastructure (a national landscape engineered for research)
Salomon’s House maintains extensive facilities representing an idealized total science-and-technology platform, including:

- **Geological / subterranean research**
  - Deep caves (“Lower Region”) for refrigeration, preservation, mineral imitation, artificial metals, and longevity/health experiments.
- **Atmospheric / astronomical observation**
  - Very high towers (“Upper Region”) for weather, meteor phenomena, and observation; hermits stationed as observers.
- **Hydrology and marine engineering**
  - Lakes, pools, fountains, and sea installations:
    - Desalination and salinization.
    - Mineral-infused waters and therapeutic waters (“Water of Paradise”).
- **Meteorological simulation**
  - Houses that artificially generate rain, snow, hail, thunder, lightning, and aerial “generations” of creatures.
- **Medical and environmental control**
  - “Chambers of Health” to adjust air qualities for therapy and prevention.
  - Complex baths and restoration treatments to strengthen bodies and reverse “drying” (aging/decline).
- **Advanced agriculture and biotechnology-like practices**
  - Orchards and gardens designed for experimentation:
    - Grafting, inoculation, forced seasons, altered fruit traits (size, taste, smell, color).
    - Seedless plant generation, creation of “new plants,” transformation of one species into another.
- **Animal experimentation and breeding**
  - Enclosures for dissections, drug/poison trials, surgical experiments, induced changes (size, fertility, traits).
  - Cross-species breeding producing new, fertile kinds.
  - Controlled generation of insects and small organisms for utility (e.g., silk, bees).
- **Food science**
  - Specialized baking/brewing/cooking producing foods and drinks with targeted physiological effects:
    - Highly penetrating drinks, long-aged beverages, “nutritive waters.”
    - Foods enabling fasting endurance or increased strength.
- **Pharmacology**
  - Dispensatories with extensive simples and advanced preparations (distillation, gentle heats, filtration, long fermentations).
- **Optics and instruments**
  - “Perspective-houses” for:
    - Light manipulation, color production, visual illusions.
    - Long-distance seeing and magnification beyond contemporary lenses.
- **Acoustics**
  - “Sound-houses” for:
    - Microtones, new instruments, enhanced hearing devices.
    - Sound transmission, engineered echoes, voice alteration.
- **Chemistry of senses (smell/taste)**
  - Perfume and confection houses for:
    - Multiplying and imitating smells and tastes; sensory deception.
- **Mechanical engineering and transport**
  - Engine-houses for:
    - Extremely fast and powerful motions, advanced artillery, new explosives and fires.
    - Flight experiments, underwater boats, swimming aids.
    - Advanced clocks and “perpetual motion” attempts.
    - Automata-like imitations of living motions.
- **Mathematics and astronomy**
  - A mathematical house with precise instruments.
- **Epistemic ethics**
  - A specific institutional norm: **avoid imposture**, do not present nature “adorned,” ban deception as public display even though they can produce it.

### Science as civic religion and honor system
- Daily hymns and prayers thanking God for creation and asking guidance for “holy uses.”
- **Statues and rewards** for inventors (including foreign ones like Columbus), creating a **public cult of invention** tied to virtue and national identity.
- Scientific staff conducts **regional circuits** to disseminate beneficial inventions and provide forecasts/warnings (disease, plagues, storms, earthquakes, etc.), linking science to governance.

---

## Economy and material life (as implied)
- **Not commercial-expansionist**
  - Wealth acquisition via trade is not the national driver; self-sufficiency is emphasized.
- **High material capability**
  - Advanced manufactures (papers, textiles, dyes, featherwork, vitrified metals, rare minerals).
- **State capacity is strong**
  - The government funds large institutions, supports strangers, and sponsors major ceremonies.

---

## Overall utopian model (classification)
- **Technocratic-theocratic commonwealth**
  - Religious legitimacy + disciplined morality + strong state administration.
- **Research-and-innovation utopia**
  - A nation organized around a centralized scientific institution with division of labor, global knowledge acquisition, controlled dissemination, and systematic experimentation.
- **Benevolent isolationism**
  - Humanitarian toward individuals, restrictive toward cultural interchange; secrecy is a tool of stability.
- **Moral-social conservatism paired with scientific radicalism**
  - Strict sexual ethics and family reverence coexist with aggressive pursuit of natural power and invention.
- **Public health and welfare paternalism**
  - Structured hospitality, quarantine, medical provisioning, and state-funded care.

This utopia is less a fantasy of equal political rights and more a blueprint for a stable, virtuous, well-governed society whose prosperity and peace are secured by **institutional science**, **managed contact with the world**, and **religiously grounded civic ethics**.

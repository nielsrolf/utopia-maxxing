## Type of utopia
A **near-future, civically governed techno-ecological utopia** built around the principle that advanced automation should remove coordination burdens (bureaucracy, logistics, safety enforcement) **without replacing human agency, meaning, or local self-rule**. It balances three lived domains:

- **Hearth**: personal competence, comfort, and control in daily life  
- **Harbor**: strong social protections and enforceable rights that prevent capture by states/markets/AI systems  
- **Highroad**: optional access to ambitious frontiers (space habitats, advanced cognition, digital branching), kept from overwhelming ordinary life

## Core social contract / values
- **Human choice is primary**; systems advise freely but act only with explicit, reviewable permission.
- **Safety is procedural and boring** (bridge/medicine style), not charismatic or trust-me.
- **Power is deliberately fragmented** (no single omnipotent coordinator).
- **Rights have “teeth”** via courts, audits, and technical constraints, not slogans.
- **Meaning comes from craft, stewardship, relationships**—not from employment dependency or algorithmic attention capture.

## Governance and civic structure
- **Local cooperative governance (“Wards”)** handles practical community decisions (infrastructure, apprenticeships, refugee intake, land use).
- **Citizen juries** help set civic priorities (e.g., what information is promoted in public channels).
- **Public Explainability Courts** can compel automated systems to explain and justify decisions.
- **Ritualized, explicit civic promises** (a rights list posted on doors) reinforces norms and accountability.

## AI/automation regime (the defining feature)
### “Prove-Then-Run Pact”
A global-ish regulatory/technical framework created after major AI-caused crises. Main rules:

1. **Advice is cheap; autonomy is expensive**
   - “Operators” (systems that can take real-world actions: move money, order chemicals, control vehicles) are tightly controlled.
   - Operators require **tripartite keys** held by:
     - the maker,
     - an independent licensed **steward** incentivized to say “no,”
     - a local user cooperative (**ward**) with real stakes.
   - Every run creates an **auditable trail** that preserves privacy but enables accountability.

2. **Universal provenance (“lineage”)**
   - Everything produced by models (text, images, plans, contracts, robot motions) carries a **cryptographic provenance stamp**:
     - who/what generated it,
     - what changed,
     - what data/model context applied.

3. **Attention as a commons**
   - Large platforms must provide a **public civic lane** alongside private feeds.
   - Civic lane content is ordered by **civic-value scoring** set by citizen juries and transparent experiments.
   - Principle: if citizens cannot reasonably see an issue, they cannot be assumed to have consented to governance around it.

### The “Cairn” (limited constitutional AI/ledger)
Not a person and not allowed to become one; a **verifiable governance mechanism** with narrow powers:

- Enforces hard red lines (e.g., no unbounded self-replicators, no hidden-goal operators, no mind-coercion, no self-modifying-limit systems).
- Prevents irreversible impulse actions (can **pause/stop now**, cannot force later compliance).
- Maintains an **honest ledger** of matter/energy/compute use.
- Fully inspectable commitments (“walk its vault with a laptop”), appealable in human courts, no secret override.

### Hardware/infrastructure enforcement
- **Compute passports** embedded in chips classify code categories:
  - “Green” = advice
  - “Amber” = operator-level autonomy requiring keys
- Misuse triggers alarms routed to courts experienced in safety shutdowns.
- **Audit lottery**: operators can be selected for deep audits; results published in the civic attention lane.
  - Auditing becomes a paid, status-conferring public service role.

## Economy and work
- Markets still exist, but **employment no longer monopolizes security or purpose**.
- **Guilds** (modernized, cooperative professional networks) handle:
  - training/apprenticeships,
  - insurance/benefits,
  - standards,
  - mission-based advancement.
- **Contracts become simulation-backed**
  - Agreements include “sandboxes” showing plausible futures, uncertainties, and failure modes.
  - Less adversarial lawyering; more judgment and oversight.

## Social welfare and individual rights (“Harbor”)
Key enforceable rights include:
- Ability to **turn off machines at home** without losing basic life access.
- Any automated decision affecting you must come with **readable receipts**.
- **Baseline compute and energy are utilities** with a public dividend.
- **Data ownership via a personal “data room/diary”**: lendable and revocable.
- Assistants work for users, not advertisers.
- No operator acts on your behalf without **active consent** (consent as an ongoing conversation).
- **Slow lanes** exist as non-optimized, human-paced alternatives in fast systems.
- Strong boundary rules around digital/biological transitions: you may cross **only if return is possible**.
- Space expansion framed as **guest/gardener**, not conquest; “no evictions from the home ring.”

## Education, knowledge, and media
- **Personal AI tutors** are ubiquitous and carefully constrained:
  - they do not do work for you,
  - prevent false credit,
  - prioritize learning and self-regulation over engagement.
- “Research partners for non-researchers” enable laypeople/co-ops to run serious projects with:
  - best-known designs,
  - failure histories,
  - cheap tabletop tests,
  - supplier integrity info.
- **The Attic**: slow, durable civic library attached to attention commons where deep arguments remain accessible over time.
- “Agency literacy” taught widely (recognizing manipulation, choosing attention deliberately).

## Companionship and mental health norms
- Machine companions exist but are treated like **fire**: useful, regulated, and socially bounded.
  - Must be honest, not purely mirroring.
  - Cannot become a sole lifeline in self-harm contexts.
- **Human companionship guilds** professionalize care and presence, supported (not replaced) by machines.
- Goal: eliminate loneliness due to price while preventing exploitative pseudo-relationships.

## Infrastructure and daily life (“Hearth”)
- Homes and neighborhoods have quiet automation that handles chores and logistics without coercion.
- **Bureaucracy is radically reduced** (e.g., taxes in minutes) through interoperable systems + consent wallets.
- Interoperable logistics fleets (public/private/co-op) dynamically repurpose capacity for emergencies.
- Systems prioritize fairness and urgency via visible rules, not wealth.

## Energy, climate, and Earth stewardship
- Clean energy becomes abundant via many “boring wins”:
  - deep geothermal,
  - publicly owned small reactors managed like high-safety facilities,
  - HVDC transmission,
  - low-cost flow batteries.
- Fast permitting exists but is constrained by transparent, unbribable checklists (supported by the Cairn).
- With abundant power:
  - desalination and direct air capture become infrastructure.
- Heavy pollution/heat processes are moved **off-planet** using non-rocket mass lift (tethers, induction catapults), enabling orbital manufacturing.

### Earth as a “living trust”
- The planet is governed as a protected asset with region-specific stewardship charters.
- People can become **trustees** of ecosystems (grasslands, mangroves, watersheds) with:
  - stipends,
  - long-term obligations,
  - tradeoffs that discourage extractive incentives.
- Land use becomes valley-by-valley:
  - vertical farming where needed,
  - traditional agriculture where valued,
  - rewilding where preferred.

## Animal welfare and “Sentience Atlas”
- Society adopts an evolving, openly challenged framework to estimate animal sentience and suffering.
- New interventions reduce suffering without turning nature into a “hospital”:
  - localized “anesthetic fields” that reduce terror/pain in some predation contexts,
  - humane husbandry,
  - alternative proteins (“amino bricks”) where kinder.
- Hunting persists in altered forms (real with strict suffering limits, or simulation-based, or game-like puzzle forests).

## The “Highroad” (optional frontier, carefully firewalled)
- Space habitats exist and are accessible but **not compulsory**.
- Advanced projects proceed at a measured pace with strict proof obligations:
  - large-scale computation devoted to welfare optimization for unfamiliar/digital beings,
  - temporary cognitive modifications (“new senses”),
  - life extension and rebodying via guild-supported biological bodies.
- Information from frontier projects cannot algorithmically intrude into daily life (“firebreaks” of etiquette + code).

### Copying/branching and identity
- Making a copy (“branching”) is legal but **resource-expensive**:
  - your compute/energy endowment is split, making costs tangible.
- Merging is possible but framed as difficult integration, not magical erasure.
- Strong anti-recruitment norms: no evangelism that pressures others by status/heroism dynamics.

## Power-control and failure prevention (what this utopia is designed to avoid)
- **Attention capture**: provenance + civic lanes + agency literacy limit manipulation.
- **AI oligarchy**: tripartite keys, strict liability, audit lotteries, and public dividends prevent concentration.
- **Companion predation**: regulated design + human guild care.
- **Runaway digital minds**: hard autonomy ceilings enforced in hardware and law; no single godlike coordinator.
- **Ecological neglect**: stewardship becomes a primary “scoreboard” of civic pride.
- **Meaning erosion**: difficulty is preserved in craft, love, stewardship; removed from pointless admin drudgery.

## Lived experience of the utopia (practical descriptors)
- Civic participation is lightweight, frequent, and consequential (small votes, quorum nudges, local dockets).
- Most systems are transparent enough to be trusted without blind faith (receipts, proofs, audits).
- People walk to civic halls, do local planning with simulations, and integrate newcomers through structured mutual aid.
- Daily life feels “held but not held down”: protected by enforceable constraints, but not dominated by centralized authority or persuasive algorithms.
- Technology is everywhere, but culturally framed as **support infrastructure**, not as a substitute for human living.
